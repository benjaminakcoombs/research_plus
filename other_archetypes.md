# Calibration Examples: Financial Services

---

- "JPMorgan's commercial lending team conducts deep credit analysis on thousands of mid-market companies annually. This proprietary industry intelligence — sector trends, default patterns, operational benchmarks — could be productized as a research/advisory service for PE firms and strategic acquirers who currently pay $X/year for inferior third-party data."

- "Insurance companies' claims adjusters develop deep expertise in specific damage types (water, fire, auto). This knowledge could be licensed to property management companies, auto dealerships, and contractors as certification/training programs — turning a cost center into a credentialing business."

- "A regional bank's mortgage processing team handles 5,000+ applications/year, building expertise in local property valuation, zoning, and regulatory compliance. They should offer white-label mortgage processing services to credit unions and community banks that can't maintain this expertise at scale — a model proven by firms like Computershare Mortgage Services."

# Calibration Examples: Healthcare / Pharma

---

- "Hospital systems operate massive laundry facilities processing 15-20 lbs of linens per patient per day. Most run below capacity on weekends and overnight. They should offer commercial laundry services to nearby hotels, restaurants, and smaller clinics — facilities that already meet healthcare-grade sanitation standards."

- "Pharma companies' failed drug candidates often have validated biological activity in non-target indications. A systematic 'drug repurposing' program could extract value from R&D investments currently sitting as write-offs. Drugs.com lists 70+ drugs that were repurposed from failed original indications — including Viagra (originally a heart medication)."

- "A hospital network's fleet of 200+ patient transport vehicles sits idle from 10 PM to 6 AM. Overnight, these vehicles could provide contract medical courier services (lab specimens, blood products, medical records) to independent clinics and physician offices — a service currently filled by expensive specialized couriers."

# Calibration Examples: Conglomerate

---

- "Berkshire Hathaway's diverse operating companies collectively purchase billions in commercial insurance. By centralizing procurement through a dedicated unit, they could negotiate better rates AND build enough data to potentially self-insure lower layers — turning what's currently a fragmented expense into a risk management profit center."

- "A multi-segment conglomerate where the agricultural division generates 50,000 tonnes/year of biomass waste that could fuel the engineering division's manufacturing operations — cross-segment synergy that neither division would pursue independently due to separate P&Ls."

- "A diversified industrial holding company's 15 subsidiaries each maintain independent IT, HR, and accounting functions totaling 200+ support staff. A shared services center could eliminate 40% of this overhead while improving consistency — the Roper Technologies playbook of stripping shared costs from acquisitions has proven this generates 200-400bps of margin improvement."

- "A conglomerate trading at a 45% discount to sum-of-parts NAV has three segments with no operational synergy. A tax-efficient spin-off of the most valuable segment (precedent: Danaher/Fortive split) would force the market to value it independently — Danaher's stock tripled in the 5 years post-split."

# Calibration Examples: Infrastructure / Utilities

---

- "A regional utility operates 2,000 miles of rights-of-way that currently carry only power lines. These corridors could host fiber optic cable for rural broadband — a model proven by utility-telco partnerships in the EU that generate €X/km/year in lease revenue."

- "A water utility's treatment plants generate thousands of tonnes of biosolids (treated sewage sludge) annually, currently disposed of at $X/tonne. Class A biosolids can be sold as agricultural fertilizer at $Y/tonne — a net swing of $Z/tonne. DC Water's Blue Plains facility has done this at scale through their 'Bloom' brand."
