# Calibration Examples: Manufacturer / Extractor / Grower

These examples illustrate the LEVEL OF SPECIFICITY expected for tension points. They are NOT suggestions for the target company — they are examples from other companies that demonstrate the pattern of connecting a SPECIFIC operational fact to a SPECIFIC market opportunity.

---

- "Jostens (yearbook company) uses vast volumes of paper. They should negotiate wholesale paper contracts and resell surplus to smaller printing companies in their region, turning a cost center into a distribution business."

- "Jostens' specialized high-quality printing presses sit idle outside of yearbook season (June-August production for September delivery). They should offer custom premium printing services (wedding invitations, limited-edition art prints, specialty packaging) to utilize spare capacity during the 8 months of off-peak."

- "Home Depot processes millions of product returns — functional but unsellable at full price. They should create 'Home Depot Outlet' sections selling imperfect building materials at 50-70% off to budget renovators and landlords, turning a cost line into a revenue stream."

- "Home Depot's 2,000 parking lots are half-empty on weekdays. They should lease space for EV charging infrastructure, last-mile delivery staging, or modular self-storage — each of which has proven unit economics in similar retail parking contexts."

- "Home Depot associates have deep trade skills built through years of customer interaction. They should create a formal trade school / apprenticeship program that feeds graduates into a Home Depot Verified Contractor marketplace, capturing value from both training fees and contractor referral commissions."

- "Camellia Plc's 12,000 hectares of Kenyan forestry adjacent to tea estates could generate an estimated $1-3M/year in carbon credits under Verra VCS, but management has not pursued certification despite competitors like Finlays successfully doing so."

- "Camellia's Kakuzi macadamia processing plant generates thousands of tonnes of macadamia shells annually — a proven biomass fuel and activated carbon feedstock. The shells are currently sold at commodity prices (~$30/tonne) but could be processed into activated carbon at $800-1,200/tonne."

- "A chemical manufacturer's heat exchangers exhaust 40MW of waste heat annually. The adjacent industrial park has three cold storage operators paying $0.12/kWh for refrigeration. An absorption chiller system ($2M capex) could convert waste heat to cooling and sell it at $0.08/kWh, undercutting grid prices while monetizing a pure waste stream."
