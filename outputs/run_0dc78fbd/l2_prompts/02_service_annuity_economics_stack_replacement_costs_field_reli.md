CONTEXT:
Bloom Energy (NYSE: BE, ~$45B market cap) has a $20B total backlog, of which approximately $14B is contracted service revenue over 10-20 year terms. Every Bloom Energy Server installation carries a mandatory service contract — a 100% "attach rate" that is structural, not optional, because the proprietary solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) stacks require periodic replacement and only Bloom can perform this work. Current annual service revenue is approximately $229M (~$157K per installed MW across ~1.5 GW deployed). However, the 2018-2022 deployment cohort (~500-700 MW, including ~600 MW deployed in Korea through SK ecoplant) is entering its mandatory 5-7 year stack replacement cycle during 2025-2028. This "repowering wave" should drive a step-change in service revenue.

Cross-industry analogues provide benchmarks: GE Aerospace's TotalCare long-term service agreements generate $1.5-2.0M per engine per year at 40-50% margins, with aftermarket representing ~70% of GE Aerospace's operating profit. Rolls-Royce's TotalCare covers >90% of wide-body engine fleet at similar margins. Otis Elevator derives 87% of operating profit from service/maintenance despite selling new equipment at lower margins. In each case, the original equipment sale was effectively a "customer acquisition cost" for a multi-decade service annuity.

Applied to Bloom: if the mature service revenue rate reaches $300-500K per MW per year (vs. $157K currently), the annualized service run rate on a projected 5-8 GW installed base by 2030 would be $1.5-2.5B per year. At a 7% infrastructure discount rate, the NPV of the total contracted service stream could be $7-14B — potentially larger than Bloom's current hardware business value.

However, SOFC stacks operate at 800-1000°C, degrade from thermal cycling, and can experience catastrophic failure modes. The 2024-vintage "Be Flexible" load-following capability is being deployed in mission-critical hyperscaler applications with limited long-term reliability data. A Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory expert noted that SOFCs "can shatter as they are heating or cooling." If a systemic degradation or manufacturing flaw is discovered across the 2022-2025 cohort, the service contracts could flip from profit centers to liabilities — exactly the dynamic that cost Rolls-Royce billions when the Trent 1000 engine showed systemic durability issues in 2017-2019.

If stack replacement costs, margins, and failure rates can be validated, this is a "2-3x multiple turn" insight that reframes Bloom from a hardware manufacturer to an infrastructure annuity platform.
