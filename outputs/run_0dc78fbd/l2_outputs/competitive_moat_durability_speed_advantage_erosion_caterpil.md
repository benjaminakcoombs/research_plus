## SECTION 2: CATERPILLAR — THE COMPETITIVE THREAT WITH COMPLETE RELIABILITY CONTEXT

### 2A. The Critical Gap Resolved: Cat G3516 Has Continuous Duty Heritage — But Not at Data Center Scale

This is the most important finding from Round 3, and it meaningfully updates the prior analysis in a nuanced way.

**What Cat's G3516 has done:** 
G3516 units are the most common large-format natural gas generator in data center backup power applications and wastewater treatment plants requiring continuous duty.
 This is significant — the G3516 has demonstrated continuous duty operation in two relevant application classes: wastewater treatment (24/7 continuous) and data center backup (though typically in standby, not prime power mode).


The W.R. Grace & Co. Curtis Bay facility runs 24/7, producing catalysts and silicas for a variety of applications. When aging infrastructure caused power to become unreliable at the plant, the company turned to its Cat dealer to commission a combined heat and power (CHP) system powered by two Cat generator sets to supply reliable power and lower energy costs.
 This is industrial continuous-duty CHP — directly analogous to data center prime power in terms of duty cycle.

**What Cat's G3516 has NOT done:** Every verified continuous-duty Cat prime power application is at small scale — the W.R. Grace installation is multiple MW, the Lehi City Power installation is <10 MW. 
With support from their Cat dealer, Lehi developed a power generation facility featuring three Cat G3520H generator sets capable of producing nearly 10 percent of the power needs for 20,000 customers.
 Three G3520H units at ~2.5 MW each = ~7.5 MW. This is the largest confirmed continuous-duty Cat gas engine array in the public record — approximately 0.75% of the scale required for a 1 GW data center deployment.

The reliability gap is therefore not "Cat has never operated continuous duty" (wrong) but rather "Cat has never operated a 100–500 MW prime power array in continuous duty at the density and integration requirements of an AI data center" (correct and much more specific). This distinction matters because:
- Individual unit reliability at continuous duty is reasonably established for the G3516 (~9,978 hours to ~11,282 hours per unit, well within the platform's design life as a used market reference point)

Used G3516 units come with verifiable operating histories — Unit 1 with approximately 9,978 hours and Unit 2 with approximately 11,282 hours. For Caterpillar's legendary G3516 engine platform, these hour meters represent well-maintained units in their prime operating window, with substantial service life remaining when properly maintained. These are proven performers with documented reliability. The G3516 is Caterpillar's workhorse natural gas engine platform, trusted worldwide in the most demanding applications.


This confirms that individual unit reliability is real and documented. The G3516 at ~10,000 hours with substantial remaining life is consistent with a ~20-year design life at continuous duty — a credible track record for individual units. The question for data center operators is whether 40–200 units operating in parallel achieve 99.9%+ availability as a system — a question with no existing answer.
