### 8.1 Be Flexible Thermal Cycling: The Only Remaining Structural Bear Case

This is now the **sole unresolved structural risk** after warranty normalization. 
Bloom's ability to fulfill its growth depends on manufacturing scaling and supply chain reliability. Key components like the specialized ceramics or interconnect metals must be procured in larger quantities. If supply chain constraints hit, Bloom might face delays or cost overruns. Similarly, ramping production to 2 GW is a heavy lift; any mis-execution (quality issues, labor shortages in factories) could slow deliveries.


The Be Flexible load-following solution, providing more than 5x faster power ramp than legacy solutions, subjects ceramic stacks to thermal cycling patterns without historical precedent in Bloom's installed base. The 2026 market commentary flagging degradation-from-cycling as a technical risk confirms this concern has entered institutional investor awareness. First-generation Be Flexible units deployed in 2024 will reach their first major reliability data point in 2026–2027.

**Quantified bear risk:** Applying the Trent 1000 analogue (16% of revenue in exceptional charges) to Bloom's $2B revenue base = ~$320M in potential abnormal warranty charges if the Be Flexible cohort fails at scale. This would transform service segment economics but would not invalidate the existing $14B backlog on steady-state units.

Bloom Energy and SK ecoplant have deployed 400 MW since the beginning of the partnership.

### 8.3 Hyperscaler Service Contract Margin Compression


Other fuel cell makers (like PEM-based systems from Plug Power or molten carbonate fuel cells from FuelCell Energy) exist, but none have scaled as much or demonstrated the same performance at scale as Bloom.
 While no current competitor can maintain Bloom SOFC stacks, hyperscalers with 100–900 MW installed bases will accumulate extraordinary negotiating leverage on service contract renewal terms. Even without self-maintenance capability, they can demand cost-plus pricing on service contract renewals — maintaining revenue but compressing margins.

### 8.4 Manufacturing Scale Execution Risk


Ramping production to 2 GW is a heavy lift; any mis-execution (quality issues, labor shortages in factories) could slow deliveries and cause customers to get frustrated.
 A quality failure during the capacity doubling — deploying substandard stacks across the 2026–2027 hyperscaler deployment wave — could simultaneously create immediate warranty obligations and damage Bloom's reputation with its most important new customer category.

### 8.5 Policy Risk: IRA Section 48E


Starting in 2026, Bloom's Energy Servers will qualify for a 30% investment tax credit under Section 48E of the IRA, significantly improving customer economics.
 The 2026 Congressional budget process could reduce or eliminate this 30% ITC, materially increasing customer payback periods and potentially slowing new deployments. The AEP Wyoming PPA at $2,950/kW was presumably underwritten with the ITC factored into customer economics — removing it would require contract renegotiation.
