- Current Korean REC spot price (estimated): KRW 30,000–45,000/REC (~$22–$33/REC)
- **Annual REC subsidy to Korean power companies using Bloom: $208M–$312M/year**

This subsidy flows to SK ecoplant's downstream customers — Korean power plant operators — not to Bloom directly. However, it is the economic foundation that makes Bloom's above-market product pricing viable in Korea. Under CHPS auction transition, this preferential subsidy converts to a contract-for-differences structure with competitively determined prices.

**Post-2027 Korean demand sensitivity:**

| Policy Scenario | Annual New Deployment | Bloom Annual Revenue | Change vs. Baseline |
|---|---|---|---|
| CHPS + 200 MW/year government target maintained | 200 MW | ~$550M/year | Baseline |
| CHPS general hydrogen track, 150 MW/year | 150 MW | ~$410M/year | –$140M/year |
| CHPS clean hydrogen required, 100 MW/year | 100 MW | ~$275M/year | –$275M/year |
| Technology-neutral auction, no fuel cell preference | 50 MW | ~$135M/year | –$415M/year |

The Korean government's own 10-year electricity plan targets 200 MW/year of fuel cell deployment — the most favorable scenario. The critical variable is whether "fuel cells" under the CHPS means Bloom's grey-hydrogen SOFCs (general track) or requires clean hydrogen (premium track). Given that Korean fuel cell manufacturers (Hanwha Impact, Doosan Fuel Cell) have declined to participate in clean hydrogen auctions due to economics, Bloom faces the same cost barrier.

## SECTION 5: FEASIBILITY ASSESSMENT — WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENS

### The Modal Path (Branch B, 38% probability): The "Quiet Fourth Amendment"

The most likely resolution of the SK ecoplant PDA stress is not a dramatic termination or a transparent renegotiation — it is a **private, undisclosed amendment** to the PDA that adjusts pricing and timelines without triggering a material definitive agreement 8-K (because the amendment may not cross the materiality threshold for SEC disclosure if structured carefully).

The precedent for this is already established: the 2021 Restatement was filed as an 8-K with material term summary; the 2023 Extension was disclosed via press release only, with no standalone SEC exhibit. A "2026 Amendment" could be disclosed even more minimally — a single sentence in the Q2 2026 10-Q noting that the PDA has been updated for "delivery timeline optimization" — without disclosing pricing changes.

**For an acquirer, this undisclosed amendment is the single most dangerous scenario:** Bloom could have already negotiated a secretly price-reduced PDA by the time due diligence begins. A VDR review of the PDA full text (including all amendments) is non-negotiable as a pre-LOI condition.

### The Key Structural Protection Most Analysts Are Missing: The Loan Right

The $311M shareholders' loan right discovered in Round 3 is the most underappreciated constraint on SK ecoplant's negotiating leverage. If Bloom exercises this right in response to SK ecoplant's share sales, SK ecoplant would owe Bloom $311M at a moment when it is desperate for liquidity. This creates a **mutually assured commercial deterrence** — SK ecoplant cannot push for aggressive PDA concessions without risking Bloom calling the loan; Bloom cannot ignore SK ecoplant's distress without risking PDA non-performance.

**The resolution most consistent with all evidence:** Bloom waives the loan right in exchange for SK ecoplant confirming PDA commitment through 2027 at modestly reduced pricing — a private, bilateral arrangement that never appears in SEC filings. This is Branch B executed quietly.

## SECTION 7: COUNTER-EVIDENCE — FINAL ASSESSMENT

### 7A. The Loan Right as the Strongest Counter-Evidence

As established in Section 1A, the $311M shareholders' loan right is the strongest structural argument against PDA impairment. If active, it gives Bloom financial leverage over SK ecoplant that is independent of and superior to the PDA's termination provisions.

### 7B. The AI Data Center Pivot Aligns SK Ecoplant's New Strategy with Bloom's Value


SK Ecoplant will now shift its focus to its Hi-tech division, centred on semiconductor infrastructure and IT asset disposition services. It aims to strengthen its position in areas aligned with growing demand from AI-related data centres.
 Bloom's fuel cells are the ideal power solution for Korean AI data centers — exactly the market SK ecoplant is pivoting toward. The PDA relationship becomes more strategically relevant, not less, under SK ecoplant's new AI infrastructure focus.

### 7C. No PDA Amendment Disclosed — Four Months Post-Share Sales

No Bloom 8-K has disclosed a PDA amendment since August 2025 (share sales). No SK ecoplant DART filing has disclosed a Bloom commercial agreement modification. The seven-month silence since the share sales is either: (a) evidence that the relationship is stable and no amendment is needed; or (b) evidence that any amendment made was below the SEC materiality disclosure threshold. The silence cannot be interpreted as conclusively positive or negative.

### 7D. Bloom's 2026 Guidance Includes Korea


2026 revenue guidance is $3.1–$3.3 billion, representing over 50% year-over-year growth.
 This guidance was issued February 5, 2026 — after SK ecoplant's share sales (August 2025), after the accounting scandal SFC ruling (September 2025), and after the FI investor standoff was reported (December 2025). If Bloom's management believed Korean revenue was at material risk, they would likely have disclosed a more conservative or range-qualified guidance with Korea-specific caveats. The fact that they issued $3.1–3.3B guidance without Korean-specific carve-outs implies they believe SK ecoplant will honor sufficient volume to support the guidance — or that U.S. demand alone supports the guidance range even without Korea.
