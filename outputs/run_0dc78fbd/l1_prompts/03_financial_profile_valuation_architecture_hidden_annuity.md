ROLE: You are a senior equity research analyst specializing in power generation and energy technology companies. You have covered Bloom Energy for 5+ years and are now conducting a pre-deal financial deep dive to support M&A valuation analysis. Your focus is on revenue quality, margin trajectory, and — critically — the valuation of the embedded service annuity in Bloom's installed base.

CONTEXT: Bloom Energy (NYSE: BE) reported the following financials:

RESEARCH TASKS:

   - Revenue recognition: How does Bloom recognize product revenue? At shipment, installation, or acceptance? Any percentage-of-completion methodology? This is a key diligence item given the 2020 SEC investigation.

5. Research the 2020 SEC and Enforcement Division activity regarding Bloom's revenue recognition practices. What was the outcome? Are there any residual concerns?

4. JPMorgan flagged that tariffs could reduce margins by ~100bp. Research the specific tariff exposure — which imported components are affected? What is the estimated annual cost impact?

**C. THE HIDDEN ANNUITY — SERVICE/REPOWERING REVENUE VALUATION**

This is the most critical analytical exercise. Bloom's solid oxide fuel cell stacks degrade over 5-7 years and require replacement — generating mandatory repowering revenue from every installed megawatt. Combined with 10-20 year service agreements, this creates an embedded annuity that is almost invisible in current financial statements.

1. Model the installed base growth trajectory:
   - Current installed base: ~1.4 GW (2025)
   - Estimate annual deployment: 2021 (~X MW), 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025
   - Project forward deployment: 2026-2030, using backlog data (AEP 1 GW, SK 500 MW, Brookfield pipeline)

2. Model the repowering cycle:
   - Average stack life: 5-7 years (use 6 years as base case)
   - Repowering cost per MW: estimate from available data or comparable fuel cell economics
   - When does the first major repowering wave hit? (Systems deployed in 2018-2019 in Korea should be approaching repowering)
   - Build a year-by-year repowering revenue forecast through 2035

3. Model the service contract revenue:
   - Average annual service revenue per MW of installed capacity
   - Contract duration: 10-20 years
   - Typical escalation clauses (CPI-linked? Fixed?)
   - Churn/cancellation rate (research any disclosed contract losses)

4. Value the annuity:
   - What is the total NPV of the contracted service and repowering revenue from the current 1.4 GW installed base?
   - What discount rate is appropriate? (Use infrastructure asset discount rates: 6-8% for contracted cash flows)
   - How does this NPV compare to Bloom's current enterprise value?
   - What would this annuity be worth as a standalone infrastructure asset? (Compare to infrastructure fund IRR targets of 8-12%)

5. Cross-validate with analogues:
   - GE Aerospace's engine service business generates ~$25B/year from an installed base of ~40,000 engines. What is the revenue per installed unit, and how does this compare to Bloom's $/MW?
   - Otis Elevator's service revenue is ~60% of total revenue with ~45% operating margins. How does this ratio compare to Bloom's trajectory?
   - Rolls-Royce's "Power by the Hour" model — is Bloom moving toward a similar structure?
