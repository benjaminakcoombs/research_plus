ROLE: You are an energy regulatory and tax policy specialist advising an M&A team on the policy dimensions of a potential transaction involving Bloom Energy Corporation. You have deep expertise in the Inflation Reduction Act, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, Investment Tax Credits, and state-level energy policy. Your analysis must be precise enough to inform a valuation model.

CONTEXT: Bloom Energy manufactures solid oxide fuel cells that convert natural gas (or biogas/hydrogen) to electricity on-site. These systems qualify for the federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) under Section 48/48E of the Internal Revenue Code. The policy landscape shifted dramatically in 2025:
- The IRA (2022) established a technology-neutral ITC under Section 48E for projects beginning construction after January 1, 2025
- The OBBBA (signed July 4, 2025) modified the IRA's clean energy provisions, accelerating repeal of many renewable credits BUT fixing fuel cell property at a 30% ITC rate NOT subject to GHG emissions tests or step-down
- Bloom's systems run primarily on natural gas (679-833 lbs CO2/MWh) — dirtier than renewables but cleaner than grid average
- SK ecoplant, a South Korean entity, is Bloom's largest customer and ~5% shareholder — creating potential FEOC exposure

RESEARCH TASKS:

**A. FEDERAL TAX CREDIT ANALYSIS — PRECISE LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

1. Research and document the EXACT statutory language governing fuel cell property under the OBBBA:
   - Section 48E ITC provisions as modified by OBBBA
   - The specific provision fixing fuel cell property at 30% ITC
   - Confirm: Does this 30% rate apply regardless of GHG emissions? (The L0 profile states this — verify with statutory text)
   - What is the "begin construction" deadline? Is there a phase-down schedule for fuel cells specifically?
   - Prevailing Wage & Apprenticeship (PWA) requirements — does the fixed 30% rate require PWA compliance?
   - Domestic content requirements — what are the specific requirements, and does Bloom comply? (Manufacturing in Newark, DE and Fremont, CA should help, but what about imported components?)

2. Quantify the ITC's impact on Bloom's customer economics:
   - For a hypothetical 10 MW Bloom installation at ~$1,500-2,000/kW all-in cost, what is the dollar value of the 30% ITC?
   - How does this change the customer's effective LCOE from Bloom vs. alternatives?
   - What is the "ITC cliff" risk? If the credit were repealed or reduced, by how much would Bloom's effective price to customers increase?
   - Research: Has Bloom been monetizing ITCs through tax equity structures, direct pay, or transferability? How does the OBBBA affect these monetization mechanisms?

3. Research the OBBBA's treatment of other competing technologies:
   - Solar ITC phase-down schedule under OBBBA
   - Wind PTC phase-down schedule under OBBBA
   - Battery storage ITC under OBBBA
   - How do these phase-downs affect Bloom's RELATIVE competitiveness? (If solar/wind credits are being phased out faster, Bloom's fixed 30% becomes relatively MORE valuable)

**B. FEOC (FOREIGN ENTITY OF CONCERN) ANALYSIS — CRITICAL**

1. Research the EXACT FEOC definition under the OBBBA:
   - Which countries/entities are covered? (Typically: China, Russia, North Korea, Iran)
   - Is South Korea explicitly excluded? Or is there ambiguity?
   - Does the FEOC restriction apply to the project owner, the equipment manufacturer, the component supplier, or all of the above?

2. Analyze Bloom's specific FEOC exposure through SK ecoplant:
   - SK ecoplant is a South Korean company — but does SK Group have any subsidiaries, JVs, or operations in FEOC-designated countries (particularly China)?
   - Research SK Group's business relationships in China — SK has significant battery manufacturing in China through SK On. Could this create indirect FEOC exposure?
   - If a Bloom fuel cell project uses components manufactured in South Korea by SK ecoplant or through SK-connected supply chains, could this trigger FEOC restrictions?

3. Research Treasury Department guidance on FEOC implementation:
   - Any proposed or final regulations?
   - Any interpretive guidance specific to the fuel cell or distributed energy sector?
   - Any industry comment letters from Bloom, the Fuel Cell and Hydrogen Energy Association, or peer companies?

4. Risk assessment: On a scale of low/medium/high, rate the FEOC risk to Bloom's business model. Quantify the potential revenue impact under a worst-case FEOC ruling.

1. What is the political risk to the fuel cell ITC? Research:
