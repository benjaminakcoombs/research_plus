CONTEXT:
Sunrun (NASDAQ: RUN) is a $4–6B market cap residential solar and battery company. Sunrun is Lunar Energy's largest strategic investor, and Sunrun co-executive chair Lynn Jurich chairs Lunar's board of directors. Sunrun is Lunar's primary hardware distribution channel and the largest customer of Lunar's Gridshare VPP software platform (Gridshare manages Sunrun's non-Tesla residential battery fleet across California).

Sunrun's next significant debt maturity is March 2028.

The governance conflict is also notable: Sunrun's own customer-facing website promotes Tesla Powerwall 3 as "one of the most trusted energy storage solutions available." Sunrun's Texas VPP and CalReady program (75,000 installations) run primarily on Tesla hardware. Within Sunrun's VPP architecture, Tesla's own software platform manages Powerwalls while Gridshare manages only non-Tesla batteries — meaning as Sunrun's fleet becomes more Tesla-dominated, Gridshare's role shrinks. Lunar's $232M Series D was raised without public acknowledgment of this concentration risk.

Together, these create an existential concentration risk that requires urgent diversification.
