CONTEXT:
Lunar Energy (through its UK subsidiary, formerly Moixa Energy) developed the Gridshare software platform, which manages over 35,000 ITOCHU Smart Star residential batteries in Japan — processing 40 billion data points per month. In December 2025, Lunar restructured its Japan operations: rather than operating directly, it took a minority equity stake in GridShare Japan (GSJ), a subsidiary with ITOCHU and Japanese utility co-investors. This means Lunar captures technology licensing revenue and equity upside from GSJ but does not control operations, pricing, or commercial decisions.

Starting in 2026, Japanese residential distributed energy resources become eligible for transmission-level capacity market and balancing market participation for the first time. METI (Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry) has allocated ¥9 billion (~$58M) in subsidies for battery aggregation. Japan's capacity market has already held auctions with clearing prices for future delivery years.
