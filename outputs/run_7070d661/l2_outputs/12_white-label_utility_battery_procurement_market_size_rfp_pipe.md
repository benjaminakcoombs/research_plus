### B. The FEOC/Domestic Content Tailwind Is Accelerating — and Tightening Into a Moat

The regulatory environment around domestic content and FEOC compliance has intensified dramatically following the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), signed July 4, 2025:


On July 4, 2025, President Trump signed into law the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), which significantly changes the clean energy tax credit landscape and introduces accelerated repeal schedules for most renewable energy tax credits, enhances domestic content requirements, eliminates several residential energy incentives, and implements new FEOC restrictions barring certain foreign entities from accessing credits.



A key part of this effort was the dramatic expansion of FEOC restrictions; under the OBBBA, the restrictions now apply more broadly to many more energy tax provisions, reaching more deeply than the original FEOC rules. "The new PFE rules are, essentially, FEOC on steroids."


For utilities seeking to claim ITC on utility-owned battery assets: 
if a qualified facility, energy storage technology, or eligible component receives "material assistance" from a prohibited foreign entity, it becomes ineligible for tax credits under Sections 45Y, 48E, or 45X.


This creates a structural procurement filter that disadvantages Chinese-content batteries — including virtually all Korean and Chinese lithium-ion supply chains with FEOC component exposure. A manufacturer with verified FEOC-free status and domestic content compliance becomes a preferred vendor for tax-credit-seeking utilities. Lunar's FEOC-free and domestic content status, per the hypothesis, could thus be a decisive differentiator in utility RFPs.

on the Tesla Powerwall partnership, the utility would add up to 1,000 Powerwall batteries per year, totaling 5 MW and just over 13 MWh.

**GMP — New Enphase Partnership (November 2025):** 
On November 12, 2025, Enphase Energy and Green Mountain Power announced an expanded partnership that adds Enphase battery systems to GMP's existing home energy storage lease program.

ES Solar has already sold an unprecedented 18 MWh of sonnen retrofit energy storage systems via the Go Back initiative, with more than 75% of its 2024 sales via the Go Back Program, resulting in 200–500 sonnen battery installations in Utah each month.

**Base Power — El Paso Electric (February 2026, the freshest comparable):** This is the most structurally analogous program to the Lunar hypothesis. 
The program represents a scalable model for utilities seeking to add flexible, customer-sited capacity to the grid on significantly shorter timelines than traditional generation buildout; through the program, Base Power will deploy a fleet of networked residential battery systems in EPE's Texas service region, providing EPE with dispatchable capacity; the batteries will be installed at qualifying residential customer homes; the partnership was selected through EPE's Residential Distributed Energy Storage Pilot Program (DESPP), which seeks to bring up to 10 MW of residential energy storage online ahead of the 2026 summer peak.
 The commercial structure is key: 
EPE will manage the battery fleet including dispatch, scheduling, and performance monitoring; Base Power is responsible for system installation and ongoing maintenance, enabling EPE to deploy capacity efficiently without adding operational complexity.
 Importantly, Base Power was founded in 2023 and 
claims to have deployed more than 100 MWh of residential battery capacity in under two years

Base is building its first energy storage and power electronics manufacturing plant

## 2. EVIDENCE AGAINST THE HYPOTHESIS

### A. Virginia's 450 MW Is Not a Residential Battery Hardware RFP — It's Mostly Aggregated Customer-Owned DERs

The most important caveat to the Virginia math is definitional. The 450 MW VPP pilot is primarily designed to aggregate *customer-owned* distributed assets — existing solar, smart thermostats, EV chargers, and batteries — into a dispatch-controlled virtual power plant. 
The program calls for 450 MW to "optimize demand" with distributed energy resources, mainly batteries but also smart thermostats, electric vehicle charging, and non-battery storage.
 The mandated residential battery incentive is a floor of just 15 MW, not 450 MW. 
The proposal must include incentives for at least 15 MW of residential batteries.


This means the "22,500 Lunar units" math in the hypothesis requires a utility-ownership model (Dominion buying Lunar systems and installing them in customer homes at its own expense) rather than the more likely customer-incentive model (Dominion offering rebates/tariffs for customers to purchase their own batteries, with Lunar competing in the retail installer channel as before). The GMP/Tesla model (utility-owned leased assets) is the exception, not the norm.

### B. The Dominion Residential Battery Pilot Was Rejected and Referred to Stakeholder Process

As noted above, 
the SCC rejected the proposed Residential Battery Storage Pilot Program and instead referred it to a stakeholder process; when the stakeholder process concludes, Dominion must file a report alongside its proposed VPP pilot.
 This introduces regulatory uncertainty and delay into the specific component (residential battery incentive/ownership programs) most relevant to Lunar.

### C. Xcel's Capacity*Connect Is Not a Residential Program

Xcel's flagship distributed capacity procurement, while the largest and most structured utility-owned battery program currently in development, targets front-of-meter commercial-scale assets. 
Xcel intends to locate the batteries at local businesses, commercial or industrial sites, or nonprofit organizations, with each site having approximately one to three megawatts of storage.
 This is fundamentally incompatible with Lunar's residential 15–30 kWh residential system design.

### D. Illinois's 3 GW Storage Procurement Is Utility-Scale, Not Residential


CRGA specifies that the Illinois Power Agency will conduct an initial procurement by August 2026 for new utility-scale battery storage projects, seeking just over 1,000 megawatts of 4-hour duration batteries in the first procurement.
 This is utility-scale grid storage — BESS containers, not residential systems. The VPP component is customer-incentive based, not utility hardware procurement.

### E. Scale/Track Record Risk Is Real but May Be Surmountable

Standard utility procurement best practices require demonstrated track records. The Port of Oakland's BESS RFP template requires: 
sellers must provide details of a minimum of three BESS projects that have been completed in the United States, including verifiable client names, project locations, quantities and sizes, and total project costs.
 Salt River Project's 2025 All-Source RFP requires: 
at least 5 years of utility scale generation experience with proposed technology and at least one 100 MW generation project in operation in the U.S.; for storage, the transacting entity must have demonstrated experience with similar, utility-scale (at least 25 MW) projects.


These requirements are for utility-scale grid storage — not residential battery programs. The El Paso Electric/Base Power precedent suggests that for residential DER programs, utilities may apply far less stringent scale minimums. Base Power was founded in 2023 with ~100 MWh deployed when it won the EPE DESPP contract — roughly comparable to Lunar's current position.

### F. No Lunar White-Label Utility Program Exists — No Evidence of Active Pursuit

There is no public evidence that Lunar has engaged any utility in a formal procurement process, submitted responses to any utility RFI or RFP, or publicly announced any utility partnership discussions. This is either a strategic gap or a deliberate sequencing decision (build installer channel first, then expand). Either way, the company lacks an existing utility reference customer — a procurement disadvantage.

### G. The Dominant Utility DER Model Is Customer-Incentive, Not Utility Hardware Ownership

The broader industry pattern from the SEPA/NCCETC 2025 State Policy Snapshot suggests utilities are primarily pursuing: (1) customer-owned BYOD with incentives (Rocky Mountain Power Wattsmart BYOD, GMP BYOD), (2) aggregator-led VPPs using existing customer assets, and (3) demand response programs. Utility-owned residential battery hardware (GMP/Tesla model) is the exception, pursued only by small utilities with unusual regulatory environments. Large IOUs like Dominion and ComEd are more likely to pursue aggregator-led VPP tariffs than to own residential hardware assets at scale.

## 4. FEASIBILITY ASSESSMENT

The "Lunar for Utilities" white-label hypothesis is strategically coherent but requires a significant reframing of both the commercial model and the realistic near-term target set. Here is a structured assessment:

### What Is Structurally Feasible (High Confidence)

**The GMP-style utility-owned lease model:** Lunar could pursue partnership with small-to-medium utilities (similar to GMP's scale) seeking to offer a branded residential battery program. GMP's expansion to Enphase in November 2025 establishes that utilities are willing to add new hardware partners, not remain exclusively Tesla-dependent. A utility seeking a domestic-content-compliant, FEOC-free system with integrated smart panel and VPP dispatch capability would find Lunar's spec sheet compelling. Target utilities for this model: Green Mountain Power (already doing this), Vermont Electric Co-op, Holy Cross Energy (CO), Seattle City Light, Austin Energy, Hawaiian Electric.

**The Base Power-style DER-as-a-service model:** Rather than selling hardware to utilities, Lunar could pursue a managed service structure analogous to Base Power's EPE contract: Lunar installs and maintains residential batteries in utility service territories; the utility controls dispatch via Gridshare; customers receive free or subsidized backup power. The utility pays Lunar a per-MW or per-kWh grid services fee from ratepayer cost avoidance. This avoids the capital requirements of a direct purchase and positions Gridshare as the platform of record. Base Power's success with this model at EPE — despite being only 2 years old at contract award — directly validates this approach for an early-stage Lunar.

**The Sonnen/RMP Wattsmart aggregated incentive model:** Lunar's Gridshare platform could participate as the dispatch software for a utility VPP program in which *customer-owned* Lunar systems are incentivized through a tariff. In this model, Lunar doesn't need to white-label hardware to a utility — it positions Gridshare as the aggregation platform and Lunar Systems as the preferred hardware within the utility's incentive program. This is the lowest-regulatory-barrier entry and most consistent with existing Gridshare CCA relationships in California.

### What Is Feasible but Hard (Medium Confidence)

**Dominion Virginia residential battery procurement:** The regulatory timeline is real but uncertain. The SCC rejected the initial residential battery pilot and sent it to stakeholder process. If that process concludes favorably and Dominion launches a utility-owned residential battery procurement (rather than a customer incentive tariff), Lunar would be a credible bidder given domestic content compliance. However, the more likely Dominion outcome is a BYOD incentive tariff with hardware-agnostic eligibility — Lunar would compete in that market through its installer channel, not a direct utility contract.

**The 22,500-unit Virginia math:** The full 450 MW VPP mandate, if converted to Lunar 20 kWh systems, would indeed require 22,500 units — but this assumes: (a) all 450 MW is residential batteries (false — includes thermostats, EVs, hot water heaters), (b) Dominion owns and installs the hardware (unlikely for most of the capacity), and (c) Lunar wins a competitive RFP against Tesla Powerwall, Enphase, and Base Power-style managed service providers. The realistic residential battery hardware opportunity within Dominion's VPP is likely the mandated 15 MW floor (~750 Lunar 20 kWh systems), not 22,500.

### What Is Not Feasible Now (Low Confidence)

**Large-scale IOU utility-scale storage RFPs:** Dominion's October 2025 battery storage RFP, Xcel's Capacity*Connect, and Illinois's 1 GW IPA procurement are all for grid-scale battery systems (1 MW+), not residential units. Lunar's residential system architecture is the wrong product for these opportunities.

**Any utility RFP requiring multi-hundred-MWh demonstrated deployments:** With ~2,000 cumulative units, Lunar cannot satisfy the track record requirements of Salt River Project-style procurement minimums. However, as the EPE/Base Power precedent shows, residential-focused pilot program RFPs apply significantly lower thresholds.

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY FOR DECISION-MAKERS

**Bottom line:** The "Lunar for Utilities" hypothesis is directionally correct and strategically timely — but the mechanism, scale timeline, and immediate target set need substantial recalibration.

The utility DER procurement wave is definitively real. Eight or more states now have legislative mandates or active regulatory proceedings for utility-integrated battery programs. The OBBBA-driven FEOC/domestic content tightening creates a genuine supply chain moat for a US-manufactured, FEOC-free system. And the Base Power/EPE precedent (February 2026) proves that a 2-year-old company with ~100 MWh deployed can win an IOU utility contract — Lunar's position is arguably comparable.

However, **the near-term opportunity is primarily in customer-incentive VPP programs, managed-service DER models, and small-utility owned-asset programs** — not large IOU hardware procurement at the Virginia math's 22,500-unit scale. The most actionable immediate targets are:

1. **Entergy New Orleans** — $28M program, implementation plan due March 2026, hardware procurement in H1 2026; right-sized for Lunar's current production capacity
2. **Green Mountain Power** or comparable small utility — seeking second hardware vendor beyond Tesla/Enphase; Lunar's spec is ideal
3. **Dominion Virginia VPP stakeholder process** — Lunar should be at the table now, shaping the residential battery program structure before it is filed with the SCC
4. **ComEd/Ameren Illinois scheduled dispatch VPP** — tariff due June 1, 2026; Lunar needs Gridshare positioned as the dispatch platform pre-filing

The competitive window is **narrowing rapidly**. Base Power raised $1 billion in October 2025 specifically to scale utility partnerships. Sonnen has 4,000+ batteries in Rocky Mountain Power's system and is scaling to 10,000 in Texas. GMP just added Enphase. Every month Lunar delays engaging the utility channel, one of these competitors is establishing the reference customer relationship that defines the preferred vendor list for the next decade of utility DER procurement.
