## 4. FEASIBILITY ASSESSMENT

### 4.1 Technical Feasibility: Two Distinct Problems

**Problem 1 — Per-Circuit Visibility:** For Bridge to manage non-Lunar devices, it needs to "see" what each circuit is doing in real time. Either approach adds cost and complexity to the installation but does not require a Bridge hardware redesign.

**Problem 2 — Device Control:** Reading power is easier than controlling devices. Controlling a Daikin heat pump requires integration with its proprietary BACnet/Modbus/Daikin-specific protocol; controlling a ChargePoint EV charger requires OCPP integration; controlling a Rheem water heater requires their EcoNet API. Gridshare already does this at the battery level for dozens of manufacturer SKUs. 
Gridshare also operates in Europe and Asia where energy markets are more advanced.
 The EU regulatory environment requiring demand response from heat pumps and EV chargers means Gridshare has likely already built some of these integrations for European markets. The question is whether Lunar's software team can port and scale them to the U.S. device ecosystem.

**Engineering Resource Assessment:** Alarm.com's developer ecosystem required substantial ongoing investment in platform APIs. 
Alarm.com invests heavily in R&D to build its core cloud platform, AI-driven analytics, and a vast ecosystem that integrates with over 100 million devices from various third-party manufacturers.

4. **Utility White-Label:** Utilities pay Lunar to run demand-response programs through Bridge-connected homes. This is Gridshare's existing business model with Sunrun and CCAs.

- **Lunar can execute this platform strategy while simultaneously scaling hardware manufacturing and installer training.** ⚠️ LOW-MEDIUM CONFIDENCE. Building a developer platform requires a distinct organizational capability (developer relations, API governance, partner integration engineering) that is fundamentally different from hardware R&D and VPP optimization. Alarm.com's advantage was that it was *only* a software/platform company—it never touched hardware manufacturing.
- **FERC Order 2222 will unlock wholesale market VPP revenue in the 2025–2028 window.** ⚠️ LOW CONFIDENCE. Regulatory implementation is confirmed as slow and contested: 
Rauscher concedes that while it feels disappointing to have seen Order 2222, which could simplify and standardize market participation at scale for aggregated DERs, fail to have an impact, the conversation around the value of DERs in the electricity system has at least been opened.


### 6.4 Overall Hypothesis Verdict

**SUPPORTED, CONDITIONALLY.** The strategic logic is sound and the structural analogy to Alarm.com is apt. Lunar's Bridge + Gridshare combination has genuine platform potential that exceeds any current competitor in one critical dimension: Gridshare is the only residential energy management software that has proven VPP co-optimization at scale across multi-manufacturer device fleets. Neither SPAN, Savant, nor Eaton AbleEdge has this. However, the hypothesis will only be validated if Lunar (a) resolves the hardware question around per-circuit sensing; (b) builds the developer API infrastructure and OEM partnership model before Eaton, SPAN, or Google Nest/Amazon Alexa energy management capabilities crowd it out; and (c) preserves the Eaton partnership while competing in Eaton's territory. The 36–48 month window before these competitive forces mature is Lunar's critical execution period.

## STRATEGIC SYNTHESIS: THE FOUR THINGS LUNAR MUST DO IN THE NEXT 24 MONTHS

Based on this analysis, the hypothesis succeeds or fails on four specific decisions that Lunar must make in its current financing window:

1. **Resolve the Hardware Question Now.** Commission an independent technical audit of whether Bridge's current architecture can support per-circuit sensing sufficient for non-Lunar device management, or whether the Eaton AbleEdge partnership is sufficient as a sensing overlay. If Bridge needs a hardware revision, the Series C/D capital should fund it before the design is frozen at scale.

2. **Build the Gridshare Developer API Before SPAN Does.** SPAN has the panel, lacks the VPP intelligence. Lunar has the VPP intelligence, may lack the panel-level sensor resolution. A Gridshare Developer Program with documented APIs for heat pump OEMs, EV charger companies, and water heater manufacturers—structured as a joint value-creation partnership rather than a toll gate—should launch before 2027.

3. **Structure the Eaton Relationship as a Sensing-as-a-Service Partnership.** Eaton's AbleEdge provides revenue-grade per-circuit metering via open APIs. Lunar should formalize a commercial agreement where AbleEdge breakers feed circuit-level data into Gridshare's optimization engine, making every Eaton-paneled home a Gridshare-optimizable home without requiring Bridge installation. This extends Gridshare's TAM by an order of magnitude.

4. **Adopt the Alarm.com Dealer Model Explicitly.** The key structural lesson from Alarm.com is that *the installer/dealer channel earns recurring revenue from the platform, creating a self-reinforcing distribution flywheel.* Lunar should structure installer economics so that Sunrun, regional solar installers, and HVAC contractors share in the recurring SaaS revenue from every multi-device integration they enable—making them active advocates for expanding Bridge's device footprint in each home.

The platform opportunity is real. The window is narrow. The execution risk is high. But the foundational asset—a proven AI-VPP platform controlling 650MW of devices across three continents—is one that no competitor currently possesses.
