## SECTION 1: BATTERY CELL SUPPLY CHAIN

### A. SK Group — Entity, Chemistry, Capacity

**Cell supplier**: SK Battery America (SKBA), Commerce, Jackson County, Georgia — subsidiary of SK On. Georgia facility: 2.7M sq ft, two plants, 22 GWh/year nameplate — but overwhelmingly allocated to NMC cells for Ford and Hyundai EVs.

**Critical LFP gap**: SK On has been an NMC-only manufacturer for its entire commercial history. LFP production at SK Battery America does not begin until H2 2026, following a 7.2 GWh deal with Flatiron Energy Development over 4 years (~1.8 GWh/year). Chinese companies (CATL, BYD) dominate LFP; Korean makers concentrated on NCM.

**Where are Lunar's cells today?** Lunar claims cells from "the United States and Europe." SK On Georgia has no commercial LFP production today. Likely sources: (a) SK On Hungary plant, (b) undisclosed alternative supplier, or (c) NMC chemistry. CEO Girotra's claim company "only contracts with battery manufacturers in the US" is inconsistent with the "US and Europe" website language.

**Contractual arrangement**: Undisclosed. SK plays "partnership and supply partner" role per Girotra — language avoiding "exclusive" or "contracted."

**Volume requirements**: Current: ~10,000 units x 20 kWh = ~200 MWh/year. 2028 target: 100,000 units = ~2 GWh/year (9% of SK Battery America's 22 GWh nameplate, which is nearly fully spoken for by Ford, Hyundai, and Flatiron).

**Cell costs**: Chinese LFP ~$87/kWh (up from $73 in 2023); US-made Korean cells $85–90/kWh. At 200 MWh/year: ~$17–18M/year. At 2 GWh/year: ~$170–180M/year. Lunar is a micro-customer (~0.9% of SK On GA capacity) with zero pricing leverage.

### B. Alternative Sources & Dual-Sourcing

No disclosed secondary cell suppliers. US LFP alternatives (AESC Smyrna TN, LG Holland MI, Freyr GA, Microvast TX) — none represents reliable 2 GWh/year residential LFP by 2028. Dual-sourcing industry standard requires volumes >500 MWh/year. **Lunar almost certainly has no dual-source cell supply today — critical single point of failure.**

### C. FEOC Compliance

SK On (South Korea) is not a FEOC. However, upstream graphite anode is the critical exposure. China produced 85% of world anodes, 82% of electrolytes, 74% of separators, 70% of cathodes in 2022. BTR (Chinese graphite company) was classified FEOC in January 2025 — disrupting US firms' plans. China imposed export controls on graphite/cathode materials effective November 2025. SK On partnered with L&F (Korean cathode maker) — cathode origin is Korea, but anode graphite remains China-dependent.

Lunar claims FEOC compliance and domestic content qualification. **Currently defensible** given IRS transition relief through 2027. **Structurally fragile** as upstream graphite FEOC rules tighten post-2027.

### Component-Level Assessment

| Component | Location | Domestic? | FEOC Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Battery cells | "US and Europe" (current source unclear) | Partial | Moderate (graphite) |
| Battery module assembly | **Mexico** | **NO** | Low |
| Inverter | Suwanee, Georgia | Yes | Moderate (semiconductors) |

| Maximizers | Suwanee, Georgia | Yes | Moderate (chips) |
| PCBAs | Unknown | Unknown | High |
| Software/Firmware | Mountain View, CA + London, UK | Mostly domestic | None |

**Critical finding**: Battery module assembly (cell sorting, busbar welding, BMS installation, housing) in Mexico — the highest-value-added step. Under current 40% domestic content threshold, Lunar likely passes. **Threshold rises to 55% after 2026** — Mexico module assembly becomes existential threat to 10% domestic content bonus.

**Competitive comparison**: Tesla Powerwall 3 — CATL (Chinese) cells, significant FEOC exposure. Enphase/FranklinWH — Chinese supply chain exposure. **Lunar has lowest FEOC risk of peer group, but Mexico module assembly is a meaningful asterisk.** "Proudly domestic content" marketing overstates reality.

## SECTION 3: MANUFACTURING SCALE-UP

### Current Footprint
1. **Mountain View, CA** (HQ, ~35K sq ft) — Design/Engineering
2. **Suwanee, Georgia** — Inverter, Bridge, Maximizer assembly
3. **Mexico** — Battery module assembly
4. **Washington State** — Role unspecified (likely distribution)

~226 total employees globally (June 2025). Estimated 50–80 direct manufacturing workers. ~2,000 systems deployed in California.

### 10K → 20K (by end 2026): Achievable
Requires: spare floorspace in Suwanee or expansion; Mexico facility doubles shifts; cell supply doubles to 400 MWh/year (manageable). Estimated CapEx: $15–25M. Additional workforce: +50–80 manufacturing employees.

### 20K → 100K (by end 2028): Highly Ambitious
**Floorspace**: Need dedicated 200,000–400,000 sq ft facility. 18–24 month timeline from decision to operational.
**Workforce**: ~500–800 direct manufacturing (vs. ~226 total today). 3–6 month training per worker.
**Cell supply**: 2 GWh/year. SK On LFP committed primarily to Flatiron (7.2 GWh/4 years). No evidence of dedicated Lunar supply agreement.
**CapEx**: New facility $50–100M; equipment $30–60M; working capital $50–80M. **Total ~$150–250M** — approximately the entire $232M raise, leaving limited buffer.

**Tesla benchmark**: 10K to 100K+ annual Powerwalls took 5+ years with Giga Nevada infrastructure. Claimed 700K/year production but celebrated 1,000/day milestone (inconsistent with ~1,900/day implied). Lunar attempting 10K→100K in 3 years.

**Realistic trajectory**: Absent major cell supply deal and new facility announcement by mid-2026, more likely **40,000–60,000 units/year by end 2028**.

## NOTABLE ANOMALIES

### ANOMALY 1 (Critical): LFP Cell Sourcing Gap
Lunar claims "US and Europe" cells + SK partnership. But SK On Georgia does not produce LFP commercially — not until H2 2026. CEO says "only contracts with battery manufacturers in the US" while website says "US and Europe." **Mutually inconsistent statements.** Current source is either SK Hungary, undisclosed supplier, or NMC chemistry.

### ANOMALY 2 (Critical): Mexico Module Assembly vs. "Proudly Domestic Content"
Website quietly discloses module assembly "just over the border in Mexico" while marketing "proudly domestic content." Module is the most value-intensive battery manufacturing step. As threshold rises to 55% post-2026, **may disqualify from 10% domestic content bonus**, eroding leasing company economics.

### ANOMALY 3: 2,000 Deployed vs. 10,000/Year Claimed Capacity
If capacity is 10K/year but cumulative is only 2,000 — either capacity ramped recently, is overstated, or demand lags supply. Suggests 10K is nameplate, not actual production.

### ANOMALY 4: Sole-Source Cell Supply
For a company targeting 100K units/year of life-safety product, **no disclosed secondary cell supplier** is highly unusual. Automotive-grade mandates dual/triple sourcing. Unmitigated force majeure risk.

### ANOMALY 5: Tesla's Domestic Content Exposure Window — But Narrow
Tesla Powerwall relies on Chinese cells — genuine Lunar FEOC advantage for Section 48E leasing. But Tesla's 700K+ unit scale means it can negotiate sourcing changes from strength. If Tesla secures domestic LFP, Lunar's differentiation evaporates.

### ANOMALY 6: China Export Controls Threaten SK On's LFP Supply
China's export controls include LFP cathode materials. SK On/L&F cathode supply chain may have Chinese-origin precursor inputs. Could disrupt SK On's Georgia LFP line before reaching full production.

### ANOMALY 7: Eaton Partnership Under-Leveraged
2024 partnership to integrate AbleEdge smart breakers. Eaton: $23B revenue, deep US manufacturing/procurement/distribution. Potential to solve multiple scale-up challenges if deepened beyond product integration.

### ANOMALY 8: Gridshare — Hidden Asset Changing Scale Economics
Gridshare manages ~130,000 batteries (mostly non-Lunar) — **65x Lunar's hardware fleet**. Software platform ahead of hardware by enormous factor. Software revenue/strategic value substantially greater than hardware revenue suggests. Investors effectively buying VPP software company at hardware-company optics.

### ANOMALY 9 (Critical): 100K Unit Target Implies ~$200M+ Undisclosed Manufacturing CapEx
At $232M total raised (funding software, expansion, operations too), there is not enough capital for 100K/year factory infrastructure. Requires new facility (~$100M+) or major contract manufacturing deal or further capital raise. Target appears **strategic aspiration designed to attract capital** rather than fully financed plan.

### ANOMALY 10: Washington State Role Unexplained
Consistently mentioned alongside CA and GA for manufacturing but never explained. Likely distribution/integration hub, but lack of transparency is unusual for a "Made in America" company.
