## SECTION 1: HARDWARE HEAD-TO-HEAD

### A) LUNAR vs. TESLA POWERWALL 3

| Dimension | Tesla Powerwall 3 | Lunar System |
|---|---|---|
| Usable Capacity | 13.5 kWh | 20 kWh (10–30 kWh modular) |
| Continuous Power | 11.5 kW | 9.6 kW |
| Peak Power | 22 kW (surge) | 15 kW |
| Chemistry | LFP | Lithium-ion |
| Warranty | 10 years | 12.5 years |
| Round-trip Efficiency | ~97% | 95.5% |
| Coupling | AC (hybrid inverter) | DC |
| Price Premium vs. Tesla | — | ~10% |

**Pricing**: Powerwall 3 ~$9–10K installed ($667–$740/kWh). Lunar ~$10–11K all-in for 20 kWh (~$500–$550/kWh) — superior on capacity-adjusted basis. CEO Girotra: "With the other companies, you have to add other components to get an apples-to-apples comparison."

**Installation**: Lunar installs in ~3 hours. Single 50A breaker for solar+battery simplifies permitting. Indoor/outdoor, wall/floor mount.

**Software**: Lunar's AI earned customers average $464 VPP + $338 bill savings = $802/year. Tesla VPP pays up to $350/year/Powerwall. Lunar app won iF Design Award.

**Tesla Brand Deterioration**: Roth Capital survey shows installers shifting away from Tesla. EnergySage: of homeowners mentioning Tesla, 13.5% expressed unfavorable views, 68% requested alternatives. Tesla marketplace share fell from 63% (H2 2024) to 59% (H1 2025). Tesla's inverters use "little to no" domestic content — direct tailwind for Lunar.

Model doesn't transfer easily to California IOUs or Northeast. Lunar's Gridshare captures VPP software revenue without battery asset risk.

## SECTION 2: VPP SOFTWARE — GRIDSHARE POSITIONING

**Gridshare scale**: 650 MW devices under management globally. 100,000+ devices across 3 continents. Manages all of Sunrun's VPPs including ~130,000 home batteries — most from non-Lunar manufacturers. Device-agnostic across batteries, thermostats, EVs.

**Unique claim**: Only company making own hardware AND software that works with others' hardware. Girotra: "Android-like software strategy" vs. Tesla/Enphase "walled gardens."

**Performance**: Gridshare squeezed ~60% more energy from Sunrun batteries than previous dispatch, while maintaining backup reserves.

**Tesla VPP**: Closed ecosystem (Powerwall-only). California VPPs delivered 500 MW in July 2025. Tesla paid Powerwall owners $9.9M in 2024 VPP programs. Up to $350/year per Powerwall vs. Lunar's $464 average.

**EnergyHub**: 70+ utilities, 2.0 GW flexible capacity. FranklinWH partnered with EnergyHub — direct Gridshare competition.

## SECTION 3: MARKET SHARE & TRAJECTORY

**US residential battery market**: 1.8 GWh installed 2023. 57.6 GWh total storage in 2025 (+30% YoY). Residential storage surged 51% YoY. Market valued at $1,520M (2024), projected $5,092M by 2032 (14.36% CAGR).

**Market share (H1 2025)**: Tesla 59% (down from 63%), Enphase 25% (down from 29%), FranklinWH 3rd. Lunar not in top 8 nationally — sub-1% share. ~2,000 systems in California.

**Battery attachment rates**: National 45% (H2 2024 all-time high). California 79%, Texas 61%, Arizona 47%, Florida 43%. California rates jumped from 11% (2022) to 55% (2024) due to NEM 3.0.

**Average $/kWh**: Tesla $926/kWh on EnergySage. Market bifurcating: commodity players pushing prices down; premium players (Lunar, Sonnen, Enphase) holding prices via software bundling.

## SECTION 5: ANALOGOUS PLAYBOOKS

**FranklinWH**: Zero to 8th nationally in 2 years, 3rd by 2025. Playbook: installer-obsession, aggressive certification, AVL listings, whole-home backup differentiation, VPP via EnergyHub. Lunar can replicate the installer obsession but is playing a more complex two-sided game (hardware + VPP).

**Ring vs. Nest**: Channel breadth won over product sophistication. Mass retail + installer engagement > boutique D2C. Lunar's 40-installer model is boutique; Ring counsel says go wide fast.

**Rivian vs. Tesla**: Differentiation by use case > out-speccing leader. Lunar should resist out-speccing Tesla; instead own: NEM 3.0 optimization, VPP-first customers, space-constrained installs, domestic content/FEOC.

**Sonos vs. Bose**: Software-defined product + affluent installer channel + ease of setup. Most apt parallel for Lunar. Risk: Sonos hit a wall expanding beyond core affluent channel.

## SECTION 7: COMPETITIVE SCENARIOS (2025–2028)

**BULL (3–5% share by 2028)**: Tesla brand deterioration accelerates; installer network scales 40→500+; Texas expansion succeeds; Gridshare becomes CA utility VPP standard; Enphase continues losing share.

**BASE (1–2% share by 2028)**: CA + TX + 2 more states. 200–300 installer partners. 3–5 new CCA/utility Gridshare relationships. Primary constraint: $232M capital split between manufacturing ramp and installer development.

## NOTABLE ANOMALIES

**1. DSGS Defunding — Quietly Devastating**: Gridshare aggregated 500 MW of Sunrun batteries for California's DSGS program. Funding vetoed by Gov. Newsom. Removes primary near-term VPP revenue driver and undercuts $464 average VPP earnings claim. Not addressed in Lunar's $232M raise communications.

**2. Tesla Simultaneously Declining AND Expanding**: Tesla ended Model S/X production for autonomous robot manufacturing AND unveiled new 420-watt solar panel with 30%+ installation time reduction. If Tesla's solar re-entry succeeds, could recreate integrated solar+Powerwall bundle.

**3. "Solar Orphan" Opportunity (~1M+ Customers) Unaddressed**: SunPower, Titan Solar, ADT Solar bankruptcies left 1M+ stranded residential solar customers — no incumbent installer. Lunar's DC-coupling and retrofit compatibility differentiate here.

**4. FranklinWH Building VPP Capability That Challenges Gridshare**: Already in 3 utility programs via EnergyHub across MA, RI, CT, NC. Hardware-first approach with 3,000 installers could reach VPP scale faster than Lunar's hardware ramp allows.

**5. Lunar's $464 VPP Earnings May Be Artificially High**: Figure earned during DSGS peak 2024 summer. With DSGS defunded, 2025 enrollees face materially lower earnings. If marketing continues "$800+ savings" without DSGS disclosure, installer/homeowner trust erodes.

**6. Installer Preferences Shifting Faster Than Lunar Can Capitalize**: Large TPO installers pivoting from Tesla name Franklin, Enphase, SolarEdge as alternatives — Lunar not mentioned. With 40 partners, Lunar is invisible during the moment of maximum opportunity.

**7. Base Power's $4B Valuation**: 100 MWh deployed; significant execution risk but attracts tier-1 VC capital (a16z, CapitalG). The "free battery" model may not fully materialize outside deregulated ERCOT, but signals where the market is heading.
