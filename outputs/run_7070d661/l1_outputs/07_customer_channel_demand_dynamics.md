# LUNAR ENERGY DEMAND CHAIN ANALYSIS (COMPRESSED)
**February 2026**

## SECTION 1: END CUSTOMER ANALYSIS

### Demographics
Median income of solar adopters: $115K (2023), down from $141K (2010), but still above US median ($75K) and owner-occupied median ($94K). Battery adoption more income-skewed: households <$50K had 6.4 kW solar with 6% battery storage; households >$200K had 8.0 kW with 14% battery storage. Solar adopters more likely college-educated, middle-aged, non-Hispanic white, single-family home.

### Purchase Motivations
**Homeowners**: Utility rate savings (30%), solar self-supply (29%), backup power (26%). **Installers pitch**: 67% lead with resilience/backup, only 11% with financial savings. **This mismatch hurts Lunar** — its $802/year savings ($464 VPP + $338 software) is the most powerful customer argument but buried in specs rather than leading sales narrative.

### Price Sensitivity
$25K median residential battery system cost. Post-25D elimination: homeowner cash purchase up 43% ($17.5K → $25K). Cost remains #1 barrier (55% of prospective buyers cite). Shift to TPO/lease: TPO accounted for 57% of market in Q3 2025, helps mitigate 25D expiration.

### VPP Engagement
Lunar average: $464 VPP earnings + $338 bill savings = $802/year. DSGS specifically: up to $450 for 20 kWh system (max 35 events, May–Oct). Range likely $100–$600/year depending on market and dispatch. Sunrun: ~35% of batteries enrolled in VPP, growing. 106,000+ customers in VPP programs by end Q3 2025. 70% storage attachment on new Sunrun customers. Sunrun scaled to 100,000+ VPP enrollees in 2025, dispatched 18 GWh.

## SECTION 2: INSTALLER CHANNEL

### Economics
15–25% gross margins on equipment + $1,500–$3,000 labor. For $20–30K Lunar project: equipment $12–18K wholesale, labor $1,500–$3,000, permitting $500–$1,500, installer margin $3,000–$6,000. **Lunar's 3-hour install** vs. Powerwall 4–8 hours = 2 installs/day per crew vs. 1. This doubles effective capacity per technician — a unit economics advantage enabling higher installer margins at same wholesale price.

### Installer Preferences (ranked)
1. Margin (most important)
2. Installation ease/speed (Lunar's clear advantage)
3. Reliability/warranty support (Tesla/Enphase lead; Lunar unproven at scale)
4. Customer demand/brand pull (Tesla dominant; Lunar essentially zero)
5. Financing availability
6. Training and support quality

**Installer feedback on Lunar**: Positive — compact modular form, 3-hour install, beautiful aesthetics, app demos well. Concerns — limited brand awareness, unproven warranty track record, California-only footprint.

### Network Scale
Lunar: ~40 certified partners, ~2,000 systems (50/partner average; many likely <10). Tesla: thousands nationally + direct crews. Enphase: tens of thousands. FranklinWH: 3,000+. Lunar needs 200–300 partners for 20K units, currently has 40.

## SECTION 3: SUNRUN CHANNEL

### Scale & Battery Trajectory
Sunrun: record 71% storage attachment rate Q4 2025. 237,000+ battery customers total. 392 MWh batteries installed Q2 2025 (+48% YoY). Attachment rate trajectory: 18% (Q2 2023) → 54% (Q2 2024) → 70% (Q3 2025).

### Dual Relationship
1. **Hardware**: Sunrun installs Lunar Systems in portfolio alongside Tesla Powerwall and others. Exact Lunar share undisclosed.
2. **Software (Gridshare)**: Manages ALL of Sunrun's VPPs — ~130,000 batteries across 12+ markets (New England, Hawaii, Puerto Rico), most non-Lunar hardware. Sunrun owns equity stake in Lunar.

### Business Model Alignment
Sunrun lease model: Sunrun owns equipment, claims ITC (available through 2027–28), sells to customer via lease/PPA. Sunrun's 4 GWh networked storage capacity across 18 VPP programs, 425 MW grid dispatch at peak.

**Critical tension**: Sunrun CEO Mary Powell highlighted VPP partnerships with NRG and **Tesla** in Texas earnings call. Tesla is direct VPP competitor to Gridshare within Sunrun ecosystem. Lunar's position is non-exclusive and contestable.

**ITC squeeze**: Expiration could deprive Sunrun of ~$6,200/system after 2027. Sunrun response: source lower-cost equipment, reduce CAC, boost grid services. "Lower-cost equipment sourcing" is **direct threat to Lunar's ~10% price premium**.

### Concentration Risk
If Sunrun represents 50%+ of Lunar hardware sales (likely given 2,000 units across 40 partners):

**Sunrun financial distress**: Altman Z-Score of -0.47 (distress zone). Total debt $14.6B vs. equity $4.6B (315.4% D/E). Stock fell 27.9% in single session to $14.72. However: $377M cash generated in 2025, paid down $150M recourse debt. No recourse debt maturities until March 2028.

**Alternative large partners**: Sunnova (#2 lessor, TX/FL/PR), Freedom Forever (#2 independent, 7% nationally), Palmetto, Base Power. No evidence of formal Lunar partnership discussions beyond Sunrun as of early 2026.

## SECTION 4: DEMAND FORECASTING

### US Residential Battery Market

| Year | Installs (MW) | YoY Growth | Driver |
|------|--------------|------------|--------|
| 2023 | ~800 MW | ~100% | NEM 3.0 |
| 2024 | 1,250+ MW | 57% | NEM 3.0 full effect, ITC pull-forward |
| 2025E | ~2,000+ MW | ~60–70% | ITC expiration rush |
| **2026E** | **~1,900 MW** | **-6%** | **Post-ITC headwind** |

Q3 2025: 647 MW installed (+70% YoY). 5-year outlook: 12 GW distributed storage, residential = 80% (~10 GW, 2024–2028). Leaders: California, Arizona, Illinois.

### Lunar's Targets
Manufacturing: 10K/year (current) → 20K (end 2026) → 100K (end 2028). Deployed: ~2,000 today → ~10,000 by end 2026 ("at least doubling" annually).

**8–10K units in 2026**: US market ~80–100K residential systems. Lunar needs 8–10% national share — from California-only company with 40 partners and 2,000 cumulative installs, this is 4–5x jump in declining (-6%) market. Extremely ambitious.

**100K in 2028**: At 20 kWh/unit = 2 GWh — roughly entire current US residential market. Would require 40–50% national share. TAM enormous (1M of 75M homes have storage) but execution formidable.

### Demand Drivers

**Positive**: Grid stress (outages up 80% in decade; Sunrun customers used batteries in 650,000+ outages in 2025). NEM 3.0 (CA attachment jumped 72%). Rising rates (Orange County +5.8–13.1% Oct 2025, 171–195% above national average). VPP market ($6.65B → $20.56B by 2030, 25.3% CAGR). TPO shift (57% of market Q3 2025).

**Negative**: 25D ITC expiration hangover (residential storage forecast to decline 6% in 2026). Sunrun ITC squeeze ($6,200/system revenue loss after 2027 → pressure on Lunar wholesale pricing). Battery pack prices fell to $70/kWh in 2025 (-45% from 2024) — cost gap with commoditized alternatives widens.

## SECTION 5: C&I OPPORTUNITY

Lunar has "businesses" in its deployment count — suggests early C&I but no documented cases. Global C&I storage: $7.94B (2025) → $31.67B (2035). Small C&I (restaurants, retail, offices: 50–200 kWh) is genuinely underserved gap between residential and utility-scale. Lunar's modular 20 kWh could stack to 60–100 kWh. Barriers: three-phase power, commercial BMS, demand charge software, different sales channel. Premature at current scale.

## NOTABLE ANOMALIES

### ANOMALY 1 (Critical): Gridshare Paradox — Software at Scale, Hardware Not
Gridshare manages ~650 MW distributed devices, but Lunar has only ~2,000 hardware installs (~40 MW). **Software 15–20x more deployed than hardware.** Lunar is already the dominant VPP software for US residential storage at scale through others' hardware. Fundamental strategic question: software company using hardware to prove thesis, or hardware company using software as moat?

### ANOMALY 2 (Critical): Sunrun's Tesla Relationship Contradicts Assumed Exclusivity
Sunrun CEO promoting Tesla Texas VPP alongside Gridshare California VPP. Sunrun hedging with multiple technology providers. If Lunar assumed exclusivity, this is a red flag.

### ANOMALY 3 (Critical): 2,000→20,000 Unit Ramp Implausible Without Installer Explosion
20K units with 40 partners = 500/partner/year = 10/week. Most installers do 100–300 batteries/year total. **Need 200–300 certified partners** to hit 20K. Gap not publicly acknowledged.

### ANOMALY 4: Installer-Customer Motivation Mismatch
Homeowners prioritize savings; installers pitch backup. Lunar's messaging emphasizes backup/aesthetics (installer-aligned), not $802/year savings (customer-aligned). Unforced marketing error.

### ANOMALY 5: Sunrun Financial Distress as Lunar Existential Risk
Z-Score -0.47, $14.6B debt/$4.6B equity. If Sunrun represents 50%+ of Lunar hardware revenue and dominant Gridshare SaaS revenue, restructuring could be devastating. Not mentioned in investor materials. $232M raise may partly be to reduce dependency.

### ANOMALY 6: 25D Expiration Creates Lease Window — But Squeeze Pressures Lunar Pricing
TPO shift is structural tailwind. But Sunrun's $6,200/system revenue loss after 2027 means pressure to source lower-cost equipment — **direct threat to Lunar's 10% price premium**.

### ANOMALY 7: California Concentration = NEM 3.0 Dependency
Nearly all 2,000 deployments in CA. Battery boom is NEM 3.0 function. Any policy reversal, SGIP exhaustion, or rate changes collapse primary market. Geographic diversification timelines: 12–18 months minimum per new state.

### ANOMALY 8: "Android of the Grid" Positioning Underexploited
If Gridshare becomes platform managing all residential batteries, Lunar captures SaaS revenue per kWh dispatched regardless of brand. VPP market growing to $20.56B by 2030. This software-as-grid-infrastructure business is fundamentally higher-margin, lower-capital than hardware — and Lunar is not publicly valuing it as standalone business.

### ANOMALY 9: Low-Income Segment Ignored
$280M CPUC initiative + SGIP Equity ($1,000/kWh). At 20 kWh, Lunar System could be **fully rebated** for qualifying customers. Counterintuitive volume opportunity in less-competitive market segment aligned with environmental justice mandates.

### ANOMALY 10: 3-Hour Install Understates Competitive Moat
50–60% labor reduction vs. Powerwall. Two installs/day vs. one per crew. **At scale, this is unit economics advantage enabling higher installer margins at same wholesale price.** Almost absent from public positioning.
