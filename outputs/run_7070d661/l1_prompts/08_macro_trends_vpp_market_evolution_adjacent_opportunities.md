You are a strategic research analyst examining macro trends, market evolution, and adjacent opportunities relevant to Lunar Energy's business. Lunar sells residential battery systems (20 kWh) through installers and operates a VPP software platform (Gridshare) managing 150,000+ devices. Your task is to map the forces shaping the company's operating environment and identify specific, actionable adjacent market opportunities.

SECTION 1: ELECTRICITY MARKET MACRO TRENDS

A) Demand growth:
- US electricity demand has been flat for 15 years but is now growing. Quantify the expected demand growth through 2030, by driver:
  - Data center buildout (AI, cloud computing): How many GW of new demand? Where geographically?
  - Electric vehicle adoption: How many additional GWh of residential charging by 2030?
  - Heat pump adoption (electrification of heating): Incremental residential electricity demand?
  - Industrial reshoring: Manufacturing demand growth?
- What does this demand growth mean for grid stress, electricity prices, and the value of distributed storage?

B) Grid reliability crisis:
- Map major grid reliability events of the past 3 years (Texas 2021/2024, California rolling blackouts, hurricane impacts, winter storms)
- How many US households experienced power outages in 2023, 2024? Average duration?
- Which regions are most vulnerable to future grid stress?
- How does grid unreliability drive consumer demand for battery backup?
- What is the estimated "willingness to pay" for backup power capability vs. pure bill savings?

C) Electricity price trajectory:
- Average US residential electricity prices: 2020 vs. 2025, by state
- Projected electricity price growth through 2030
- Which states have the highest residential rates? (Hawaii, California, Connecticut, Massachusetts, New York)
- At what electricity rate does a battery storage system become economically compelling without incentives?

D) Time-of-use rate proliferation:
- How many US utilities have adopted time-of-use (TOU) rates?
- What is the peak-to-off-peak price differential? (This drives battery arbitrage value)
- Are TOU rates expanding or contracting?

SECTION 2: VPP MARKET EVOLUTION — THE $100B OPPORTUNITY?

A) VPP market sizing:
- What is the current US VPP market size (MW enrolled, revenue generated)?
- How many utilities have active VPP programs?
- What is the projected VPP market size by 2030?
- How much utility transmission and distribution (T&D) investment could VPPs defer or avoid?

B) VPP business model evolution:
- Map the evolution of VPP business models:
  - Utility-owned (utility procures and controls equipment)
  - Third-party aggregator (company like Lunar aggregates customer-owned devices)
  - "Battery-as-a-Service" (company installs free battery, monetizes VPP — Base Power model)
  - Hybrid models
- Which model is winning? Why?
- Where does Lunar's model fit? Could Lunar adopt the Base Power "free battery" model?

C) Grid operator perspective:
- What do ISOs and utilities actually need from VPPs? (Capacity, energy, ancillary services, resilience)
- What are the technical requirements for VPP participation in wholesale markets?
- How reliable are residential VPPs vs. utility-scale batteries or gas peakers?
- What premium do grid operators pay for proven, reliable VPP capacity?

D) VPP technology requirements:
- What software capabilities are essential for next-generation VPPs?
- How important is forecasting accuracy vs. real-time dispatch vs. bidding optimization?
- Is there a "winner-take-most" dynamic in VPP software, or is it fragmented?
- Could Gridshare become a VPP platform standard, or will utilities develop their own?

SECTION 3: ADJACENT MARKET OPPORTUNITIES

For each potential adjacency, analyze the market size, competitive landscape, fit with Lunar's capabilities, and specific entry strategy:

A) Whole-home electrification:
- The "Bridge" smart panel suggests Lunar is thinking about more than just batteries. Could they become a whole-home energy management company?
- What does whole-home electrification include? (Solar, battery, heat pump, water heater, EV charger, smart panel, induction cooktop)
- How large is the whole-home electrification market? Who are the players? (Span.io for smart panels, various heat pump manufacturers)
- Could Lunar bundle all electrification equipment and offer "one-stop" whole-home conversion?
- What margins are available in whole-home electrification vs. battery-only?

B) Electric vehicle integration (V2H/V2G):
- The Honda partnership suggests EV integration interest. What is the V2H (vehicle-to-home) and V2G (vehicle-to-grid) market opportunity?
- Which EVs support bidirectional charging? (Ford F-150 Lightning, Hyundai Ioniq 5, Nissan Leaf, others)
- How could Lunar's system integrate with an EV as a secondary battery?
- What additional hardware would be needed?
- When does V2H/V2G reach meaningful adoption? (2026? 2028? 2030?)
- Could Gridshare optimize both stationary batteries and EV batteries simultaneously?

C) Commercial & Industrial (C&I) storage:
- Lunar currently focuses on residential (15-30 kWh). The C&I market uses 100-500 kWh systems.
- Could Lunar scale up its modular design for small commercial customers?
- What's the C&I storage market size? (Behind-the-meter commercial storage)
- Who serves this market? (Tesla Powerpack/Megapack, Fluence, Stem/AlsoEnergy)
- Is there an underserved segment (small businesses: restaurants, retail, offices) that needs 50-100 kWh?

D) Energy-as-a-Service:
- Could Lunar transition from selling hardware to providing "energy-as-a-service" — where homeowners pay a monthly fee and Lunar owns/operates the battery?
- This is essentially what Base Power does. Could Lunar replicate it?
- What are the financing requirements for an asset-heavy energy-as-a-service model?
- What margins are achievable?

E) Grid services marketplace:
- Could Gridshare evolve into a two-sided marketplace — matching distributed energy resources with utility/grid operator needs?
- This would be a platform play similar to Uber for energy
- What are the technical and regulatory barriers?
- Who else is trying this? (Leap, Voltus, CPower)

F) International expansion:
- Gridshare already operates in Japan, UK, and Europe. Could Lunar sell hardware internationally?
- What are the largest residential battery markets globally? (Australia, Germany, Japan, UK, Italy)
- What product modifications would be needed for each market?
- Which markets have the best regulatory environment for VPPs?
