You are a senior corporate governance analyst specializing in venture-backed cleantech companies with complex strategic investor relationships. You are analyzing Lunar Energy, Inc., a private residential battery manufacturer that has raised $532M from a mix of strategic and financial investors. Your task is to map every stakeholder relationship, identify tensions and conflicts, assess governance quality, and evaluate likely endgame scenarios.

SECTION 1: THE SUNRUN RELATIONSHIP — ANATOMY OF A STRATEGIC DEPENDENCY

Sunrun (NASDAQ: RUN), the nation's largest residential solar installer, is simultaneously:
- Co-investor in Lunar's initial $300M rounds (with SK Group)
- The company whose co-founder (Lynn Jurich) chairs Lunar's board of directors
- Lunar's primary distribution channel for hardware sales
- A licensee of Lunar's Gridshare VPP software (for its fleet in Hawaii, New England, Puerto Rico, California)
- A potential acquirer

Research and analyze:

A) Distribution dependency:
- What percentage of Lunar's ~2,000 installations have gone through Sunrun vs. independent installers?
- Does Sunrun have exclusivity on Lunar products in any market? Volume commitments? Minimum purchase obligations?
- What are the contractual terms of the Sunrun-Lunar distribution arrangement? (Wholesale pricing, marketing commitments, installation standards)
- Does Sunrun also install Tesla Powerwall, Enphase, FranklinWH, or other batteries? What determines which product Sunrun recommends to a given customer?
- If Sunrun reduced orders by 50%, what would happen to Lunar's revenue?

B) Software licensing:
- What are the terms of Sunrun's use of Gridshare for its VPP programs?
- Is Sunrun paying licensing fees, or is this bundled as part of the investment relationship?
- Could Sunrun build or acquire its own VPP platform? Is there evidence it's exploring this?
- What happens to Gridshare revenue if Sunrun in-sources VPP capabilities?

C) Board control dynamics:
- Lynn Jurich as board chair: How does she balance Sunrun's interests (wanting the best-priced product, wanting to control its VPP platform, potentially wanting to acquire Lunar) with Lunar's interests (independence, fair pricing, diversified distribution)?
- Has Jurich recused herself from any board decisions? Are there documented governance policies for managing this conflict?
- Who else sits on the board? What are the voting dynamics?

D) Acquisition scenario analysis:
- What is the probability that Sunrun acquires Lunar within 3 years? What price would be fair?
- Would Sunrun acquiring Lunar strengthen or weaken the combined entity?
- How would other Lunar investors (B Capital, Activate Capital, Prelude, DCVC) view a Sunrun acquisition vs. an IPO?
- Are there anti-dilution, drag-along, or change-of-control provisions that affect deal dynamics?

SECTION 2: THE SK GROUP RELATIONSHIP — MANUFACTURING PARTNER OR FUTURE COMPETITOR?

SK Group (South Korea) co-led the initial $300M investment and is Lunar's manufacturing partner for battery production in Georgia.

Research and analyze:
- What is SK Group's strategic interest in Lunar? Pure financial return, technology access, channel access to US residential market, or vertical integration?
- SK supplies battery cells to multiple companies (Ford via BlueOval SK, Hyundai, others). Is there evidence SK supplies cells to Lunar's competitors?
- Could SK forward-integrate into residential batteries, bypassing Lunar? Has SK shown interest in the residential storage market independently?
- What is SK's investment stake in Lunar? Board seat? Veto rights?
- How would SK react to a Sunrun acquisition of Lunar? Would it support, block, or be indifferent?
- What happens to Lunar's battery cell supply if the SK relationship deteriorates?

SECTION 3: VENTURE INVESTOR DYNAMICS

Map the investor landscape and return expectations:

A) Investor profiles and expectations:
- Activate Capital ($130M Series C lead): What is their typical hold period? Return expectations? Do they prefer IPO or M&A exits?
- B Capital ($102M Series D co-lead): With $9B AUM and BCG as strategic partner, what is B Capital's value-add beyond money? What exits have they achieved in cleantech?
- Prelude Ventures (Series D co-lead): Climate-focused VC — do they have a strategic thesis on residential storage that informs their expectations?
- DCVC, Piva Capital, Leitmotif, Q Capital Partners: Smaller checks — what are their influence and preferences?

B) Capitalization table analysis:
- At $532M raised, what is the estimated post-money valuation? (Use industry multiples and comparable rounds)
- What liquidation preferences exist? (1x, 2x? Participating preferred?)
- At what exit price do common shareholders (founders, employees) receive meaningful returns?
- What is the minimum viable exit to return capital to Series C/D investors?

C) Exit scenario modeling:
- IPO: What revenue, growth rate, and profitability metrics would be required? What comparable public companies set the valuation benchmark? (Enphase at $25B peak, but now $8B; Stem Inc. went from $1.3B SPAC to near-zero)
- Strategic acquisition: Sunrun ($3-4B market cap), Schneider Electric, Eaton, Shell (owns Sonnen), Siemens, or others?
- Acqui-hire or distressed sale: Under what conditions? What would trigger this?
- Secondary sale: EquityZen already facilitates some trading — what does secondary market pricing indicate about valuation?

SECTION 4: FOUNDER & MANAGEMENT ASSESSMENT

Evaluate CEO Kunal Girotra's leadership:
- Track record at Tesla Energy: What was his specific scope and accomplishments? What was Tesla Energy's performance during his tenure?
- Why did he leave Tesla in March 2020? Was this voluntary or was he pushed out?
- Has he made any public statements about long-term ambitions for Lunar (IPO, independence, acquisition)?
- Key-person risk: How dependent is the company on Girotra? Who is the #2?
- Management team stability: The headcount declined from ~250 (2022) to 226 (June 2025) — was there a layoff? Voluntary attrition? What happened?

Evaluate the ex-Tesla concentration:
- CEO, CTO, and CFO are all ex-Tesla Energy. What are the benefits and risks of this homogeneity?
- How well has the Moixa UK team (Chris Wright, Ed Hatfield) integrated? Any evidence of cultural friction?
- Are there any Glassdoor reviews, LinkedIn patterns, or public employee comments revealing culture issues?

SECTION 5: COMMERCIAL RELATIONSHIP LEVERAGE

Beyond Sunrun and SK, map all strategic relationships:
- ITOCHU Corporation: What is the ongoing relationship for Japan operations? Is GridShare Japan being spun off?
- Honda: What is the EV charging integration partnership status? Is Honda actively promoting Lunar/Gridshare?
- Eaton: What is the smart breaker integration arrangement? Revenue-sharing? OEM licensing?

- Greentech Renewables: Distribution partnership details?

For each relationship: Could it be leveraged for additional value beyond the current transaction? Could Lunar become the VPP platform of record for multiple solar installers beyond Sunrun?

SECTION 6: STATED STRATEGY VS. REVEALED STRATEGY

Compare what management says publicly with what their actions reveal:
- "We will never need to raise again" — Is this credible given burn rate and scale-up ambitions?
- "100,000 units by 2028" — What evidence supports this timeline? Is this aspirational or backed by specific contracts/commitments?
- "Expanding to Texas, Utah, Nevada, Illinois" — How much resource has actually been deployed to these markets?
- "Everything downstream of the solar panel" — Is vertical integration a genuine strategic moat or a capital-intensive liability?
- The GridShare Japan investment (November 2025) — Is this expansion or separation/divestiture of a non-core asset?

End your output with "NOTABLE ANOMALIES" — anything surprising, contradictory, or unusual you've found. Pay particular attention to:
- Conflicts of interest that aren't being publicly discussed
- Gaps between stated strategy and observable actions
- Unusual investor terms or governance structures
- Evidence that any stakeholder is positioning for an exit, acquisition, or strategic pivot
- Signs that the Sunrun relationship is tightening or loosening
- Any board members, advisors, or relationships not mentioned in public materials
