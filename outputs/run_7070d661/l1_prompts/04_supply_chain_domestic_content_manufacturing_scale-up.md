You are a senior supply chain and manufacturing analyst specializing in battery storage and clean energy equipment. You are conducting a deep-dive analysis of Lunar Energy's supply chain, domestic content compliance, and manufacturing scale-up feasibility. The company claims its products are "proudly domestic content" and qualify as non-FEOC for ITC and domestic content incentive purposes. Your job is to verify these claims, map supply chain vulnerabilities, and assess the feasibility of scaling from 10,000 to 100,000 units per year by end of 2028.

SECTION 1: BATTERY CELL SUPPLY CHAIN

Lunar Energy's core input is lithium battery cells, likely lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) chemistry, sourced through its partnership with SK Group.

A) SK Group's battery operations:
- Which specific SK entity supplies Lunar? (SK On, SK Battery America, SKC?)
- Where are the cells manufactured? (SK's Commerce, GA plant produces cells — but for which customers? Do they make LFP or only NMC chemistry?)
- SK Group is primarily known for NMC (nickel-manganese-cobalt) cells for EVs. Do they produce LFP cells? If not, where does Lunar actually source LFP cells?
- What is the contractual arrangement? (Long-term supply agreement, spot purchasing, tolling?)
- What volume of cells does Lunar need at current scale (10K units × 20 kWh = 200 MWh) and target scale (100K units × 20 kWh = 2 GWh)?
- Can SK supply 2 GWh/year of residential-format cells? What's the lead time?
- What is the per-kWh cell cost at current volume vs. what it would be at 2 GWh/year?

B) Alternative cell sources:
- Does Lunar have any secondary cell suppliers? (CATL, BYD, EVE Energy, CALB, Samsung SDI?)
- If SK is the sole supplier, what happens if SK has a quality issue, capacity allocation dispute, or strategic pivot?
- Are there US-based LFP cell manufacturers Lunar could source from? (Microvast, AESC, Freyr?)
- What is the typical dual-sourcing strategy in this industry?

C) FEOC compliance for cells:
- Under the IRA's FEOC rules, battery cells cannot be manufactured by or contain critical minerals extracted/processed by a "foreign entity of concern" (effectively China, Russia, North Korea, Iran)
- SK sources its raw materials (lithium, graphite, phosphate for LFP) from where? Any Chinese-origin critical minerals?
- Does the SK Georgia plant process raw materials, or just assemble cells from imported components?
- What is the specific FEOC compliance pathway for Lunar's battery cells? Has Treasury/IRS provided guidance applicable to their situation?

- Battery Management System (BMS) chips: Where manufactured?
- Enclosure/sheet metal: Domestic vs. imported?
- Cables, connectors, wiring: Source?
- Smart panel (Bridge): Components source?
- Maximizer (solar optimizer): Components source?
- Software: Developed in US and UK — qualifies as domestic

B) Domestic content calculation:
- Under IRS guidance, what percentage of total cost must be domestic to qualify?
- Based on the component mapping above, does Lunar genuinely meet the threshold?
- What specific components are most at risk of disqualification?
- How does Lunar's domestic content position compare to Tesla Powerwall, Enphase IQ Battery, FranklinWH?
