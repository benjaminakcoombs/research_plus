# DELIVERABLE 1: COMPANY SITUATION MAP

## What Lunar Energy IS

Lunar Energy is a venture-backed residential battery storage company that is simultaneously two businesses operating under one roof—and the market is pricing it as the wrong one.

**The Hardware Business** (~$35M revenue, ~2,000 units deployed): Lunar designs, assembles, and sells an integrated home energy system comprising a modular LFP battery (10–30 kWh in 5 kWh blocks), a 9.6 kW hybrid DC-coupled inverter, a "Bridge" smart electrical panel, and "Maximizer" panel-level solar optimizers. Assembly occurs across three sites: Suwanee, Georgia (inverter/Bridge/Maximizers), Mexico (battery module assembly—a fact quietly disclosed on the company's website but omitted from "proudly domestic content" marketing), and Washington State (role undisclosed). Mountain View, California houses HQ, engineering, and pilot-line facilities at 35,000 sq ft running at roughly 20% utilization. The company claims 10,000 units/year manufacturing capacity but has deployed only ~2,000 cumulative systems, all in California, through approximately 40 certified installer partners. Hardware is sold primarily through Sunrun's lease channel at an estimated $12,000–$16,000 wholesale ASP, carrying an approximately 10% premium to Tesla Powerwall 3 on a headline basis (though more favorable on a $/kWh basis given Lunar's larger capacity).

**The Software Business** (revenue undisclosed, likely $5–15M): Gridshare is a cloud-based VPP/DERMS platform acquired via the 2022 Moixa purchase. It manages approximately 650 MW and 150,000 devices across three continents—overwhelmingly non-Lunar hardware. The platform processes 40 billion data points monthly from Japan alone and carries 150 million hours of historical optimization data. Key customers include Sunrun (managing its entire non-Tesla VPP fleet across 12+ US markets), ITOCHU/TEPCO (35,000+ homes in Japan), Peninsula Clean Energy, Ava Community Energy, and Honda UK (EV smart charging). Gridshare's managed fleet is approximately **16x larger** than Lunar's own installed hardware base. This is, by any operational measure, a software company that also makes batteries—not the reverse.

**Financial Position**: $532M raised total. Estimated cash on hand mid-2026: $85–115M. Estimated net burn: $8–12M/month ($96–144M annualized). At base-case burn, runway extends to approximately January 2027—barely 11 months post-Series D close. The company is pre-profit, with estimated hardware gross margins of 15–22% at current volumes, and fully loaded manufacturing cost per unit (~$12,100 at 2,000 units) that likely exceeds or barely meets the wholesale ASP. The implied post-money valuation at Series D is approximately $900M–$1.2B against $35M in trailing revenue—a 26–34x multiple justified only by the combined hardware+software growth thesis.

**Physical Assets**: Three leased assembly facilities (combined estimated 120,000–200,000 sq ft), engineering lab/prototype infrastructure in Mountain View, manufacturing equipment worth an estimated $20–50M, and the Gridshare software platform with its associated IP portfolio (58 patents, including Moixa's 2008-vintage smart home patents cited by ~200 international companies).

## What Lunar Energy SAYS It's Doing

Management's narrative rests on four pillars:

1. **"Everything downstream of the solar panel"**: CEO Girotra positions Lunar as the only company that builds its own battery hardware AND operates a device-agnostic VPP software platform. The "Android of the grid" analogy is deployed frequently—open software managing diverse hardware, versus Tesla's and Enphase's walled gardens.

2. **Aggressive manufacturing scale-up**: 10,000 units/year today → 20,000 by end 2026 → 100,000 by end 2028. This is a 50x increase from current cumulative deployments in 36 months.

3. **"Never raise again"**: Girotra has stated the $232M Series C+D is the company's last equity raise. The math does not support this claim under most scenarios—100,000 units/year requires an estimated $230–450M in manufacturing CapEx and working capital beyond current resources. This statement is best understood as investor signaling, not financial commitment.

4. **"Proudly domestic content"**: Lunar markets its non-FEOC, domestic-content-compliant supply chain as a key differentiator, particularly for the TPO/lease model where Section 48E ITC eligibility is the primary economic mechanism. This claim has meaningful asterisks: battery modules are assembled in Mexico, SK On's Georgia facility does not yet produce LFP cells commercially (not until H2 2026), and upstream graphite anode materials remain China-dependent.

**Where narrative diverges from reality**: Management describes California as the launchpad and Texas/Utah/Nevada/Illinois as imminent expansion markets—but no signed distribution partnerships, installer certifications, or utility contracts in any of these states have been publicly disclosed. The 100,000-unit target has no precedent in US residential battery manufacturing; Tesla took 7+ years to reach that level with vertical cell integration. The "never raise again" claim is contradicted by runway math showing ~11 months of cash at base burn. The hidden Series C ($130M, raised in 2024, not announced until February 2026) signals either unfavorable terms or deliberate information management.

## What the ENVIRONMENT Is Doing to Lunar Energy

**Massive tailwind, narrow window**:

- **Section 25D ITC elimination** (effective January 1, 2026): The homeowner tax credit is gone. The only path to federal subsidy for residential battery customers is now through TPO/lease structures under Section 48E—which is precisely Lunar's Sunrun channel. This is a structural tailwind that concentrates market power in lease companies (Sunrun) and FEOC-compliant battery makers (Lunar). However, 48E for solar/wind expires after 2027 (with beginning-of-construction safe harbor extending ~12 months). **Battery storage 48E extends through 2033**—Lunar's single most important and underreported regulatory advantage.

- **Battery cost collapse**: Pack prices for stationary storage hit $70/kWh in 2025, down 45% YoY. This is a double-edged sword: it enables adoption but commoditizes hardware and empowers capital-intensive competitors like Base Power ($1.3B raised, $3B valuation) who can offer "free batteries" in deregulated markets.

- **Grid reliability deterioration**: Outage durations up 80% over the past decade. Sunrun customers used batteries during 650,000+ outages in 2025. Every major grid event (Texas winter storm, California PSPS events, hurricanes) drives sustained battery inquiry spikes.

- **VPP market acceleration**: 37.5 GW of flexible capacity in North America. DOE targeting 80–160 GW by 2030. But the largest near-term California VPP program (DSGS) was defunded by Governor Newsom—undermining the $464/year customer earnings figure that anchors Lunar's marketing.

- **Competitive encirclement**: Tesla Powerwall 3 holds 59% market share and is the default recommendation at most large installers. FranklinWH has scaled from zero to third nationally in ~3 years with 3,000+ certified installers (vs. Lunar's 40). Base Power raised $1B for a battery-as-a-service model in Texas. Enphase is struggling but still commands 25% share. Sonnen (Shell-backed) is expanding aggressively.

- **FEOC/domestic content rules tightening**: Current 40% domestic content threshold rises to 55% after 2026. Lunar's Mexico-assembled battery modules—the most value-intensive manufacturing step—put the 10% domestic content bonus at risk under the higher threshold. SK On's upstream graphite supply chain remains China-dependent. Transition relief expires 2027.

## What's CHANGED Recently (Last 12–18 Months)

1. **Series C+D raise ($232M, February 2026)**: Oversubscribed Series D signals investor appetite. But the hidden Series C (unannounced for 2+ years) raises questions about terms and valuation trajectory.

2. **Section 25D elimination (One Big Beautiful Bill Act)**: Eliminated homeowner clean energy credits. Forces market to TPO/lease. Structurally advantages Lunar's Sunrun channel.

3. **DSGS defunding**: California's largest VPP compensation program vetoed by Governor Newsom. Lunar's widely-cited $464/year VPP earnings figure derived primarily from this program. Ongoing marketing of this figure without disclosure of program termination is a material misrepresentation risk.

4. **SK On enters US stationary storage market**: $1.5B deal with Flatiron Energy Development for 7.2 GWh of utility-scale LFP. SK is building the capability to bypass Lunar and sell directly into US storage markets.

5. **GridShare Japan restructuring (December 2025)**: Lunar took a minority stake in ITOCHU's GSJ subsidiary alongside Japanese utilities. Marketed as "deepening commitment to Japan"; structurally, this is a managed partial exit that hands operational control to ITOCHU while Lunar retains licensing revenue and equity upside. Signals a US-first strategic pivot.

6. **Sunrun's 71% storage attachment rate**: Record high, but Sunrun is simultaneously promoting Tesla Powerwall 3 as its preferred hardware and reducing its affiliate installer channel by 40% in 2026—both of which directly threaten Lunar's distribution.

7. **Base Power's $1B Series C (October 2025)**: The most capitalized residential battery competitor, operating in Texas (Lunar's announced expansion market), with a fundamentally different own-and-operate model.

8. **Honda's ChargeScape JV**: Honda—a Lunar shareholder and historical Moixa partner—is building its primary V2G infrastructure through a joint venture with BMW, Ford, and Nissan, not through Gridshare. This suggests Gridshare may be excluded from Honda's most commercially significant EV-to-grid opportunity.

9. **Japan VPP market opening (2026)**: For the first time, residential DERs in Japan will be eligible for transmission-level capacity and balancing markets. Gridshare already manages 35,000+ homes via ITOCHU—a potential step-change in software revenue.

10. **Sunrun financial stress**: Altman Z-Score of -0.47 (distress zone), $14.6B total debt, stock down significantly. If Sunrun represents 50%+ of Lunar's hardware revenue and the dominant Gridshare licensing relationship, Sunrun's financial health is Lunar's financial health.
