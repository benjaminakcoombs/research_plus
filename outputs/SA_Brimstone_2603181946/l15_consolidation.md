# DELIVERABLE 1: COMPANY SITUATION MAP

## What Brimstone IS

Brimstone Energy, Inc. is a pre-revenue, venture-backed industrial technology company that has developed a patented hydrometallurgical process — branded the "Rock Refinery" — to co-produce ASTM C150 portland cement, supplementary cementitious materials (SCM), and smelter-grade alumina (SGA) from calcium silicate rocks rather than limestone. The company was founded in 2019 at Caltech by Cody Finke (CEO) and Hugo Leandri (CSO). It is incorporated in Delaware with headquarters in Oakland, California, a growing operational presence in Reno, Nevada (demonstration-scale plant in engineering design as of December 2025), and a planned first commercial facility near Lawton/Duncan, Oklahoma.

**Physical assets today are minimal.** Brimstone operates bench/laboratory-scale equipment in Oakland and is engineering — but has not yet broken ground on — a demonstration-scale plant near Reno. There is no commercial plant, no revenue-generating asset, and no inventory of saleable product at scale. The company has ~82 employees (PitchBook, March 2026). Its core intellectual property consists of patent families filed jointly with Caltech (core PCT application WO2021030529A1, priority August 2020) with international coverage across nine jurisdictions, plus undisclosed trade-secret process integration know-how and a proprietary feedstock selection database derived from screening 23,000+ U.S. quarries.

**Plant 1 (Oklahoma) specifications:** ~$378–400M investment for a 20-acre facility designed to produce 80,000 t/yr of portland cement and 20,000 t/yr of smelter-grade alumina, with the Dolese Bros. Co. Roosevelt quarry in Kiowa County providing calcium silicate feedstock via a signed letter of intent. The plant targets full production before 2030. In December 2025, Brimstone expanded its product roadmap to include steel, magnesium, titanium, and other critical minerals from the same feedstock — the "Fourth Great Refinery" vision — though none of these additional co-products have been demonstrated at any scale.

**Revenue composition (projected, not actual):** At Plant 1 nameplate, estimated annual revenue of $21–43M split approximately 35–55% from alumina ($8.6–19.2M at $430–960/t including domestic premium), 40–55% from cement ($9.6–16.0M at $120–200/t), and a residual from SCM ($3–8M). No revenue has been recognized. The only executed commercial agreement is a non-binding reservation from Amazon (August 2025) for unspecified annual volumes of cement and SCM, conditional on testing completion and commercial-scale production.

**The critical tension in the business model:** Brimstone tells a cement story to climate investors and a critical minerals story to defense and political stakeholders. The cement product is validated (ASTM C150 certified by Twining Consulting, third-party tested by Amazon). The alumina product — which carries the strategic value, the national security narrative, and the EGA/Century demand thesis — has zero publicly disclosed purity data, zero confirmed smelter trials, and zero independent validation after six years of development. This gap between the certified cement and the unverified alumina is the single most important fact about this company.

## How It MAKES MONEY (and How That's Changing)

**It doesn't make money yet.** Brimstone is pre-revenue. The revenue model, when operational, would consist entirely of commodity product sales: portland cement, SCM, and alumina sold at prevailing market prices plus any green/domestic premium. There is no SaaS component, no recurring subscription, no technology licensing fee in the current model. Revenue quality at maturity would be moderate — commodity products with known demand but price volatility (alumina ranged $330–810/t in 2024–2025).

**The revenue quality narrative is changing fast.** Three developments are reframing what this company is:

*First*, the Amazon reservation agreement (August 2025) creates the prospect of a take-or-pay offtake with an investment-grade counterparty — the gold standard for project finance bankability. But it is currently non-binding and conditional. Converting it to binding is the most commercially important near-term milestone.

*Second*, the EGA/Century Oklahoma smelter (announced January 2026, $4B, 750,000 t/yr aluminum requiring ~1.5M t/yr alumina) creates a structural domestic alumina demand anchor that did not exist 12 months ago. Brimstone's 20,000 t/yr is only 1.3% of that requirement, but as the only announced domestic non-bauxite alumina source in the United States, the strategic value per tonne is dramatically higher than the commodity price implies.

*Third*, the "Fourth Great Refinery" product expansion (December 2025) — adding steel, magnesium, titanium to the roadmap — transforms the TAM from $400B (cement) to $2.4T (all building materials) in management's framing. This is pre-roadshow narrative anchoring. None of the new products have been demonstrated at any scale.

**Margin trajectory:** Unknown at commercial scale. The co-production model is the economic thesis — cement revenue subsidizes rock processing, making the marginal extraction cost of alumina dramatically below standalone Bayer process economics ($125–225/t estimated co-production cost vs. $560–585/t landed imported alumina). If the co-production economics work as modeled, gross margins should be structurally advantaged vs. any standalone product producer. But this has never been demonstrated outside of bench/pilot scale.

**Competitive dynamics have shifted decisively in Brimstone's favor.** Sublime Systems — the primary next-generation cement competitor and the only other ASTM-certified green OPC alternative — has effectively imploded. Workforce reduced from 128 to ~28 employees between December 2025 and March 2026. Holyoke, MA plant paused indefinitely. CRH and Holcim/Amrize both hold strategic investments in Sublime that now face impairment — creating a forced decision point where at least one cement major must pivot to Brimstone or accept that its green cement technology bet has failed.

**Regulatory/reimbursement landscape is simultaneously hostile and favorable.** Hostile: DOE cancelled $189M OCED grant (May 2025) as part of a systematic $20B+ rescission of Biden-era clean energy awards. No commercial OCED grantee has won an appeal. The grant is gone for planning purposes. Favorable: Trump's March 2025 executive order on critical minerals production, the December 2025 White House aluminum strategy brief, and Century Aluminum's $500M OCED grant (actively maintained) create a policy incoherence that is Brimstone's strongest political argument. The DOD has deployed $777M in critical minerals investments through the DIBC in FY2025 alone, with award sizes of $7M–$400M. A Section 232 tariff inclusion petition for alumina could be filed through BIS's established Inclusions Process — no Congressional action required — imposing a 50% tariff on imported alumina and transforming Brimstone's economics.

**Sector M&A activity is concentrated in three relevant areas:** (1) CRH's $2.1B acquisition of Eco Material Technologies (SCM, September 2025) establishes a valuation benchmark for cement technology businesses; (2) Holcim's spinoff of Amrize (June 2025, ~$30B valuation) creates a new NYSE-listed acquirer with an explicit U.S. acquisition mandate and a "US dollar-denominated currency and stock advantageous for future acquisitions"; (3) EGA is actively seeking equity partners for its Oklahoma smelter (Bloomberg, December 2025).

**Macro forces:** U.S. data center construction CapEx is accelerating (Amazon alone guided $200B total CapEx for 2026; data centers projected to consume ~1M MT/yr of cement). 50% tariffs on bauxite imports make Brimstone's non-bauxite alumina process uniquely tariff-immune. Oklahoma's industrial cluster is reaching critical mass with EGA/Century (aluminum), USA Rare Earth (neodymium magnets), Stardust Power (lithium), and Westwin Elements (nickel) all building in-state.

# DELIVERABLE 2: STRATEGIC TENSION POINTS

## TENSION POINT 1 ★
**THE OBSERVATION:** Brimstone claims to produce "smelter-grade alumina" in every press release since January 2025, yet after six years of development, $80M+ raised, and pilot-scale operations, the company has published zero of the thirteen standard SGA specification parameters — no Al₂O₃ percentage, no Fe₂O₃ assay, no TiO₂ level, no crystal phase XRD data, no BET surface area, no attrition index. By contrast, its cement product has been independently certified to ASTM C150 by Twining Consulting and tested by Amazon's construction team. Customer engagement language consistently names "cement and SCM" — never alumina — when describing testing programs. [Sources: All Brimstone press releases 2024–2026; patent US20210070656A1; Canary Media January 2025; Fast Company September 2025; metal.com trade press; Amazon commercial agreement disclosure]

**WHY IT MATTERS:** The entire EGA/Century Oklahoma alumina supply thesis — arguably the most valuable strategic narrative available to this company — rests on the assumption that Brimstone produces on-spec SGA. If the alumina meets only chemical-grade specification (98–99% Al₂O₃ without the physical properties required for Hall-Héroult cells), the product is worth $150–250/t, not $400–500/t, and the EGA supply thesis collapses. The revenue mix shifts from 35–55% alumina to potentially <15%, fundamentally changing the valuation from "critical minerals platform" to "green cement startup." A buyer paying a critical-minerals multiple (higher) vs. a cement-tech multiple (lower) needs this resolved before term sheet.

**THE QUESTION IT RAISES:** What would it take to confirm SGA spec? A diligence requirement should be: (a) independent lab COA from the Reno pilot on Al₂O₃ purity, Fe₂O₃, TiO₂, SiO₂; (b) XRD confirming γ/θ-Al₂O₃ >85%; (c) BET surface area 50–120 m²/g; and (d) a 1–5 tonne batch trial at any operating smelter. Cost: ~$150K total. Until this exists, "smelter-grade alumina" must be treated as an unverified marketing claim.

**CATEGORY:** DILIGENCE FLAG

**POTENTIAL MAGNITUDE:** Transformational — the difference between a critical minerals valuation and a cement-tech valuation is 2–3x.

**CONFIDENCE:** High that the data gap exists; Medium that it reflects a genuine technical problem (Hypothesis C from the purity analysis — chemical purity likely achieved at bench scale, physical properties unresolved).

**L2 RESEARCH QUESTION:** Obtain and analyze the mineral composition (via XRF) of run-of-mine rock from Dolese's Roosevelt quarry in Kiowa County, Oklahoma. The feedstock's Fe₂O₃ and TiO₂ content sets the theoretical ceiling on achievable alumina purity — if feedrock iron is >4% and titanium is >1%, the decontamination factor required to reach SGA spec exceeds 200x, which has not been demonstrated commercially from any acid-leach process.

**PRE-MORTEM:** If this insight turned out to be wrong, the most likely reason would be that Brimstone has already achieved full SGA specification at pilot scale and is withholding publication as trade secret pending commercial plant construction — a deliberate information management strategy rather than a technical gap.

## TENSION POINT 7

**THE OBSERVATION:** The Southern Oklahoma Aulacogen — the geological formation underlying Brimstone's Dolese Roosevelt quarry — is documented by the Oklahoma Geological Survey as containing "titanium-rich and iron-rich gabbros." The Glen Mountains Layered Complex in this area contains anorthosite with An70 plagioclase (exceptionally alumina-rich and calcium-rich — ideal for Brimstone's process), but with associated ilmenite (FeTiO₃) and magnetite (Fe₃O₄) as accessory minerals. SGA specification requires TiO₂ <0.003% (30 ppm) and Fe₂O₃ <0.02% (200 ppm). Getting from ~2–6% Fe₂O₃ in the feedrock to <200 ppm in the product requires a 100–300x decontamination factor. For TiO₂: 100–667x. The Brimstone patent describes no titanium removal step. [Sources: Oklahoma Geological Survey "Geology of the Southern Oklahoma Aulacogen"; GMLC mineral composition studies; US Patent US20210070656A1]

**WHY IT MATTERS:** This is the most specific technical risk identified in the research. ## a) ARCHETYPE CHALLENGE

**Asset 2: Domestic Alumina Supply Franchise** — the only announced domestic non-bauxite alumina source in the United States, located in the same state as the first new aluminum smelter since 1980.
