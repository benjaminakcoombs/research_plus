### 1.2 Plain-Language Business Description

Brimstone has invented a patented process — the Rock Refinery® — that produces ordinary Portland cement and smelter-grade alumina simultaneously from silicate rocks (e.g., basalt, diopside) rather than limestone. Conventional Portland cement manufacturing releases CO₂ from two sources: combustion energy (addressable with electrification) and the decomposition of limestone feedstock (chemically unavoidable — roughly 60% of all cement emissions). Brimstone eliminates the second source entirely by substituting calcium silicate rock, which is carbon-free. The resulting OPC is identical in chemistry, physical properties, and performance to conventional Portland cement — it meets ASTM C150 standards, requires no new building codes, and needs no customer re-education.


By making and selling more than one product from the same rock, all of the products — including the low-carbon cement — can be cost-competitive when produced at an industrial scale. The process is inherently more efficient. Right now, other companies make cement, alumina, and SCM separately, and start each process by mining and grinding rock.


The economics are built on co-production: from one mining and grinding operation, Brimstone extracts calcium for OPC, extracts aluminum compounds for smelter-grade alumina, and generates SCM (supplementary cementitious material) as a co-product. Residual magnesium compounds passively absorb atmospheric CO₂, producing carbon negativity and carbon credit value at no marginal cost. The process is fully electrifiable and uses commercially available equipment, limiting scale-up risk to process integration.

**Today: Pre-revenue. Pilot operations at Oakland lab and Reno demo plant. Target commercial plant in Oklahoma (near Dolese Bros. Roosevelt quarry) before 2030. **Process mechanics (confirmed at pilot scale):**
1. Mine silicate rocks (basalt, diopside) — calcium and aluminum-bearing
2. Crush into particles (single quarry/mining operation)

4. Heat calcium compounds → mill with gypsum → produce ASTM C150 OPC
5. Residue → SCM co-product
6. Aluminum compounds → smelter-grade alumina
7. Residual magnesium compounds → passive CO₂ absorption (carbon credit generation)

## SECTION 5: M&A OBSERVATIONS — FINAL INTEGRATED ASSESSMENT

### The 10 Most Strategically Significant Facts About This Company From a Deal Perspective

---

**1. ⚡ SUBLIME'S PAUSE IS BRIMSTONE'S MOMENT — THE STRATEGIC WINDOW IS NOW OPEN**


Sublime Systems has paused work on its low-carbon cement plant in Holyoke and plans to cut its workforce by 10% due to the Trump administration's cancellation of an $87 million grant. Sublime announced it will pause work on the project and cut its workforce after losing an $87 million federal grant.
 CRH and Holcim combined have $75M in equity and binding offtake invested in a company that just paused construction and cut 10% of its workforce. Their strategic green cement supply pipeline now has a gap. Brimstone is the only alternative that: (a) produces ASTM C150 OPC (not the ASTM C1157 material Sublime makes); (b) has a quarry site LOI; (c) has an anchor customer; (d) has a credible CFO; and (e) is not paused. **The window to invest in or acquire Brimstone before CRH and Holcim pivot to it is measured in months, not years.** For a competing buyer (Heidelberg, EGA, or a sovereign wealth fund), this is the sharpest timing signal in the analysis.

---

**2. 🚨 THE LPO PATH IS DEAD — THE $400M MUST COME FROM PRIVATE MARKETS**

The legislative closure of the IRA LPO loan authority by the OBBBA (July 4, 2025) eliminates what would have been the cleanest alternative financing route for the Oklahoma plant. 
The budget reconciliation bill signed on July 4, 2025 applied sweeping changes to the DOE's Loan Programs Office, overhauling its authorities and reshaping its role in energy infrastructure financing. Notably, the law repeals the Inflation Reduction Act's loan authority for LPO programs.
 Combined with the cancelled OCED grant, Brimstone is now entirely dependent on private capital to fund a $400M plant. This is not a death sentence — Tim Rens raised $750M for Monolith's first plant through private project finance — but it eliminates a significant risk-sharing tool and increases the pressure for either a strategic equity partner or a full acquisition. Every month without a capital announcement is a month closer to a distressed situation.

---

**3. 🏆 AMAZON TESTING COMPLETION IN 2025-2026 IS THE NEAR-TERM GATE**


Due to the success of the results, the companies have set out plans for more extensive testing over the next two years.
 When that testing successfully concludes — likely H1 2026 to H1 2027 — Amazon's reservation agreement upgrades to a fully binding commercial contract. That contract is the single most valuable piece of paper Brimstone doesn't yet have. A fully binding Amazon offtake agreement: (a) establishes revenue visibility for any project finance lender; (b) creates a "first customer validated" milestone that unlocks Series B pricing power; (c) triggers any co-development provisions in the Amazon investor agreement. **A banker advising Brimstone should be ready the day that testing concludes — that is the moment to launch a formal process.**

---

**4. 💎 THE OKLAHOMA ALUMINA PLAY IS THE MOST UNDERAPPRECIATED STRATEGIC THESIS**

No analyst or journalist has yet connected the Brimstone Oklahoma plant directly to EGA/Century Aluminum's Inola smelter as a supply chain pair. 
Brimstone's Rock Refinery would represent the first new, domestic source of alumina in a generation — a critical step toward onshoring U.S. manufacturing, a Trump Administration priority. EGA, an international aluminum company, plans to invest $4 billion to establish a primary aluminum smelter in Oklahoma, the first such facility in the U.S. in decades.
 Both target pre-2030 operations in Oklahoma. Both need each other. A direct alumina supply agreement or EGA/Century equity co-investment in Brimstone would transform the financing picture, add a creditworthy industrial sponsor to the plant capital stack, and create a second strategic buyer universe (aluminum/critical minerals) entirely distinct from the cement sector. **This is the "how did they know that?" insight for the client call.**

---

**5. 🔑 ASTM C150 vs. C1157 — THE CERTIFICATION DIFFERENCE THAT CHANGES THE DEAL PRICE**

Sublime produces an ASTM C1157-compliant material. Brimstone produces ASTM C150. 
Brimstone has developed a more efficient, economical, and sustainable process for producing industry-standard materials that can be used today with standardized, known testing programs, which allows for fast market adoption. Because Brimstone makes ASTM C150 Ordinary Portland Cement, the most widely used cement in the world, at competitive prices, its path to commercialization is well-defined with existing regulation, know-how, and material safety protocols.
 ASTM C1157 materials require new specification approvals from architects, engineers, and insurers on each project. ASTM C150 does not. For a cement major acquirer, this means Brimstone's product drops into existing supply chains, customer contracts, and specification libraries without modification. The commercial scalability of the technology is structurally higher than Sublime's — and this should command a premium in any transaction multiple.

---

**6. ⚠️ THE GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE COULD BLOCK OR ENABLE EVERYTHING**

A two-person founder board controlling a company that needs $400M in plant financing is an unusually concentrated governance structure for a Series A-stage company. This cuts both ways: if the founders are aligned with a transaction, the process is fast and clean — there is no activist board member, no conflicted investor, no reluctant minority. If the founders are not aligned, no transaction is possible regardless of strategic logic. The Amazon investor/customer relationship adds complexity — Amazon's agreement terms, change-of-control provisions, and information rights are unknown. Any banker must resolve the governance question — with the founders directly, in a private conversation — before investing significant process time.

---

**7. 📋 THE SERIES B IS STRUCTURALLY OVERDUE — AND THE CLOCK IS RUNNING**


It is probable that Brimstone will raise another funding round. If they raise again, there is a 76% chance it would happen in the next 6 months.
 That forecast was made from a database assessment date that appears to be late 2025 — meaning the 6-month window likely expires in Q2 2026. No raise has been announced as of March 2026. The hire of Tim Rens (October 2025) strongly implies a raise is being prepared but not yet launched. Most likely structure: **a Series B of $100-200M led by a strategic cement major or critical minerals investor, structured as equity + binding offtake + co-development rights for future plants** — the Sublime/CRH+Holcim transaction anatomy applied to Brimstone. A banker who is in front of Brimstone before that process launches is positioned to advise on structure and buyer selection.

---

**8. 🏗️ THE PLANT FID IS THE ULTIMATE FORCING FUNCTION — AND IT HAS NOT BEEN MADE**

No Final Investment Decision for the Oklahoma plant has been announced. No EPC contractor has been named. No project finance debt has been arranged. No anchor equity sponsor has been confirmed beyond Dolese Bros. (which is a rock supplier, not a capital partner). The DOE OCED grant is cancelled. The LPO is closed. Amazon's reservation is not yet binding. At $400M, this plant cannot be financed without either: (a) a strategic equity co-investor, (b) a full acquisition, or (c) a government-backed instrument that does not currently exist. **The FID is the gun pointed at every stakeholder — it will force resolution of the capital structure question, and it is approaching.**

---

**9. 🌐 "FOURTH GREAT REFINERY" — BRILLIANT REPOSITIONING, BUT REQUIRES VALIDATION**

The December 2025 narrative expansion to include steel, titanium, and magnesium co-production is analytically important because it widens the addressable investor and buyer universe beyond cement majors. 
Co-produced products include ASTM C150 portland cement, SCM, smelter grade alumina, steel, aluminum, magnesium, titanium, and more.
 But this is a roadmap claim, not a demonstrated process. A sophisticated buyer will commission an independent engineering assessment (e.g., Wood Group, KBR) to validate the expanded product slate before paying a platform multiple. The risk is that the expanded narrative narrows the buyer pool to visionaries — while the near-term financing need requires pragmatists who will accept OPC + alumina + SCM as the actual product set and value accordingly. **The Series B pitch must be disciplined about distinguishing demonstrated chemistry from future roadmap.**

---

**10. 🎯 THE MOST LIKELY TRANSACTION SCENARIO — AND WHY TIMING MATTERS MORE THAN PRICE**

Based on the totality of the analysis: the most likely near-term transaction (12-18 months) is a **Series B of $100-200M led by CRH or Holcim/Amrize, structured as equity + binding offtake + co-development rights**, analogous to the Sublime transaction but for a company that: (a) produces ASTM C150 (more commercially scalable than C1157); (b) co-produces alumina (unique economic upside vs. pure-play cement); and (c) is not paused (unlike Sublime). The full acquisition scenario becomes probable after Plant 1 is operational (2028-2030), when the cost curve has been demonstrated. An IPO is possible but unlikely before 2030 given the capital markets environment for industrial deep-tech.

**The irreducible timing insight:** Both CRH and Holcim need to redeploy their green cement strategic mandate after Sublime's pause. 
Holcim and CRH, two of the largest global building solutions companies, each separately commit to working with Sublime to deploy the technology in full-scale cement plants; accelerated co-development activities have already commenced.
 That commitment is now in limbo. The company that moves first on Brimstone — with equity + offtake, before the Series B is formally launched — sets the strategic terms. **The time to act is Q2-Q3 2026.**
