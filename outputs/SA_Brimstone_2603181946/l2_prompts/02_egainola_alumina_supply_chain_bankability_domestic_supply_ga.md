You are a project finance analyst specializing in primary aluminum smelter development, with expertise in alumina supply chain bankability assessments and raw material sourcing strategies. You are investigating whether Brimstone Energy has a realistic pathway to become a domestic alumina supplier for the largest new aluminum smelter project in North America — and what the timing and structural requirements are for that relationship.

CONTEXT:
Emirates Global Aluminium (EGA) and Century Aluminum announced a Joint Development Agreement (JDA) in January 2026 for a 750,000 t/yr aluminum smelter in Inola, Oklahoma. This smelter will require approximately 1.5 million tonnes/year of alumina feedstock — roughly 1.9–2.0 tonnes of alumina per tonne of aluminum. The United States has only one operating alumina refinery: Almatis/Gramercy (formerly Atalco) in Louisiana, operating at roughly one-third capacity (~600,000 t/yr max, actual ~200,000 t/yr). The U.S. currently imports ~1.1M t/yr of alumina.

EGA is organizing a reported $5 billion multi-tranche debt financing for the Inola project. In standard smelter project finance, lenders require bankable alumina supply agreements as a condition precedent to financial close — alumina is the single largest operating cost input. EGA's existing 8-year alumina supply agreement with Alcoa (up to 15.6 million MT) explicitly covers UAE smelter operations, not Inola. Oklahoma's Energy Secretary has stated alumina for Inola will arrive "by barge" — no domestic source has been named.

Separately, Public Service Company of Oklahoma (PSO, an AEP subsidiary) acquired the 795 MW Green Country gas-fired power plant partly to serve Inola's power needs, and PSO's newly approved 995.5 MW Fuel-Free Power Plan adds nearly 1 GW of renewable generation. Oklahoma's agreement with EGA includes "more than $275 million in incentives, as well as discounted power rates." These commitments suggest ~$1B+ in utility infrastructure is being deployed in anticipation of the smelter.

Brimstone Energy, located in Kiowa County, Oklahoma (~200 miles from Inola), plans to produce 20,000 t/yr of alumina at its first commercial plant. Its June 2025 press release used the phrase "mine to metal" in the same paragraph as an EGA reference, issued six weeks after EGA's original announcement.

HYPOTHESIS TO INVESTIGATE:
EGA's $5B debt financing will require a verified alumina supply agreement as a bankability condition. The window for Brimstone to enter the Inola ecosystem as a domestic supply partner — before EGA locks in a 10-year imported alumina contract — may be 6–12 months. A Brimstone equity stake by EGA/Mubadala ($50–100M) combined with an alumina offtake option is financially immaterial to EGA (~1.5% of the Inola financing) but existentially important to Brimstone. PSO's $1B+ infrastructure commitment independently confirms the smelter will be built, anchoring the 1.5M t/yr alumina demand on a 10+ year horizon.

SPECIFIC RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. What is the standard bankability requirement for alumina supply in aluminum smelter project finance? Research precedent smelters (Ma'aden/Alcoa Ras Al Khair, Alouette, Hillside) — what percentage of alumina needs must be contracted before lenders commit? What contract tenor is typical?

2. Has EGA or Century disclosed any alumina supply arrangement for Inola in public filings, trade press, or regulatory submissions? Check Century Aluminum's 10-K/10-Q filings (SEC EDGAR), EGA press releases, Oklahoma Commerce Department records, and Oklahoma Corporation Commission filings related to the Inola project.

3. Obtain PSO's filed testimony and exhibits from the Green Country power plant acquisition and Fuel-Free Power Plan proceedings before the Oklahoma Corporation Commission (OCC). These are public regulatory documents that should contain load forecasts, industrial customer projections, and potentially power rate indications that quantify PSO's assumptions about the Inola smelter timeline and capacity ramp.

4. What alumina transportation infrastructure exists between potential supply sources and Inola? Map the barge route from Gramercy, LA to the Verdigris River/Port of Catoosa near Inola. What is the cost per tonne of barged alumina vs. truck transport from Kiowa County (~200 miles)?

5. At 20,000 t/yr, Brimstone would supply only ~1.3% of Inola's alumina needs. Is this commercially meaningful to EGA, or is it too small to justify the procurement complexity? What is the minimum commercial offtake volume that smelters typically contract? How does this compare to strategic "anchor" supply arrangements vs. spot purchases?

6. Is there any evidence that EGA/Mubadala has invested in or partnered with other non-traditional alumina producers globally? Check EGA's venture portfolio and Mubadala's technology fund (Mubadala Capital) for any alumina or critical minerals investments.
