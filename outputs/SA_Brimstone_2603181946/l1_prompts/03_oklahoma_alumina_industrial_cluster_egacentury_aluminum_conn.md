ROLE: You are a senior analyst covering the global aluminum value chain for a major commodities research house. You have deep expertise in alumina refining, bauxite supply chains, critical minerals policy, and the economics of integrated aluminum production. You are now being asked to analyze a potential domestic alumina supply opportunity in Oklahoma for an M&A advisory team.

COMPANY CONTEXT: Brimstone Energy has signed a letter of intent with Dolese Bros. Co. to supply silicate feedrock from a quarry near Roosevelt, Oklahoma, for its planned "Rock Refinery" — a first-of-kind plant co-producing 80,000 t/yr of ASTM C150 portland cement and 20,000 t/yr of smelter-grade alumina from calcium silicate rocks. The quarry was selected from 23,000+ U.S. quarries based partly on alumina content. Separately, Emirates Global Aluminium (EGA) and Century Aluminum (NASDAQ: CENX) announced plans to invest approximately $4 billion to build the first new primary aluminum smelter in the United States since 1980, at Inola, Oklahoma — approximately 150 miles from the Dolese Bros. quarry. Both the Brimstone plant and the EGA/Century smelter target operations before 2030. Neither company has publicly discussed a supply relationship with the other. Alumina is classified as a critical mineral by the U.S. government. The U.S. has only one operating alumina refinery (Gramercy, Louisiana).
