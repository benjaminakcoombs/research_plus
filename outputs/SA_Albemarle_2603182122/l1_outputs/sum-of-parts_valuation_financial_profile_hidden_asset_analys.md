### ANOMALY #1 (ROUND 3 — HIGHEST PRIORITY): THE SQM/CODELCO PRECEDENT MEANS THE CHILEAN ASSETS ARE WORTH MATERIALLY LESS IN AN M&A TRANSACTION THAN IN A DCF

This is the most consequential finding across all three rounds of research. 
Starting in 2031, the state will receive 85% of the operating margin of the new production through payments to Corfo, taxes, and the profits received by Codelco as a shareholder.
 And even in the transition period: 
the Chilean state, through Codelco, Corfo and the Treasury, will receive approximately 70% of the operational margin generated by the new production between 2025 and 2030.


The market values Albemarle's Chilean operations as if 100% of the economic margin accrues to Albemarle shareholders. But the SQM/Codelco deal proves that Chile views its Atacama rights as generating 70–85% of operating margin for the state — leaving only 15–30% for the private partner. If Chile applies this logic to Albemarle in a CoC scenario:

- At 30% private retention of $450M mid-cycle Chilean EBITDA → $135M EBITDA to acquirer
- At 8x EBITDA multiple → **$1.08B** for Chilean operations (vs. my undiscounted $3.52B estimate)
- **This is a $2.4B valuation gap** hidden inside the single largest M&A risk factor

**No currently published sell-side research or M&A analysis has modeled this worst-case scenario.** Every analyst uses a simple CoC discount of 10–20%. The correct CoC discount in the worst case is 60–70%.

### ANOMALY #5 (ROUND 1/2, CONFIRMED): THE CORFO COMMISSION CAPS UPSIDE IN CHILE AT HIGH PRICES

Previously flagged — still the most under-modeled feature of Chilean operations. The commission structure means that at $25,000/MT vs. $20,000/MT:
- Revenue increase: +25%
- CORFO commission increase: +25% (it's a percentage of sales price — scales perfectly with price)
- **Net margin increase to Albemarle: ~0% of the revenue increment above the commission threshold**

Chile's brine operations are effectively a **fixed-fee-plus-residual structure** at high prices — not a leveraged commodity play. Any acquirer or analyst projecting Chilean EBITDA as price × volume without the commission structure will systematically overvalue this asset by 25–40%.

### ANOMALY #6 (ROUND 1/2 MAINTAINED): THE CHINA/SEABORNE PRICE BIFURCATION IS WIDENING, NOT CONVERGING


Growing geopolitical and trade tensions are creating an environment in which the Western price of lithium could trade at a significant premium to the Chinese price, which would be good news for Albemarle.
 But paradoxically: Albemarle's FEOC-classified Chinese plants are the only ones capturing the current $20,800/MT domestic China price. The company is simultaneously: (a) benefiting today from high Chinese domestic prices at Meishan/Qinzhou, and (b) facing a future where FEOC restrictions make those same plants liabilities for U.S. customers.

The IRA FEOC expansion in the "One Big Beautiful Bill" makes this worse. Any volume from Chinese plants that reaches U.S. customers causes those customers to lose IRA Section 45X credits — making Chinese-sourced Albemarle product effectively worth less per unit to the most valuable customer segment. The $656M mid-case Chinese operations value may still be generous.

The potential restart of Kemerton is contingent on prices holding above the $25,000/tonne mark.
 This is higher than most investors assume and is a confirmed management statement. At current $20,800/MT, Kemerton remains idled. The only pathway to Kemerton restart is: (a) further ~20% price appreciation from current levels, and (b) Australian operating costs must also fall relative to Chinese alternatives. As Albemarle's CEO noted: 
"recent lithium price improvements alone are not enough to offset the challenges facing Western hard-rock lithium conversion operations."


Kemerton's option value should be zero below $25,000/MT and $400M at $25,000/MT+ (sustained). The current market price of $20,800/MT means Kemerton contributes **zero option value in the current environment** — the $250M mid-case in our SOTP is already generous and assumes a partially probabilistic upside.
