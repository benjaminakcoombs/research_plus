## NOTABLE ANOMALIES — DEFINITIVE FINAL LIST

### Anomaly #1: The NPS/KIC Divergence — Opposite Moves at the Same Time

In Q2 2025, Korea Investment Corporation **sold 87.9%** of its ALB position. At the exact same time, the National Pension Service **bought 261.9% more** ALB shares. These are the two largest arms of Korean sovereign capital. They moved in **opposite directions** on the same stock in the same quarter. The KIC position was meaningful; the NPS position is trivially small (1,636 shares, $103K). The divergence is likely explained by passive index rebalancing at NPS (ALB's weight changed in an index NPS tracks) and active portfolio management at KIC (deliberate exit). But the juxtaposition is striking and remains unexplained by any public statement from either institution.

### Anomaly #2: "Energy Security" Proxy Language — Not Commercial Boilerplate


In response to shareholder preferences, Albemarle's proxy includes a management proposal to approve a Charter amendment to remove the remaining supermajority provisions.
 Embedded within the same proxy document is the description of the $350M prepayment as "an important recognition of Albemarle's critical role in **energy security**." This is Albemarle management's own characterization. In a company that deals in commodity chemicals, describing a customer relationship as "energy security" is a deliberate signal about the nature of the counterparty — consistent with a government-adjacent, strategic buyer for whom lithium supply is a national security matter. POSCO fits this description exactly.

### Anomaly #3: Kemerton Idling + POSCO Argentina Phase 2 Ramp — Mirror-Image Capacity Movements

POSCO's Argentina Phase 2 (delayed to Q3 2026) will add ~22,500 t/yr LiOH. Kemerton Train 1 nameplate capacity: ~24,000 t/yr LiOH. These are virtually identical volumes. As POSCO brings Phase 2 online in Australia's time zone neighbor, Kemerton goes dark 120 km from Wodgina. The capacity and geographic coincidence is striking. From a logistics standpoint, Kemerton LiOH (produced from co-located Wodgina spodumene) would be cheaper to deliver to Korean customers than Argentina LiOH (which must cross the Pacific). POSCO is, in effect, building a capacity that Kemerton already has — but in the wrong hemisphere.

### Anomaly #4: Albemarle's Affiliated Transaction Supermajority Removal — Timing with POSCO's Wodgina Stake


At the 2025 annual meeting, a majority of shareholders supported simple majority voting. The Board is now asking shareholders to remove provisions requiring 75% support for **affiliated transactions.** Approval would remove the last supermajority vote standard from the Company.


POSCO, through the MinRes JV, now holds an indirect 15% interest in Wodgina — making POSCO an affiliated party in the mining JV context. If POSCO were to acquire, say, 5–10% of ALB shares, it would become an "affiliated" shareholder for corporate governance purposes. The removal of the 75% affiliated-transaction threshold occurs *exactly* as POSCO becomes a Wodgina co-stakeholder for the first time. The timing correlation is notable. Whether it is causal (POSCO asked Albemarle to remove the provision as a condition of deepening the relationship), coincidental (shareholder activists pushed for this regardless of POSCO), or driven by the 2025 shareholder vote (which preceded POSCO's Wodgina move), is unknown. The 2025 shareholder vote appears to have been the primary driver — but the result coincidentally benefits any POSCO-led transaction.

### Anomaly #5: 
Albemarle's news descriptions explicitly include "offtake and prepayment arrangements connected to lithium and **cesium.**"


The reference to **cesium** in Albemarle's official news feed description is unexpected. Cesium is an extremely rare, high-value critical mineral used in atomic clocks, GPS systems, and defense applications. Albemarle's Greenbushes mine is one of the few global sources of cesium. A cesium prepayment arrangement would be even more strategically sensitive than a lithium prepayment — cesium is a genuinely rare strategic mineral with direct defense applications. **This is an entirely new dimension** not previously identified in this analysis. If the $350M prepayment includes cesium offtake, the counterparty is even more likely to be a sovereign-adjacent entity. POSCO does not have an obvious cesium use case — but a Korean defense-industrial complex purchaser might. This warrants further investigation as a separate research thread.

### Anomaly #6: Mark Widmar Board Appointment — First Solar Parallel

First Solar is the dominant US domestic solar panel manufacturer that has benefited enormously from IRA domestic content provisions and FEOC restrictions on Chinese solar panels. Albemarle is positioning itself as the First Solar equivalent for lithium — a Western-controlled, FEOC-clean supplier that commands a domestic price premium under IRA. Widmar's appointment is the most explicit possible signal that Albemarle's board believes the IRA Western premium thesis is real and that they want the executive who has successfully navigated it. This is a standalone strategy reinforcement, not an M&A preparation signal — but it does indicate Albemarle's management believes the standalone premium thesis justifies continuing independence.

### Anomaly #7: The Expanded Tender Offer — Albemarle Raised the Cap From $500M to $650M
