### ANOMALY #1 — THE GUIDANCE/PRICE PARADOX: MANAGEMENT GUIDES LOWER WHILE SPOT SURGES 30%

The most analytically dissonant finding across this entire three-round research effort is the simultaneous existence of: (a) official management guidance stating EBITDA will be *lower* in 2026 than in 2025; and (b) actual Q1 2026 spot lithium carbonate prices that 
surged nearly 30% since the start of the year to a two-year high
 and have 
jumped to about $24,086 per metric ton, marking a sharp increase from earlier lows in 2025 after a period of oversupply had weighed on the market.


Management's guidance was issued February 11, 2026 — when January's spike was known but not confirmed as sustained. The conservative $10/kg base case scenario for FY2026 EBITDA ($0.7-0.8B) is now substantially below the $17-24/kg spot environment actually prevailing in Q1 2026. 
Management set 2026 scenarios using ~$10/kg full-year 2025 avg, ~$20/kg January 2026, and ~$30/kg 2021-2025 avg
 — suggesting they believed $20/kg was possible (January 2026 spot) but not the conservative planning base.

**Implication for M&A:** If Q1 2026 actual EBITDA (reported May 6, 2026) confirms prices are tracking at $17-24/kg, every financial model underpinning this analysis must be rebuilt. At $20/kg sustained, pro-forma FY2026E EBITDA could be $1.5-1.7B — making the current $20.5B EV a 12-14x acquisition multiple, not 21x. **This is the most time-sensitive analytical update in this document. May 6, 2026 Q1 earnings will be the most important near-term M&A catalyst.**

### ANOMALY #2 — THE GREENBUSHES ROFR INVERSION: THE MOST UNDERPRICED STRATEGIC ASSET IN MINING M&A


Albemarle Corp said it wants to take control of Australia's Greenbushes, signaling it intends to block any rival from buying Tianqi Lithium Corp's controlling stake. While Albemarle said earlier it was interested in the sale, Thursday's comments were the strongest to date that the company intends to exercise its right of first refusal to supersede other potential buyers. "We would be interested in taking a controlling stake in that venture," Eric Norris, head of Albemarle's lithium division, said in a webcast.


The Tianqi 2020 debt crisis has been resolved via the IGO deal, and 
Tianqi Lithium Corporation has set an annual cap of up to 950,000 tons for transactions in 2026 under its long-standing Albemarle Agreements
 — confirming a stable, commercial operating relationship. However, the ROFR structurally remains, and an Albemarle acquirer inherits the right to potentially acquire the controlling 51% Tianqi/IGO bloc if either party seeks exit. 
The 51% is now controlled by Tianqi (26.01%) and IGO (24.99%)
 — and IGO's own strategic review could create a future liquidity event. **No analyst or investor presentation for Albemarle explicitly values this ROFR. It is worth hundreds of millions in optionality — particularly given Greenbushes CGP4 comes online Q1 2027, adding substantial future production.** This option is a strategic free gift to any Albemarle acquirer.

### ANOMALY #3 — CORFO'S LATENT ASSET ACQUISITION RIGHT CREATES A BUYER NATIONALITY FILTER

The CORFO contract explicitly incorporates "the option to purchase assets in the Salar de Atacama" under certain conditions — while simultaneously confirming the contract is valid through 2043 and "will not change." This creates a silent nationality filter: any buyer that Chilean political authorities view as geopolitically problematic (Saudi Arabia, China, Russia) risks inadvertently activating CORFO's asset acquisition mechanism. The "safest" buyers from a CORFO perspective are those with established relationships in Chile (existing Western mining companies with Chilean operations) or those who can demonstrate that their ownership protects Chilean economic interests (U.S. domicile under the Free Trade Agreement with Chile). **ExxonMobil and BHP are the most CORFO-favorable buyers; Saudi Aramco/PIF is the most CORFO-risky despite having the most financial capacity.**

### ANOMALY #4 — THE EXXONMOBIL COMPETITOR-ACQUIRER PARADOX, UPDATED WITH TRAFIGURA ENTRY

The Smackover lithium DLE ecosystem has expanded beyond ExxonMobil and Albemarle: 
Trafigura signed a battery-grade lithium carbonate offtake agreement with Smackover Lithium in March 2026
 — a third player now has offtake in the same Arkansas formation where ExxonMobil and Albemarle both hold rights. This intensifying Smackover competition paradoxically *increases* ExxonMobil's motivation to acquire Albemarle rather than compete: if ExxonMobil's Smackover DLE advantage (first-mover U.S. domestic lithium branding) is being diluted by Trafigura and Albemarle, the value of switching to acquiring the world's lowest-cost *brine* operation (Atacama, not Smackover) grows. **ExxonMobil's calculus: compete for second place in Smackover DLE, or acquire first place globally via Albemarle's Atacama CORFO rights.**

### ANOMALY #5 — KEMERTON IDLING IS "ACCRETIVE" — A SIGNAL THAT ALBEMARLE IS READYING IT FOR SALE

Management confirmed that 
Kemerton idling is accretive to adjusted EBITDA from Q2 2026
 — meaning the plant was EBITDA-dilutive when operating at trough lithium prices. A fully idled, commercially proven plant that is now positively contributing to EBITDA (by removing cash operating costs) represents an asset in optimal sale condition: no ongoing loss drag, no stranded operating contracts, and a clean physical asset ready for a new owner's restart plan. This is the classic PE carve-out setup: an asset that the parent cannot afford to operate at trough prices but which has full replacement value. POSCO, with Wodgina spodumene feed and IRA compliance needs, is the natural operator. **The idling decision reads as implicit preparation for a Kemerton sale.**

### ANOMALY #6 — CHINA'S ANTI-INVOLUTION CAMPAIGN: A SUPPLY SHOCK THAT ACCELERATES WESTERN BUYER URGENCY


Authorities stated 27 mining permits were canceled in the lithium hub of Jiangxi, aligned with the earlier suspension of activity in CATL's Jianxiawo lithium mine amid Beijing's anti-involution campaign.
 Beijing's campaign to eliminate "involutionary" (loss-making, subsidy-dependent) production is functionally equivalent to a supply curtailment — and 
the proposed cancellation of 27 mining rights in Jiangxi has raised market concerns over the pace of regional lithium ore supply releases and regulatory compliance risks, leading to a downward revision in expectations of effective supply.
 If China's government is deliberately constraining domestic lithium supply (to protect margins for politically important battery manufacturers like CATL and BYD), Western lithium producers — especially those outside Chinese regulatory reach (Atacama, Greenbushes, Wodgina) — become disproportionately more valuable. **China's supply curtailment via the anti-involution campaign is the single most underreported bullish catalyst for Albemarle's strategic value to Western buyers in 2026.**

### ANOMALY #7 — JBC FLOODING EVENT: A CLIMATE RISK DISCLOSURE OBLIGATION IN ANY M&A PROCESS


Production volume in 2026 is expected to be impacted by a major flooding event that occurred at the Jordan Bromine Company (JBC) joint venture in January 2026.

Any buyer of the Specialties segment must model recurring JBC climate risk into their underwriting. **This event has likely reduced the achievable Specialties sale multiple by 0.5-1.0 turns ($140-280M on a $2.8B base) by surfacing a previously underappreciated geographic concentration risk.**
