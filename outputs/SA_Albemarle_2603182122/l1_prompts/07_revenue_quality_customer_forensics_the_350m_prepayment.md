ROLE: You are a quality of earnings analyst and revenue forensics specialist at a Big 4 accounting firm, conducting pre-transaction commercial due diligence on Albemarle Corporation (NYSE: ALB). You have been engaged by a potential buyer's advisors to assess the quality, durability, and risk profile of Albemarle's revenue base — with particular focus on customer concentration, contract structure, pricing mechanisms, and the $350M customer prepayment.

CONTEXT: Albemarle reported ~$5.1B revenue in FY2025, with the following geographic breakdown (FY2023 data, most recent available): U.S. 17.3%, China 39.4%, South Korea 15.4%, Japan 7%, Other 20.9%. The company serves approximately 1,900 customers across 70 countries, but this figure includes all segments (lithium, bromine, catalysts). The lithium (Energy Storage) segment — ~67% of revenue post-Ketjen — almost certainly has significantly higher concentration among a handful of major cathode manufacturers and battery cell makers in Northeast Asia. A 2022 proxy disclosed one customer at ~19% of lithium sales. The FY2024 10-K does not appear to disclose any customer above 10% of consolidated revenue in publicly accessible portions. In January 2025, Albemarle received a $350M customer prepayment for future lithium deliveries — the customer identity is undisclosed but POSCO is the most likely candidate.

RESEARCH TASKS:

1. CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION FORENSICS
a) Search Albemarle's FY2024 and FY2025 10-K filings on SEC EDGAR for any customer concentration disclosure, particularly in: Item 1 Business (Customers section), Item 1A Risk Factors, and Notes to Financial Statements (specifically "Concentration of Credit Risk" and "Revenue from Contracts with Customers" notes). Extract and reproduce any language about customer concentration verbatim.
b) Research Albemarle's DEF 14A (proxy statements) from 2023, 2024, and 2025 — proxies sometimes disclose customer-specific revenue in the context of related party transactions or compensation performance metrics.
c) Search for any Albemarle 8-K filings that reference customer agreements, supply contracts, or the $350M prepayment specifically.
d) Cross-reference: which battery manufacturers and cathode producers are Albemarle's most likely top 5 customers? Build a customer identification matrix using:
   - South Korean companies (POSCO Future M, Samsung SDI, LG Energy Solution) — 15.4% of revenue
   - Chinese companies (CATL, BYD, Ganfeng downstream, Tianqi downstream) — 39.4% of revenue
   - Japanese companies (Panasonic, Sumitomo Metal Mining, Toyota/Prime Planet Energy) — 7% of revenue
   - U.S. companies (Tesla, GM Ultium, Ford) — some portion of 17.3% U.S. revenue
   For each, research their disclosed lithium supply agreements (check their own filings, press releases, and investor presentations for Albemarle-specific references).

2. THE $350M PREPAYMENT — COMPLETE FORENSIC ANALYSIS
a) Research every Albemarle filing and earnings call transcript that references the prepayment. What exactly did management say? When was it received? What is the delivery schedule? What pricing terms were disclosed?
b) Research whether POSCO, Samsung SDI, LG Energy Solution, or any other major battery materials company has disclosed a $350M lithium prepayment in their own filings. Search Korean (DART), Japanese, and Chinese filings.
c) Research industry precedents for lithium prepayments: have other producers (SQM, Pilbara Minerals, Ganfeng) received similar prepayments? What were the typical terms — fixed price, index-linked, volume-committed? What change-of-control provisions were included?
d) Assess the M&A implications of the prepayment:
   - If the prepayment includes a change-of-control termination clause, what is the cash impact? ($350M refund obligation)
   - If the prepayment includes fixed-price delivery obligations, is this an above-market or below-market contract?
   - Could the prepayment counterparty have a ROFR or matching right in an Albemarle sale process?
   - Does the prepayment create a "blocking" relationship where the counterparty must consent to a deal?

3. CONTRACT STRUCTURE AND PRICING MECHANISMS
a) Research Albemarle's contract vs. spot revenue mix. Management has targeted approximately 50/50. Verify this using 10-K disclosures or earnings call transcripts.
b) How do Albemarle's lithium supply contracts typically price? Research: quarterly resets, annual resets, floor/ceiling mechanisms, index-linked (which index — Fastmarkets, LME lithium?), fixed-price, or hybrid. What is the typical contract duration?
c) Compare Albemarle's contract structure to SQM, Pilbara Minerals, and other public lithium producers. Is Albemarle more or less exposed to spot pricing?
d) Research Albemarle's CORFO preferential pricing obligation — how much volume must be sold at below-market rates to Chilean manufacturers? What pricing formula applies? How many companies have applied through the CORFO process?

4. REVENUE QUALITY INDICATORS
a) Research Albemarle's accounts receivable trends, DSO (days sales outstanding), and any bad debt provisions in the last 3 years. Are receivables from Chinese customers growing as a share of total?
b) Research Albemarle's cash flow conversion: net income to cash from operations ratio over 2022-2025. The ISS governance score of 9/10 and cash flow restatements flagged in prior research suggest potential quality issues.
c) Research the specific cash flow reclassifications that Albemarle has made in recent filings. What amounts were reclassified between operating, investing, and financing activities? Why?
d) Research the FY2025 "adjusted" EBITDA reconciliation — what adjustments are being made? Are they recurring? Quantify the gap between GAAP and adjusted metrics.

5. GEOPOLITICAL CUSTOMER RISK
a) With ~40% of revenue from China, research: how exposed is Albemarle to Chinese government retaliatory actions (e.g., informal direction to Chinese battery makers to reduce purchases from Albemarle)? Has this happened to any other Western mining company?
b) Research IRA FEOC customer migration: are Albemarle's Korean and Japanese customers increasing their purchases (to replace Chinese-origin lithium for IRA compliance), while Chinese customers are potentially decreasing? Any evidence of this in quarterly volume trends?
c) If U.S.-China trade tensions escalate to the point where Chinese customers stop buying Albemarle product, estimate the revenue at risk (39.4% of ~$5B = ~$2B) and the time required to redirect that volume to non-Chinese buyers.

6. CUSTOMER SWITCHING COSTS AND REVENUE DURABILITY
a) How easy is it for a cathode manufacturer to switch lithium suppliers? Research the qualification process for battery-grade lithium hydroxide and carbonate — does each customer need to re-qualify Albemarle's product for their specific cell chemistry? What is the typical qualification timeline?
b) Are there any exclusive supply arrangements between Albemarle and specific customers?
c) Research customer churn: has Albemarle lost any significant customers in the last 3 years? Has it gained any significant new customers?

OUTPUT FORMAT: Customer concentration matrix, prepayment forensic timeline, contract structure analysis table, and revenue quality scorecard. All findings must cite specific filings and documents.

End your output with "NOTABLE ANOMALIES" — anything surprising. Pay particular attention to:
- Any evidence of customer concentration that contradicts Albemarle's "1,900 customers" narrative
- The prepayment customer identity — any confirmed or near-confirmed identification
- Unusual cash flow patterns that suggest revenue recognition issues
- Discrepancies between geographic revenue mix and disclosed customer base
- Any customer-specific risk that would not be visible from segment-level reporting
- Evidence of customer migration (from Chinese to Korean/Japanese buyers) that could change the concentration profile
