**ANOMALY #1 (CONFIRMED, UPGRADED): THE CTT IS NOW AN INDUSTRY STANDARD — AND FERVO OWNS THE TEMPLATE**


The regulatory process to approve the CTT has been key to proving the model to other potential customers — demonstrating that "deals like this really do allow customers to choose their resource without having an additional rate burden on other customers."
 More significantly: 
Google has already used a variation on the clean transition tariff for two geothermal deals with NV Energy in Nevada — with Fervo and with Ormat — and is now expanding it to Minnesota.
 Fervo helped architect a commercial and regulatory template that is now being adopted industry-wide. This is a **platform creation event** — analogous to Stripe creating the payment infrastructure that all fintechs now use. Any acquirer of Fervo does not merely get a power plant portfolio; they get the company that invented the dominant financing mechanism for firm clean power in the U.S. This is not reflected in the $2-3B IPO valuation.

**ANOMALY #2 (NEW, HIGHEST PRIORITY): PROJECT BLANFORD — THE UNDISCLOSED RESOURCE BASE**


In February 2026, Fervo drilled its hottest well to date at a nearby greenfield site (Project Blanford), achieving temperatures over 555°F at approximately 11,200 feet — building confidence for Cape Station scalability.
 This finding, two weeks before the current analysis date, has not been widely covered in investment or cleantech media. Its significance: 555°F at 11,200 feet is an extraordinary resource. By comparison, the geothermal gradient that makes Cape Station viable is approximately 350-400°F at similar depths. A 555°F resource would support significantly higher power output per well pair and could anchor a future development phase potentially larger than Cape Station itself. **Any acquirer conducting pre-IPO due diligence on Fervo's resource base would be doing so with the knowledge that Project Blanford exists and is hotter than Cape Station.** This is a material upward revision to the total resource value — and it is not yet priced into the IPO valuation.
