You are a GEOTHERMAL OPERATIONS AND PROJECT EXECUTION ANALYST investigating two related signals about Fervo Energy's operational performance and development timeline: the conspicuous absence of production data from its pilot project, and the apparent gap between announced timelines and visible construction activity.

CONTEXT:
Fervo Energy's Project Red is a 3.5 MW (net) pilot enhanced geothermal system (EGS) located near Milford, Utah, interconnected with the Nevada grid. Project Red achieved first power in November 2022, making it the world's first confirmed EGS project to deliver electricity to a commercial grid. Fervo published initial results in collaboration with DOE and academic partners, reporting flow rates of ~63 liters/second and temperatures of ~191°C from a horizontal well pair with a ~1,000-foot lateral. These results were described as exceeding expectations.

However, since the initial announcement and publication (approximately early 2023), Fervo has released ZERO updated production data from Project Red — no capacity factor reports, no flow rate degradation curves, no reservoir sustainability data, and no thermal drawdown measurements. For a pre-IPO company whose entire investment thesis rests on the scalability and durability of its EGS technology, this 28-month silence is a material information gap. Geothermal reservoirs can experience "thermal drawdown" — declining temperature over time — which is the single greatest long-term risk for EGS projects.

Separately, Cape Station Phase II (the expansion from ~116 MW to ~400 MW) has a reported commercial operation date (COD) of 2028 under the SCE PPA. Drilling for Phase II has not been publicly announced as of mid-2025. Given that Phase II requires drilling potentially 50-100+ additional wells (at ~20 days per well, this is 3-6 years of single-rig drilling, or 1-2 years with 3-4 rigs), plus surface plant construction and interconnection, the 2028 COD requires drilling to begin essentially immediately. Any delay beyond mid-2025 risks missing the COD and triggering PPA penalties or termination.

HYPOTHESIS TO INVESTIGATE:
The absence of Project Red production data for 28 months is a deliberate information management strategy by Fervo — either because (a) the data shows thermal drawdown or flow rate decline that would concern investors, or (b) the data shows exceptional performance that Fervo is holding for the S-1 prospectus as a "reveal" to support IPO pricing. Meanwhile, Phase II drilling has likely already begun (or is imminent) but has not been publicly announced because Fervo is in a pre-IPO quiet period.
