CONTEXT:

BLM geothermal leases are competitively bid and require environmental review (NEPA), making them time-consuming (12-24 months) and expensive to obtain. Fervo's lease portfolio may represent hundreds of thousands of acres of geothermal resource rights in the most prospective EGS basins in the western U.S.

This IP has been developed with partial DOE funding (which may impose march-in rights or government use licenses) but is largely proprietary.

HP has publicly signaled interest in geothermal as a growth market (see HP CEO John Lindsay's comments in earnings calls, 2023-2024). HP's market cap is ~$3-4B, and it has the rig fleet, drilling expertise, and field crews that would make a Fervo acquisition immediately synergistic — HP could internalize the geothermal technology, eliminating the contractor margin and creating a vertically integrated geothermal development company.
