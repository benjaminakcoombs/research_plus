A close look at the production rate plot suggests it is not levelling out as Fervo states; the rate declines from about 97 to 93 kg/s over the last 12 hours shown, with this declining trend continuing at the end of the plot. This has obvious implications for the consistency and sustainability of production.


However, Project Red's 12-month zero-decline result at lower temperature provides countervailing evidence. Cape Station's higher temperature (220°C+ vs. ~200°C) cuts both ways: better power output but greater potential for thermal breakthrough. D&M certification of reserves implies D&M has reviewed and accepted Fervo's decline modeling — this is the strongest single piece of evidence that decline risk is manageable.

**Risk 3 — DOE LPO Absence: CONFIRMED RISK [MODERATE IMPACT, MODERATE UNCERTAINTY]**

No LPO guarantee exists for Cape Station. Without federal credit support, Phase II project finance will price at 8–10% cost of debt rather than 4–6%, adding $8–15/MWh to LCOE. Fervo's relationship with Energy Secretary Wright (Liberty Energy) provides a political pathway but no guarantee of execution. This is a **$15–25/MWh potential improvement** in LCOE if an LPO guarantee is subsequently announced.

**Risk 4 — IPO Execution and Capital Markets Window: MODERATE RISK**


The Series E funding comes as Fervo reportedly prepares to become a publicly traded company, which would let it raise even more capital for its ambitious projects.
 The IPO window requires: (a) stable equity markets, (b) Phase I on track for October 2026 COD, and (c) no major EGS technical failures at comparable projects. All three are currently favorable but not guaranteed. A successful IPO at $2.5B+ unlocks Phase II capital on favorable terms; a pulled or disappointing IPO forces Fervo back to expensive private markets.

**Risk 5 — Phase II Geology Extrapolation: LOW-MODERATE RISK**


Fervo has now set flow rate records in each of their two production wells, and along with FORGE's results, it is proven that very high flow rates are consistently reproducible. FORGE results corroborate results from Fervo's Project Cape and Project Red — that multistage hydraulic fracturing with plug and perf and proppant can achieve high flow rates per well.


Three independent confirmations (Project Red, Cape Station, FORGE) of the core flow rate result at different locations and depths substantially de-risk Phase II geology extrapolation.

**"The drilling cost breakthrough MORE than offsets the parasitic load penalty, making Fervo's true unit economics significantly better than any published estimate — potentially at $45–55/MWh LCOE competitive with solar+4hr storage."**

- **The drilling breakthrough is real** and accelerating beyond the original Stanford Workshop data. 79% reduction vs. DOE baseline at Sugarloaf (15,765 ft) implies Cape's 8,500 ft wells will achieve costs well below $4M/well as Phase II ramps.
