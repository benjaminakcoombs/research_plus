## I. The Opportunity

CRH and Holcim invested $75M in Sublime Systems in September 2024 — equity, binding offtake, and priority site selection rights for a company Holcim called "a disruptive force in cement making."^1 Eighteen months later, Sublime operates with roughly 28 employees, its Holyoke plant paused, its $87M DOE grant cancelled.^2 The company that appeared to have won the green cement race has exited it.

There is exactly one replacement. Brimstone Energy is the only company in the world to have produced ASTM C150 portland cement from non-limestone feedstock, independently certified by Twining Consulting.^3 The certification distinction is not cosmetic: ASTM C150 is mandatory for all federally funded infrastructure under AASHTO M85, covering an estimated $4–5.6B annual DOT/infrastructure segment for which no state transportation agency has approved C1157-only materials — the standard Sublime's product meets.^i ^4 Brimstone addresses the full U.S. cement market. Sublime was structurally excluded from more than half of it. CRH and Amrize (Holcim's NYSE-listed U.S. spinoff) now face an urgent gap in their green cement portfolios, and the sole C150-certified replacement has raised only ~$80M, has a $400M plant to fund, and has not closed a Series B.^5 ^6 The entity that secures equity plus offtake before Q3 2026 sets the strategic terms for the next decade of decarbonized cement supply.

## II. Company Situation

Brimstone Energy has developed a patented hydrometallurgical process to co-produce ASTM C150 portland cement, supplementary cementitious materials, and smelter-grade alumina simultaneously from calcium silicate rocks — one mining and grinding operation yielding calcium for cement, aluminum compounds for alumina, and SCM as residue, with magnesium compounds passively absorbing atmospheric CO₂.^7 The company operates bench and lab-scale equipment at its Oakland headquarters and is engineering — but has not broken ground on — a demonstration-scale plant near Reno, Nevada.^7 The first commercial facility targets 80,000 t/yr of cement and 20,000 t/yr of alumina at the Dolese Bros. Roosevelt quarry near Lawton, Oklahoma, at a capital cost of $378–400M, with operations before 2030.^6

At Plant 1 nameplate, estimated annual revenue is approximately $21.8M: $11.2M from cement, $9.6M from alumina at domestic-premium pricing, and $1.0M from SCM.^ii The co-production model allocates shared mining and grinding costs primarily to cement, making alumina extraction cost an estimated $125–225/t versus $560–585/t for landed imported alumina — a structural advantage embedded in the process architecture, not dependent on any subsidy.^ii But this has never been demonstrated outside bench scale, and no independent party has verified the alumina quality. Revenue quality is further constrained by customer concentration: the only executed commercial agreement is a non-binding reservation from Amazon for unspecified volumes of cement and SCM, conditional on testing completion through 2026–2027.^8

Brimstone is private, founder-led, and VC-backed. Total disclosed fundraising exceeds $80M, anchored by a $55M Series A in March 2022 co-led by Breakthrough Energy Ventures and DCVC, with participation from Amazon Climate Pledge Fund, Fifth Wall, and eleven other investors.^5 Kajima Ventures — the corporate venture arm of Kajima Corporation, Japan's largest construction conglomerate — appeared on the October 2025 investor list without a standalone funding announcement, disclosed only inside the Amazon commercial agreement press release.^5 Amazon itself occupies four distinct roles — equity investor, commercial customer, SCoBA founding member, and testing partner — each generating independent change-of-control consent considerations. No Amazon Climate Pledge Fund portfolio company has ever been acquired in a change-of-control transaction across the fund's history.^30 The board comprises only the two co-founders, an unusually concentrated governance structure for a company with institutional backing at this scale.^9 No Form D has been filed for a new equity round as of March 2026, though Crunchbase assigns a 76% probability that Brimstone raises within six months.^10

The last twelve months have produced the strongest competitive positioning and the most precarious financing environment in Brimstone's history. The DOE cancelled the company's $189M OCED grant in May 2025 — only $8.7M had been disbursed — and the One Big Beautiful Bill Act subsequently repealed the IRA's LPO loan authority, closing the alternative government debt path.^11 ^12 In parallel, Brimstone signed a feedstock LOI with Dolese Bros., secured Amazon's reservation, and hired CFO Tim Rens — whose career includes raising nearly $20B across industrial capital markets, most recently $750M for Monolith Materials before that company's own DOE loan was cancelled.^5 ^13 The competitive landscape shifted decisively when EGA and Century Aluminum signed a joint development agreement for a 750,000 t/yr aluminum smelter in Inola, Oklahoma — the first new U.S. smelter since 1980, requiring approximately 1.5M t/yr of alumina against domestic supply of roughly 400,000 t/yr.^14 Brimstone is the only announced domestic non-bauxite alumina source, in the same state. Meanwhile, 50% Section 232 tariffs on aluminum imports since June 2025 have made domestic smelting economics favorable but left alumina itself uncovered — creating a structural demand premium for any domestic producer that can deliver.^31 Amrize's investor day on March 25, 2026 may signal whether the post-Holcim entity intends to pivot from Sublime toward an alternative green cement position; the timing, days from this document's publication, makes it the nearest hard catalyst in the analysis.^15

The most revealing gap between narrative and reality appears in the December 2025 "Fourth Great Refinery" announcement, which expanded Brimstone's product roadmap to include steel, magnesium, titanium, and other critical minerals — positioning against a "$2.4 trillion market."^7 None of these additional products have been demonstrated at any scale. The first plant produces only cement, SCM, and alumina. More critically, the alumina itself — which carries the highest per-tonne value, the national security narrative, and the EGA demand thesis — has zero published specification data across any of the thirteen standard smelter-grade parameters after six years of development.^16 The cement is independently certified. The alumina is an assertion awaiting verification, and every investor paying a "critical minerals platform" multiple is pricing in that unconfirmed claim.

## III. Key Findings

### $245M in Platform Value Rests on Alumina No Third Party Has Tested

**The Observation.** Brimstone has claimed "smelter-grade alumina" in every communication since January 2025, yet has published none of the thirteen standard SGA specification parameters — no Al₂O₃ purity, no Fe₂O₃ or TiO₂ assay, no crystal phase data, no BET surface area.^16 The cement, by contrast, holds independent ASTM C150 certification.^3 The Roosevelt quarry feedstock is documented in peer-reviewed geological literature not as pure anorthosite but as "Fe-rich, Ti-rich" anorthositic gabbro, with massif-type anorthosite — the low-impurity variant — explicitly absent from the formation.^17 Published hydrometallurgical studies confirm that ilmenite, the primary titanium mineral in this rock, dissolves at 93–97% in HCl under standard leach conditions, introducing massive dissolved titanium into the alumina leachate.^18 The Brimstone patent describes no dedicated titanium removal step.^19 Decontamination factors of 738–5,905× would be required to reach SGA specification from this feedstock — levels not demonstrated in any published non-Bayer process.^iii

**The Deal Implication.** This binary determines whether Brimstone warrants a critical-minerals multiple (12–15× EBITDA) or a cement-technology multiple (7–8×).^ix At Plant 1, the NPV difference between SGA pricing and chemical-grade is approximately $49M; at five-plant platform scale, $245M.^iv Any term sheet pricing a "critical minerals platform" without independent SGA verification accepts this as unquantified risk. A single ICP-MS measurement on pilot leachate — costing approximately $50 — resolves the titanium question. The alternative explanation — that Brimstone has solved this in unpublished pilot work and is withholding as trade secret — is plausible but cannot be assumed at these dollar magnitudes.

**Open Questions:**

- Has any aluminum smelter operator — EGA, Century, or Alcoa — received Brimstone alumina samples for smelter qualification testing?
- What is the dissolved titanium concentration in Brimstone's HCl leachate when Roosevelt quarry rock is processed at operating conditions?

### The $400M Financing Problem Has a Single Key — and It's Already on the Cap Table

**The Observation.** The DOE grant cancellation and IRA LPO closure require Brimstone to finance its entire ~$400M plant privately.^11 ^12 The most favorable remaining government instrument — the DOD Office of Strategic Capital critical minerals loan ($500M OBBBA appropriation, Treasury rates, 50-year tenor) — requires "a repayment guarantee from a creditworthy sponsor."^20 Dolese Bros. cannot serve: its nonvoting majority shareholders are three university foundations holding fiduciary duties that preclude speculative co-investment in a first-of-kind startup.^21 This leaves Kajima Corporation — A+ rated by R&I, with seven decades building chemical plants, steelworks, and nuclear reactors — as the only cap table entity satisfying the OSC's creditworthiness, construction capability, and equity relationship requirements simultaneously.^22 ^23 Kajima's documented pattern of linking CVC investments to parent-company EPC mandates is consistent with a linked construction role, though no Brimstone-specific EPC disclosure has been made.^23 The MP Materials precedent — $400M DOW equity plus $150M OSC loan, July 2025 — confirms the OSC is actively deploying to domestic mineral processors at this scale.^24

**The Deal Implication.** If the hypothesis holds, a Kajima LSTK EPC contract with performance bonds costs approximately $5–6M in bond premiums while simultaneously satisfying the OSC sponsor requirement, transferring scale-up risk to an A+-rated counterparty, and compressing the financing timeline by an estimated 18–24 months.^v The OSC debt tranche ($100–150M at Treasury rates) would reduce the Series B equity requirement by that full amount — fundamentally changing the dilution calculus for every co-investor. If the alumina quality finding above resolves unfavorably, the revenue model weakens, potentially undermining the debt serviceability that underpins this loan thesis.

**Open Questions:**

- Does Kajima's investment include any commercial terms linking the equity position to an EPC mandate for the Oklahoma plant?
- Has Brimstone filed an OSC pre-application consultation or registered as a DIBC member?

### Three Clocks, One Window: Any Strategic Position Must Be Secured by Q3 2026

**The Observation.** Three independent deadlines converge on a six-month window, creating urgency invisible from any individual data point. *First*, Sublime's collapse has impaired CRH's and Amrize's green cement investments, but Sublime has not formally dissolved — CRH's "priority site selection rights" and any associated non-compete provisions likely remain legally in force, constraining CRH from freely investing in a competing C150 technology until a formal dissolution or restructuring occurs.^25 Amrize holds a convertible note, not equity, and can exit cleanly — but lacks a dedicated venture vehicle.^26 *Second*, EGA's Bechtel FEED for the Inola smelter will lock in alumina receiving infrastructure design within an estimated 6–12 months;^14 if Brimstone is excluded from this phase, it risks permanent lock-out from the Oklahoma alumina supply chain — a window operative only if the alumina quality question resolves favorably. *Third*, Brimstone's Series B is structurally imminent: 76% Crunchbase probability within six months, a CFO with $20B raise experience hired October 2025, and "investors in place" language in September.^10 ^5 ^27 Brimstone's ASTM C150 certification further amplifies the strategic premium: it addresses ~100% of U.S. cement demand including the approximately $5B DOT/infrastructure segment that Sublime's C1157 product could never access.^i

**The Deal Implication.** Any banker advising a strategic investor must calibrate outreach to the earliest-closing window. If CRH is the target, the threshold question is the Sublime non-compete — its answer determines whether CRH can move at all in the near term. If EGA is the target, engagement must precede FEED infrastructure decisions. In all cases, the Series B represents a hard deadline for pre-competitive pricing. If the Kajima EPC hypothesis from Finding 2 holds, the Series B may close faster — a smaller equity round closes sooner — compressing this window further. An approach in Q2 2026 captures all three windows; an approach in Q4 2026 may find all three closed.

**Open Questions:**

- Does CRH's Sublime agreement contain a non-compete covering C150 green cement technology — and does it terminate upon dissolution or survive?
- Has Brimstone or any intermediary initiated contact with EGA's project team regarding alumina supply inclusion in the Bechtel FEED scope?
