## Finding 1

**Working title:** "The $245 Million Question Brimstone Has Never Answered: Why Six Years of 'Smelter-Grade Alumina' Claims Have Zero Published Specification Data — and What the Roosevelt Quarry Geology Implies"

**Source tension points:** TP1, TP7, TP13, TP4

**The core insight:** Brimstone's most valuable narrative — that it produces smelter-grade alumina from domestic rock, making it a critical minerals platform rather than a green cement startup — rests on a product quality claim supported by zero published data across any of the 13 standard SGA specification parameters. Meanwhile, the company's confirmed quarry feedstock (GMLC anorthositic gabbro) is documented in peer-reviewed geological literature as "Fe-rich, Ti-rich" — and the published hydrometallurgical literature shows that ilmenite (the Ti-bearing mineral in this rock) dissolves at 93-97% in HCl under standard leach conditions, meaning the process leachate carries massive titanium contamination requiring decontamination factors of 738-5,905x to reach SGA spec. The Brimstone patent describes no titanium removal step. The EGA/Century Inola smelter — creating 1.5M t/yr of alumina demand in the same state — makes this question financially consequential: SGA-grade alumina at $400-500/t vs. chemical-grade at $150-250/t represents a $49M NPV difference at Plant 1 and $245M at platform scale. The binary outcome determines whether this company warrants a critical-minerals multiple or a cement-tech multiple — a 2-3x valuation swing.

**Evidence mix:**
- DOCUMENTED: The absence of SGA specification data (confirmed across all press releases, patents, and investor communications 2019-2026). The GMLC geology (Oklahoma Geological Survey, SOA Springer chapter). Ilmenite dissolution rates (93-97% Ti extraction confirmed by three independent studies). SGA specification thresholds (industry standard). Patent architecture (US20210070656A1 — zero Ti removal steps described). ASTM C150 cement certification (Twining Consulting, third-party verified). Amazon commercial agreement mentions cement and SCM only — never alumina.
- CIRCUMSTANTIAL: The inference that this reflects a genuine technical limitation rather than deliberate trade-secret withholding. The decontamination factor calculations (derived from published dissolution rates applied to estimated feedstock composition). The crystal phase risk (TP13 — established in literature, unconfirmed for Brimstone's specific process).
- **Writer signal guidance:** The writer should present the documented evidence chain (geological characterization → dissolution chemistry → absence of data) as established fact, then frame the interpretive conclusions ("this may reflect an undisclosed technical barrier") with appropriate hedging ("until specification data are published"). The pre-mortem — that Brimstone may have solved this in unpublished pilot work and is withholding as trade secret — must be acknowledged as a plausible alternative.

**Deal implication:** Any investor or acquirer paying a "critical minerals platform" multiple (12-15x EBITDA) without independent SGA verification is accepting a $49-245M unquantified risk. A single ICP-MS measurement on pilot leachate — costing ~$50 — resolves the titanium question. This is due diligence item #1 for any term sheet, and an advisor who surfaces it before a cap table negotiation prevents value destruction for their client.

**Key open questions:**
1. What is the dissolved titanium concentration in Brimstone's HCl leachate when GMLC rock is processed at their actual operating conditions? (This single measurement — the most valuable undisclosed data point in Brimstone's possession — collapses the entire SGA feasibility analysis to a binary answer.)
2. Has any aluminum smelter operator — EGA, Century, Alcoa, or any third party — received Brimstone alumina samples for qualification testing? (The complete absence of any disclosed alumina customer engagement, contrasted with the detailed Amazon cement testing program, is the most telling asymmetry in the company's commercial record.)
