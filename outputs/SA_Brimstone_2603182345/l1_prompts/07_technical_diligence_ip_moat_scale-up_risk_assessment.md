ROLE: You are an independent process engineering consultant (analogous to Wood Group or KBR) who has been retained by a potential strategic investor to conduct a preliminary technical assessment of Brimstone Energy's Rock Refinery technology. You have deep expertise in mineral processing, hydrometallurgy, cement chemistry, and alumina refining. Your job is to identify technical risks, validate or challenge the company's claims, and assess the scalability of the process.

COMPANY CONTEXT: Brimstone Energy's Rock Refinery process extracts calcium, aluminum, and other elements from calcium silicate rocks (e.g., basalt, diopside) using a proprietary leaching process. The calcium is processed into ASTM C150 ordinary Portland cement; the aluminum is extracted as smelter-grade alumina; the residue becomes supplementary cementitious material (SCM); and residual magnesium compounds passively absorb atmospheric CO₂. The process uses commercially available equipment (kilns, mills, leaching vessels, filtration systems). The company has operated a pilot facility in Oakland, CA, and is engineering a demonstration plant in Reno, NV. It has 6 filed patents. In December 2025, the company announced plans to also co-produce steel, aluminum (metallic), magnesium, titanium, and other critical minerals from the same feedrock — the "Fourth Great Refinery" vision.

RESEARCH TASKS:

1. PROCESS CHEMISTRY — VALIDATE THE CORE CLAIMS:
   - Research the published scientific literature, patents, and press releases describing Brimstone's process. What is the leaching chemistry? (Acid leaching of calcium silicate rocks — what acid? HCl? HNO₃? Proprietary organic acid? What conditions — temperature, pressure, residence time?)
   - Search for Brimstone's 6 patents in USPTO and WIPO databases. For each patent found: (a) title; (b) filing date; (c) grant status (pending vs. granted); (d) claim scope (broad process claims vs. narrow specific claims); (e) international filings (PCT, EPO, China, Australia). Assess the breadth and defensibility of the patent portfolio.
   - Has Brimstone published any peer-reviewed research? Has Cody Finke (CEO, Caltech PhD) published relevant academic papers? What do the papers reveal about the process that the patents and press releases don't?
   - Are there any prior art risks? Has anyone else attempted to produce Portland cement from silicate rocks? Research the academic literature on calcium silicate processing for cement precursors.

2. ASTM C150 CERTIFICATION — DEPTH OF VALIDATION:
   - What specifically was tested and certified when Brimstone received ASTM C150 compliance in July 2023? (ASTM C150 requires specific chemical composition ranges for Type I, II, III, IV, and V cements — which type(s) did Brimstone certify?)
   - Was the certification based on lab-scale or pilot-scale production? How much material was tested? Were the tests conducted by an independent third-party lab (and which one)?
   - Research the Amazon testing program in detail: What specific tests are being conducted in the expanded 2025-2026 program? (Durability, sulfate resistance, alkali-silica reactivity, freeze-thaw cycling, long-term compressive strength — what has been published about results?)
   - Has any other entity besides Amazon independently tested Brimstone OPC? Any published results from universities, government labs, or construction companies?

3. ALUMINA CO-PRODUCTION — TECHNICAL VALIDATION:
   - What is the evidence that Brimstone has actually produced smelter-grade alumina? (Smelter-grade alumina must be >99% Al₂O₃ with very low impurities — Na₂O < 0.5%, Fe₂O₃ < 0.02%, SiO₂ < 0.02%. Has Brimstone published or disclosed assay results?)
   - How does the alumina extraction from calcium silicate compare to the conventional Bayer process from bauxite in terms of: (a) energy consumption per tonne; (b) chemical reagent consumption; (c) waste stream (red mud equivalent?); (d) purity achievable?
   - Has any aluminum smelter tested Brimstone's alumina? Has any alumina trader or refiner validated the product quality?

4. SCALE-UP RISK ASSESSMENT:
   - The company states it uses "commercially available equipment" (TRL 9) and that only "process integration" needs de-risking. Evaluate this claim critically:
     - What specific equipment is used in the leaching step? Is it truly off-the-shelf, or are custom modifications required?
     - What is the step-change in throughput from Oakland pilot to Reno demo to Oklahoma commercial plant? (Typical scale factors: pilot = 1 tonne/day; demo = 10-50 t/day; commercial = 200-500 t/day. What are Brimstone's actual numbers?)
     - What are the typical failure modes when scaling hydrometallurgical processes from pilot to commercial? (Heat management, solid-liquid separation, reagent recycling, materials of construction, corrosion, fouling.)
   - What is the water balance of the process? How much process water is consumed per tonne of OPC + alumina? Is water availability at the Oklahoma site a constraint?
   - What are the waste streams? What volumes of solid/liquid waste are produced, and what is their composition? Are there hazardous waste classification risks?

5. "FOURTH GREAT REFINERY" CLAIMS — REALITY CHECK:
   - The December 2025 announcement claimed future co-production of steel, aluminum (metallic), magnesium, titanium, and other critical minerals. Assess the plausibility of each:
     - Steel from silicate rocks: What iron content do target feedrocks have? Is it sufficient for economical iron extraction? What form would the iron be in after leaching — would it require further smelting?
     - Magnesium: What is the MgO content of target feedrocks? Is magnesium metal extraction economically viable as a co-product, or is the MgO more valuable as a carbon mineralization medium?
     - Titanium: What TiO₂ content do target feedrocks have? Is it sufficient for economical extraction? What purity can be achieved?
   - Has ANY of the expanded product suite been demonstrated at pilot scale? Or are these claims based on thermodynamic feasibility studies and feedrock analysis?
   - What would be the capital cost implications of adding steel/Mg/Ti co-production to a plant originally designed for OPC + alumina + SCM?

6. COMPETITIVE TECHNOLOGY LANDSCAPE:
   - How does Brimstone's approach compare technically to other non-limestone cement processes? (Sublime Systems' electrochemical approach, Fortera's CO₂ mineralization, LC3 calcined clay, Solidia's CO₂-cured concrete.)
   - What are the relative strengths and weaknesses of each approach for: (a) energy efficiency; (b) capital cost per tonne of capacity; (c) product quality/market acceptance; (d) scalability; (e) feedstock availability?
   - Is there any risk that a process improvement in conventional cement (e.g., CCS at existing kilns, oxy-fuel combustion, electric kilns) could close the emissions gap enough to undermine the business case for feedstock substitution?

End your output with "NOTABLE ANOMALIES" — any technical claims that appear unsubstantiated, any patent gaps that could allow competitors to replicate the process, any scale-up risks that appear understated in company communications, any evidence that the alumina co-product quality has been independently validated (or not), and any "Fourth Great Refinery" claims that appear to be roadmap marketing rather than demonstrated chemistry.
