You are a building materials sector M&A analyst covering the global cement industry. You are investigating whether Sublime Systems' operational collapse creates a time-limited window for cement majors to pivot their green cement technology bets toward Brimstone Energy — and which specific company is most likely to move first.

CONTEXT:
Sublime Systems, a venture-backed startup developing an electrochemical process to produce low-carbon cement, has reduced its workforce from ~128 to approximately 28–30 employees between December 2025 and March 2026 (per Bloomberg, March 12, 2026). Its demonstration plant in Holyoke, Massachusetts, is paused indefinitely. Sublime's investors include three of the world's largest cement companies:

- CRH plc: Holds equity in Sublime (estimated ~$37.5M based on round allocation). CRH is the world's largest building materials company by market cap (~$65B), operates 12 U.S./Canada cement plants through its Ash Grove division, and has a $250M ventures fund with remaining dry powder. CRH separately spent $2.1B to acquire Eco Material Technologies (SCM producer) in 2024. CRH generates significant revenue from DOT-funded infrastructure projects.

- Amrize (formerly Taiheiyo Cement USA): Independently NYSE-listed (October 2025), holds a convertible note (not equity) in Sublime plus advance payments for future supplies, per Amrize's Form 10-Q SEC filing. Amrize is expanding its Dallas-Fort Worth conventional cement capacity. Its investor day is scheduled for March 25, 2026. Amrize has a data center concrete partnership with Meta.

- Holcim: Global cement leader with a pending split (North American business spinning as "Amrize" — note: this is a different entity; Holcim's NA spin is separate). Holcim invested in Sublime at an earlier stage.

Additionally, Siam Cement Group (SCG), Thailand's largest industrial conglomerate, is an early investor in Sublime predating the CRH/Holcim/Amrize round. SCG represents a potential non-Western acquirer of Sublime's IP.

Meanwhile, Brimstone Energy — which lost a larger DOE grant ($189M vs. Sublime's $87M) — is advancing: hiring a seasoned CFO, adding strategic investors (Kajima Ventures), expanding its product roadmap, and progressing Oklahoma plant site selection. Critically, Brimstone's cement holds ASTM C150 certification (the prescriptive standard required for federally funded infrastructure), while Sublime's cement meets only ASTM C1157 (a performance-based standard not accepted by AASHTO for DOT projects). This means Brimstone addresses ~100% of the U.S. cement market while Sublime addresses only ~60–65%.

HYPOTHESIS TO INVESTIGATE:
The simultaneous impairment of all three cement majors' primary green cement technology bets creates a once-in-a-cycle competitive opportunity. The first mover among CRH, Amrize, and Holcim to secure a strategic position in Brimstone captures the replacement asset at pre-competitive pricing. CRH is the most natural acquirer/investor (Ash Grove's geographic overlap, $250M ventures fund, DOT infrastructure revenue dependency requiring C150), but Amrize's convertible note structure allows it to walk away from Sublime cleanly and pivot. The 6-month window from Sublime's December 2025 pause to mid-2026 resolution is the highest-leverage entry period.
