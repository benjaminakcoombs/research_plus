## I. THE OPPORTUNITY

CRH and Holcim invested $75M in Sublime Systems in September 2024 — equity, binding offtake, and co-development rights for the next generation of green portland cement.^1 Sublime has since collapsed to approximately 28 employees, paused its Holyoke plant indefinitely, and replaced its founder-CEO.^2 Both cement majors' primary decarbonized cement technology bet is now effectively stranded. There is exactly one other company in the world that has achieved ASTM C150 certification for cement produced from non-limestone feedstock: Brimstone Energy, a pre-revenue Oakland startup with approximately 82 employees, $80M+ raised, and no cement-major investor on its cap table.^3 ^4 ^5

## II. COMPANY SITUATION

Brimstone Energy has invented a patented process — the Rock Refinery — that co-produces ASTM C150 portland cement, supplementary cementitious materials, and smelter-grade alumina simultaneously from calcium silicate rocks rather than limestone. Founded in 2019 at Caltech by CEO Cody Finke and CSO Hugo Leandri, the company operates bench and pilot-scale equipment at its Oakland headquarters and is engineering a demonstration plant near Reno, Nevada. It has not yet generated revenue, broken ground on a commercial facility, or produced product at commercial scale.^9 ^10

The economics are built on co-production: from a single mining and grinding operation, the process extracts calcium for cement, aluminum compounds for alumina, and silicate residue for SCM — using exclusively commercially available (TRL 9) equipment, which limits scale-up risk to process integration rather than unproven chemistry.^9 The cement product holds independent ASTM C150 certification and has been tested by Amazon's construction team.^3 Plant 1, sited at Dolese Bros.' Roosevelt quarry in southwest Oklahoma (selected from an evaluation of 23,000+ U.S. quarries), targets 80,000 t/yr of cement and 20,000 t/yr of alumina at a total cost of approximately $378–400M.^11 ^12 Dolese Bros. is one of the top 20 aggregate producers in the United States, with $450M+ in annual revenue and more than 60 facilities across two states — a substantial feedstock partner, not a speculative quarry operator.^37 At nameplate capacity and current market pricing, estimated annual revenue is approximately $22M — split roughly $11M cement, $10M alumina, and $1M SCM.^i

Ownership is private and founder-led. The board consists of only two members — both founders — an unusual governance structure for a company with $80M+ from institutional investors that typically demand board representation.^5 ^13 Breakthrough Energy Ventures and DCVC co-led the $55M Series A in March 2022; Amazon Climate Pledge Fund participated as an equity investor and later signed a conditional reservation agreement for annual cement volumes (August 2025).^14 ^15 Kajima Ventures — the CVC arm of Japan's largest construction conglomerate — entered the cap table in mid-2025, a strategic signal that the construction industry establishment is validating the technology and a potential indicator of an EPC relationship for the Oklahoma plant.^16 No Series B has been announced.

The capital structure underwent a seismic shift in 2025. In January, DOE finalized a $189M cooperative agreement, disbursing $8.7M for Phase 1.^17 In May, the Trump administration terminated the remaining ~$180M as part of a systematic rescission of Biden-era clean energy awards.^17 In July, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act repealed the IRA's LPO loan authority, closing what would have been the most viable alternative government financing path.^18 The entire ~$400M plant must now be financed privately — an estimated equity gap of approximately $200–225M after anticipated project finance debt.^vi Brimstone is appealing the DOE decision, framing its alumina output as a critical mineral aligned with Trump administration priorities, but no commercial OCED grantee whose award was terminated in this cycle has had funding restored as of this writing.^17 ^19

The strongest transaction signal is the October 2025 hire of CFO Tim Rens, who raised approximately $20B across his career including $750M for Monolith Materials' first-of-kind industrial plant through private project finance with J.P. Morgan and Goldman Sachs as placement agents.^20 CEO Finke is a confirmed speaker at CERAWeek 2026 (March 23–27) — the premier venue for institutional energy infrastructure capital formation.^21 The timeline has compressed further: Sublime's December 2025 collapse eliminated Brimstone's primary competitor; the EGA/Century JDA (January 2026) created 1.5M t/yr of in-state alumina demand; and the Amrize investor day (March 25) may signal Amrize's green cement strategy pivot after its Sublime investment entered distress.^2 ^6 ^22

Where the narrative diverges from reality: in December 2025, Brimstone announced the "Fourth Great Refinery" vision, expanding its product roadmap to include steel, magnesium, titanium, and other critical minerals — repositioning addressable market from $400B (cement) to $2.4T (all building materials).^9 None of these additional products have been demonstrated at any scale. The Reno demonstration plant was still "currently being engineered" as of that announcement.^9 And on the alumina product that anchors the entire critical minerals narrative, zero of the thirteen standard smelter-grade specification parameters have been publicly disclosed after six years of development — a gap with transformational valuation implications explored below.^23

## III. KEY FINDINGS

**Six Years and Zero Alumina Specs: A Single Lab Measurement Separates a $175M Cement Company From a $450M Critical Minerals Platform**

**The Observation.** Brimstone has claimed "smelter-grade alumina" in every press release since January 2025, yet has published none of the thirteen standard SGA specification parameters — no Al₂O₃ purity percentage, no Fe₂O₃ assay, no TiO₂ level, no crystal phase data, no BET surface area.^23 Its cement product, by contrast, holds independent ASTM C150 certification and has been tested by Amazon.^3 The geological literature on Brimstone's feedstock — the Glen Mountains Layered Complex — describes it explicitly as "Fe-rich, Ti-rich, and P-rich biotite-bearing gabbros."^24 Independent studies show ilmenite (the primary titanium mineral in this formation) dissolves 93–97% in hydrochloric acid under standard leaching conditions^25 — the leaching agent described in Brimstone's patent, which contains no dedicated titanium removal step.^26 Achieving SGA specification (TiO₂ ≤ 30 ppm) from a feedstock containing 1.5–4% TiO₂ would require a decontamination factor exceeding 500x — a purification level not demonstrated commercially from any acid-leach process.

**The Deal Implication.** SGA-grade alumina commands $400–500/t; chemical-grade commands $150–250/t — a revenue difference of approximately $5.6M per year at Plant 1 scale.^ii The "critical minerals platform" valuation framework justifying multiples of 12–18x EBITDA rests entirely on this claim. If the alumina is chemical-grade, the applicable framework reverts to green cement technology at 7–8x, compressing implied base case enterprise value from approximately $300M to approximately $175M — a ~$125M impact and approximately four multiple turns on the same underlying revenue.^iii This finding directly constrains Finding 3 below: the EGA alumina supply thesis is commercially compelling only if the alumina meets smelter specification. If it does not, the entire non-cement buyer universe narrows to chemical-grade alumina consumers at sharply lower valuations.

**Open Questions:**

- The entire SGA thesis reduces to one ICP-MS measurement: what is the dissolved titanium concentration in Brimstone's leachate when Glen Mountains rock is processed under their conditions? If below 100 ppb, the SGA pathway is open; if above 1 ppm, it requires purification steps not described in any public document. This $50 lab test has a $125M+ valuation implication — and after six years of development, has never been disclosed. Why?

- Has any aluminum smelter operator conducted qualification testing on Brimstone's alumina? Amazon tested the cement extensively — the complete absence of equivalent alumina customer testing disclosure is conspicuous and may indicate the product has not yet reached the specification threshold required for smelter engagement.

- The most benign explanation is deliberate information management — Brimstone has achieved SGA specification in unpublished pilot work and is withholding data as trade secret. If so, the Series B data room should contain the independent certificate of analysis. Any investor committing capital at critical-minerals multiples without this document is accepting unquantified technical risk on the product line that generates essentially all of the company's economic surplus.

**CRH and Holcim Just Lost Their Green Cement Bet — and the Only C150-Certified Replacement Has No Strategic Investor**

**The Observation.** Sublime Systems went from approximately 128 employees in September 2024 to approximately 28 by March 2026, with its Holyoke plant paused indefinitely and its founder-CEO replaced by a former COO in November 2025.^2 ^27 CRH holds equity (estimated ~$37.5M), binding offtake, and "priority site selection rights for full-scale plant development."^1 Amrize holds a convertible note and advance payments — structurally senior to all equity in any recovery.^28 Microsoft holds a binding agreement for 622,500 tons of Sublime cement over six to nine years — an offtake now attached to a company that cannot build a plant.^38 Notably, no bankruptcy filing has been made; all contractual restrictions remain in force.^2 Sublime produces an ASTM C1157-compliant material — a performance-based standard for which AASHTO has no equivalent — meaning it cannot be specified on federally funded DOT highway projects without years of specification changes across 50 state transportation agencies. Approximately 40–50% of U.S. cement demand is categorically inaccessible to C1157-only suppliers.^iii Brimstone is the only company in the world with C150 certification from a non-limestone process.^3

**The Deal Implication.** CRH, as the largest road builder in North America, cannot deploy Sublime's product across its DOT-governed revenue base. CRH Ventures retains approximately $130–170M in estimated remaining dry powder.^iv A $75–125M Series B anchor in Brimstone at $400–550M pre-money would secure approximately 12–20% ownership — mirroring the Sublime deal structure (equity plus binding offtake plus co-development rights) but for a C150-certified product that addresses 100% of the U.S. cement market.^iii If the alumina product resolves as chemical-grade rather than smelter-grade (see Finding 1 above), the cement-major thesis actually strengthens — the valuation would compress to cement-technology multiples, making a CRH or Amrize entry less expensive while the C150 moat remains fully intact.

**Open Questions:**

- Does CRH's Sublime agreement contain non-compete provisions covering competing green cement technologies? If so, CRH may need to structure its initial Brimstone position as a passive financial investment — deferring operational partnership until Sublime's formal dissolution releases restrictions. The legal status of Sublime — suspended but not dissolved — is the binding constraint on CRH's freedom to act.

- Amrize's investor day is March 25 — days away. If Amrize discloses impairment of its Sublime convertible note, it signals strategic freedom to pursue Brimstone independently. Amrize's FY2025 10-K, filed February 18, 2026, carried the Sublime position forward with no named impairment charge.^28 The question is whether Amrize has been strategically quiet pending a board-level decision that the AGM (April 21) will reveal.

- Siam Cement Group, an early Sublime investor, represents a wildcard: SCG could acquire Sublime's IP for adaptation in Southeast Asian markets where C1157 is more accessible. If SCG moves first, CRH and Amrize lose any residual option on the Sublime technology, compressing the timeline to secure Brimstone from "sometime in 2026" to "immediately."

**EGA Is Building a $4B Oklahoma Smelter With No Domestic Alumina Supply — Brimstone's Plant Is 200 Miles Away**

**The Observation.** EGA and Century Aluminum's JDA targets a 750,000 t/yr aluminum smelter at Inola, Oklahoma, part of a $200B White House-level U.S.-UAE commercial package, with Bechtel selected for preparatory engineering.^6 ^29 ^30 The smelter requires approximately 1.5M tonnes per year of alumina.^v The United States has one operating alumina refinery (Atalco/Gramercy, Louisiana), running at roughly one-third of its 1.2M t/yr capacity.^31 No alumina supply agreement for Inola has been publicly disclosed across three months of monitoring of EGA, Century, and project-level announcements.^23 ^6 Brimstone's Roosevelt quarry site is approximately 200 miles from Inola — a truck-delivered, tariff-immune, hurricane-immune domestic supply chain within a single state.^11 ^12 Century's Jamalco refinery in Jamaica — the most likely primary supply source — has experienced multiple operational disruptions in recent years, including equipment failure and Hurricane Beryl damage, establishing a structural weather risk that project finance lenders will require mitigation against.^39

**The Deal Implication.** Brimstone's 20,000 t/yr covers only 1.3% of Inola's requirement — negligible at Plant 1 scale.^v The strategic value is the option on scale: at ten plants, 200,000 t/yr supplies approximately 13% of the smelter's needs at an estimated delivered cost of $145–258/t versus $460–550/t for ocean-freighted imports.^i An equity stake of $50–100M — combined with an alumina offtake option — would be financially immaterial to the $5B+ Inola project but existentially important to Brimstone, filling 25–50% of the Plant 1 financing gap while adding an investment-grade credit to the capital stack.^vi Century Aluminum's corporate history — including its 55% stake in Jamalco, structured as a joint venture rather than full acquisition — suggests Century may be the more natural minority equity partner, while EGA's pattern of taking majority control or anchor customer positions makes it better suited as an offtake counterparty.^6 ^39 Critically, this finding is contingent on Finding 1: if the alumina product is chemical-grade rather than smelter-grade, EGA's technical team will not accept a supply agreement at any price, and the entire non-cement buyer universe for Brimstone narrows materially.

**Open Questions:**

- Has any commercial discussion occurred between Brimstone and EGA or Century? Brimstone's June 2025 press release used the phrase "mine to metal" in the same paragraph as the EGA reference, issued six weeks after EGA's original Oklahoma announcement — language consistent with active discussions under NDA, though it could be aspirational positioning.^12 The answer determines whether this is novel intelligence or already priced into the Series B.

- The Bechtel FEED process underway will lock in alumina receiving infrastructure design (barge-only versus truck-compatible) within 12–18 months. If Brimstone is not in the conversation before FEED concludes, it risks being physically designed out of the Inola supply chain permanently. This is an engineering deadline — earlier and harder than the financing timeline.

- Century Aluminum's $500M DOE grant for Inola was paused (not cancelled) under executive orders and originally scoped for a Kentucky site that Century has since abandoned.^32 Its transferability to Oklahoma is legally unresolved. If the grant is permanently lost, Century's capital constraints increase — making a cost-reducing domestic alumina partnership with Brimstone more financially compelling, while simultaneously validating the political channel Brimstone is pursuing for its own DOE appeal.
