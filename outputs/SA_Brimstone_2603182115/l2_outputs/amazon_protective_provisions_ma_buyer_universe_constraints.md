### 2.2 The Four Independent Blocking Positions — Complete Mapping

Amazon holds four independent contractual blocking positions, any of which is sufficient to prevent or materially complicate an acquisition:

**Blocking Position 1: Preferred Stock Merger Consent (Charter Level)**
Standard NVCA protective provisions require preferred stockholder consent for "any merger, other corporate reorganization, sale of control, or other transaction in which holders of a company's voting securities prior to [the transaction] own less than a majority of the voting securities after." The consent threshold is typically 50–66.7% of outstanding preferred shares on an as-converted basis. Amazon's ~6–10% preferred position means it cannot independently block a merger if other preferred holders consent — **but** Amazon's board seat gives it advance notice of any proposed transaction, allowing Amazon to build coalitions among preferred holders and negotiate conditions before the class vote.

**Critical Delaware Issue — "By Merger or Otherwise":**
As established in *Benchmark Capital Partners v. Vague* (Del. Ch. 2002), protective provisions that do not include "by merger or otherwise" language cannot prevent a triangular merger from eliminating preferred stock rights. Any acquirer's Delaware counsel must review Brimstone's certificate of incorporation for this exact language before structuring a transaction. If absent, a triangular merger structure could theoretically bypass the preferred class vote — though this would be reputationally toxic and likely trigger Amazon's commercial agreement termination rights as a second-line defense.

**Blocking Position 2: Commercial Reservation Agreement — Non-Assignment and Change-of-Control Termination**
The reservation agreement almost certainly contains: (a) a non-assignment clause requiring Amazon's prior written consent to any assignment (modeled on the Rivian EDV Agreement); and (b) a change-of-control termination right allowing Amazon to terminate the reservation agreement if Brimstone undergoes a change of control without Amazon's consent. Either provision independently blocks an acquirer from closing without Amazon's cooperation — because a closing without reservation agreement assignment means the acquirer acquires a company whose primary commercial customer relationship immediately terminates upon closing.

**Blocking Position 3: Manufacturing Location Consent (Commercial Agreement)**
The Rivian EDV Agreement required Amazon's prior written consent for any change in manufacturing location. A similar provision in Brimstone's reservation agreement would give Amazon effective veto power over any acquirer's plan to relocate or consolidate the Roosevelt Quarry/Oklahoma plant site. For a cement major who might prefer production at an existing facility, this consent right is operationally dispositive.

**Blocking Position 4: Testing Collaboration IP Rights**
The four-month testing collaboration, developed based on Amazon slab mix designs, likely created jointly-developed specifications and performance benchmarks. Any testing collaboration agreement would include IP ownership clauses, confidentiality restrictions, and potentially use restrictions — preventing an acquirer from commercializing the jointly-developed Amazon-slab-mix testing data for non-Amazon customers without consent.

### 2.3 The Drag-Along Mechanic — Can Amazon Be Overridden?

This is the most sophisticated legal question in the entire analysis, and the one most frequently underweighted by M&A practitioners:

Standard NVCA drag-along provisions allow a company to compel all stockholders to sell if: (a) the board approves the transaction; (b) a majority or supermajority of common stockholders approve; and (c) a majority or supermajority of preferred stockholders approve on an as-converted basis. If BEV (estimated 15–20% of cap table) + DCVC (estimated 10–15%) + Kajima (estimated 10–15%) collectively hold more than 50% of preferred shares, they could theoretically drag Amazon into a transaction Amazon opposes — provided the transaction meets charter-level minimum consideration requirements (typically, acquirer pays at least 1x liquidation preference to preferred holders).

**However, the drag-along does not override the commercial agreement blocking positions.** Even if Amazon is dragged into the equity transaction, the commercial reservation agreement's change-of-control termination right remains independently actionable — Amazon can terminate the reservation agreement even after being dragged along in the equity sale. An acquirer who relies on the drag-along to override Amazon's equity consent while ignoring the commercial agreement termination right acquires a company whose primary commercial relationship terminates at closing. This is a catastrophic outcome that eliminates the value driver for the entire acquisition.

**The correct legal analysis:** Amazon's blocking power cannot be circumvented through the drag-along because the commercial agreement — a separate, independent contract — provides a second-line defense that the drag-along mechanic cannot reach. The only path to a completed acquisition that preserves the Amazon commercial relationship is obtaining Amazon's affirmative consent to both the equity transaction and the commercial agreement assignment.

## PART VI: THE "DUAL CONSENT EVENT" — STRUCTURING ARCHITECTURE

### 6.1 The Mechanism

The "dual consent event" is the recommended structuring approach for any M&A or strategic investment transaction involving Brimstone. It bundles two simultaneous negotiations — Amazon's M&A consent and the conversion of the reservation agreement to a binding take-or-pay — into a single, interdependent transaction package. This structure:

1. **Converts an M&A liability into an enterprise value catalyst.** Amazon's consent, if obtained alongside a take-or-pay conversion, adds $35–45M net to enterprise value (see Part VII).
2. **Aligns Amazon's interests with the acquirer's interests.** Both parties want the conversion — Amazon for supply security, the acquirer for project finance bankability. This alignment eliminates the adversarial dynamic that would otherwise characterize a consent-only negotiation.
3. **Creates a "consent bounty" for Amazon.** Amazon agrees to consent to the M&A transaction and convert its reservation to binding take-or-pay in exchange for: (a) reaffirmation of its board observer rights in the acquiree; (b) supply priority protections (Amazon gets first right to X% of annual cement output); (c) pricing protections (Amazon's take-or-pay price is fixed or indexed at a pre-agreed level); and (d) technology development covenants (acquirer commits to maintain Brimstone's Rock Refinery development roadmap on schedule).

### 6.2 The Project Finance Nexus

The conversion of the reservation to a binding take-or-pay is the prerequisite to project finance debt for the Rock Refinery. Project finance lenders require binding long-term offtake covering at least 60–80% of production capacity. Amazon's 80,000 t/yr reservation represents 100% of the Rock Refinery's cement capacity. Even at 50% of the reservation volume (40,000 t/yr), a binding Amazon take-or-pay would represent 50% of cement production — sufficient to underwrite project finance debt.

This creates a cascading financial benefit: Amazon take-or-pay → project finance debt availability → lower equity requirement → higher equity IRR for the acquirer. The dual consent event is not merely a consent mechanism; it is the financial architecture that makes the Rock Refinery viable without the DOE grant.
