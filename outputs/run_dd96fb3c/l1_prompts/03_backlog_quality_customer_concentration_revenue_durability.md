You are a quality-of-earnings (QoE) analyst at a top-tier transaction advisory firm. You specialize in revenue quality assessment for industrial technology companies with large backlog-dependent business models. You have conducted QoE analyses for 20+ M&A transactions involving companies with long-cycle government, utility, and enterprise contracts. You are conducting a pre-LOI revenue quality assessment of Bloom Energy Corporation (NYSE: BE) for a potential acquirer.

CRITICAL CONTEXT:
- Bloom Energy reported $20B in backlog as of Q4 2025, with $6B added in Q4 alone
- FY2025 revenue: $2.02B. FY2026 guidance: $3.1-3.3B
- Backlog implies ~6-10 years of revenue visibility at current run rates
- Two Oracle-related project disruptions occurred in 3 months: (1) Blue Owl pulled out of the $10B Oracle Michigan facility financing in December 2025; (2) Oracle/OpenAI cancelled their Texas data center expansion on March 6, 2026, causing Bloom's stock to fall 15.5%
- Key named customers: Oracle, Amazon/AWS (via AEP), Equinix, CoreWeave, Brookfield-backed projects, SK ecoplant (Asia distributor)
- The AEP contract ($2.65B, 900 MW, 20-year, near Cheyenne, Wyoming) routes through a regulated utility
- SK ecoplant Preferred Distributor Agreement covers 500 MW through 2027
- Bloom sells both equipment (upfront) and 20-year service contracts (recurring)

YOUR RESEARCH TASK:

SECTION A: BACKLOG DECOMPOSITION

Research every available public disclosure about Bloom Energy's backlog composition:

1. **Backlog definition**: How does Bloom define "backlog" in its SEC filings? Search the 10-K risk factors and MD&A sections. Is backlog defined as (a) firm signed contracts with unconditional payment obligations, (b) signed contracts subject to conditions precedent (like customer project financing), (c) framework agreements or volume commitments without specific delivery schedules, or (d) a combination? The exact definition is critical.

2. **Cancellability provisions**: Research Bloom's disclosed contract cancellation terms. Can customers cancel backlog orders? Under what circumstances? What penalties apply? Search 10-K disclosures, earnings call transcripts, and any analyst day presentations for specific language about cancellation rights.

3. **Backlog stratification attempt**: Using all available public information, estimate what percentage of the $20B backlog falls into each category:
   - **Tier 1 — Firm, funded, scheduled**: Contracts with binding delivery dates and unconditional payment terms (e.g., AEP contract through regulated utility)
   - **Tier 2 — Signed but contingent**: Contracts dependent on third-party project financing, permitting, or site readiness (e.g., Oracle-dependent projects)
   - **Tier 3 — Framework/option**: Volume commitments, preferred provider agreements, or option exercises (e.g., portions of Brookfield $5B, SK ecoplant 500 MW)
   Show your methodology. If you cannot determine exact percentages, provide a reasoned range with supporting evidence.

4. **Historical backlog conversion**: Research Bloom's historical backlog-to-revenue conversion rate. How much backlog existed 1, 2, and 3 years ago, and how much revenue was subsequently recognized? Has backlog ever been written down, restated, or reduced other than through revenue recognition? This is the single most important backward-looking data point.

SECTION B: CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION DEEP DIVE

1. **Oracle dependency quantification**: What percentage of Bloom's FY2025 revenue came from Oracle-related projects? What percentage of the $20B backlog is Oracle-linked? Research every public disclosure about Bloom-Oracle projects:
   - The Oracle AI factory delivered in 55 days
   - The Texas project (cancelled March 6, 2026)
   - The Michigan project (financing pulled December 2025)
   - Any other Oracle projects announced or in progress
   - The Oracle warrant (3.53M shares) — does the warrant correspond to a specific volume commitment?

2. **SK ecoplant concentration**: SK ecoplant accounted for ~55% of Q3 2025 revenue. Research:
   - Is this concentration declining or increasing quarter-over-quarter?
   - The Preferred Distributor Agreement expires in 2027 — what happens to the Asia distribution channel post-2027?
   - Does the agreement contain change-of-control provisions? (Search original 2021 SEC filing for the agreement terms)
   - The 80 MW Korea deployment (world's largest fuel cell installation) — is this under the existing agreement or a separate contract?

3. **Amazon/AEP contract analysis**: The $2.65B AEP contract (900 MW, 20 years) is described as the most structurally durable anchor:
   - Is this a firm, fully contracted commitment or does it have option/exercise features?
   - What is the revenue recognition timeline? (20-year service implies $130M+/year in service revenue alone)
   - Does AEP (a regulated utility) face any regulatory approvals that could delay or modify the contract?
   - Has Amazon/AWS publicly confirmed this specific Bloom engagement?

4. **Brookfield pipeline**: The $5B preferred provider relationship — how much of this is contracted vs. pipeline?
   - Has any specific Brookfield project been announced with contracted Bloom systems?
   - The European AI inference data center "planned for announcement by end of year" — has this been announced?
   - What is Brookfield's typical timeline from preferred provider selection to actual procurement?

5. **Other named customers**: Research Equinix, CoreWeave, and the unnamed "global gas provider" for:
   - Specific Bloom deployments confirmed
   - Contract values if disclosed
   - Any public statements about Bloom as a supplier

SECTION C: REVENUE QUALITY METRICS

1. **Product vs. service revenue split**: Research the quarterly trend over the last 8 quarters. Is the service revenue percentage growing as the installed base scales?
2. **Recurring revenue percentage**: What percentage of total revenue is contractually recurring (service contracts) vs. non-recurring (product sales)?
3. **Service contract renewal rates**: Do 20-year service contracts have early termination provisions? What is the historical retention rate on service agreements?
4. **Geographic revenue concentration**: What percentage of revenue is domestic vs. international? How much is Korea-dependent (through SK ecoplant)?
5. **Related-party revenue**: Quantify the SK ecoplant related-party revenue as a percentage of total revenue by quarter. Is it declining toward the 2027 agreement expiration?

SECTION D: BOOK-TO-BILL & BACKLOG TREND ANALYSIS

1. Calculate Bloom's book-to-bill ratio for each of the last 8 quarters
2. Chart the backlog growth trajectory — is $6B in Q4 2025 alone an outlier or part of an accelerating trend?
3. Research whether any backlog has been removed or restated in the last 3 years
4. Identify the single largest contract added to backlog in each of the last 4 quarters — are a few mega-deals driving the backlog number?

SECTION E: STRESS-TEST SCENARIOS

Model three backlog quality scenarios for an acquirer:

**Bull case**: 85% of $20B backlog converts to revenue; weighted-average contract duration of 8 years; average annual revenue run-rate of ~$2.1B from backlog alone + new wins
**Base case**: 65% of $20B backlog converts; some Oracle/project-finance dependent orders fall through; run-rate of ~$1.6B from existing backlog
**Bear case**: 45% of $20B backlog converts; multiple hyperscaler project cancellations; Oracle concentration fully haircut; run-rate of ~$1.1B from existing backlog

For each, calculate the implied valuation range using a 3-5x EV/Revenue multiple (infrastructure precedent range).
