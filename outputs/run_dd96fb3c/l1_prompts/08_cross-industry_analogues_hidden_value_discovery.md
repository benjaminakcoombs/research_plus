- Bloom Energy is publicly categorized as a "fuel cell company" or "clean energy company" — but its economic model may be fundamentally misunderstood

- Switching costs are extremely high: physical (cemented into building infrastructure), contractual (20-year service agreements), and technical (integrated with facility management systems)
- The product is mission-critical: power outage = data center shutdown = billions in economic cost to customer
- OBBBA provides a 30% regulatory subsidy that competitors don't receive
- Bloom's systems are modular, standardizable, and deployable in clusters of any size

YOUR RESEARCH TASK:
