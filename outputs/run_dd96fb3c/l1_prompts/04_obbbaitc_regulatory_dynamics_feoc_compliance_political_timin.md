You are a regulatory and tax policy specialist who advises energy companies and their acquirers on investment tax credit (ITC) structures, FEOC (Foreign Entity of Concern) compliance, and the interaction between tax policy and M&A valuations. You have deep expertise in the Inflation Reduction Act, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), and Section 48/48E clean energy tax credits. You are preparing a regulatory risk assessment for a potential acquirer of Bloom Energy Corporation.

CRITICAL CONTEXT:
- The OBBBA (signed July 4, 2025) provides a 30% ITC for "fuel cell property" under Section 48E beginning construction after December 31, 2025, regardless of emissions levels
- The OBBBA simultaneously reduced or eliminated ITC eligibility for wind and solar projects
- FEOC rules effective January 1, 2026 prohibit ITC eligibility for components sourced from "covered nations" (primarily China)
- Bloom Energy manufactures solid-oxide fuel cells using ceramic electrolyte materials, rare-earth elements, and specialty metal interconnects
- Bloom's primary manufacturing facility is in Newark, Delaware (200,000 SF)
- Bloom has international operations in 9 countries, including South Korea (SK ecoplant partnership)
- 46% of publicly announced data center builds are in EPA non-attainment zones, creating air permitting risk for gas-fed generation
- Bloom's fuel cells run primarily on natural gas, with hydrogen and biogas capability

YOUR RESEARCH TASK:

SECTION A: OBBBA TAX CREDIT ANALYSIS — BLOOM-SPECIFIC

1. Research the EXACT statutory text of the OBBBA's Section 48E provisions as they apply to fuel cells:
   - What is the definition of "fuel cell property" under the new law?
   - Is there a capacity threshold (minimum kW) for eligibility?
   - Does the 30% rate apply to both the fuel cell hardware AND installation/balance-of-plant costs?
   - Are 20-year service contracts eligible for any separate tax treatment?
   - Is the credit transferable (Section 6418) — can Bloom or its customers sell the credit to a tax equity investor?

2. Calculate the financial impact of the 30% ITC on Bloom's unit economics:
   - Bloom Energy Servers reportedly cost $7,000-10,000 per kW installed
   - For a 10 MW installation (~$70-100M), the 30% ITC = $21-30M credit
   - How does this change the payback period for a data center customer?
   - Show the comparison: Bloom with 30% ITC vs. comparable diesel generator, gas turbine, and grid-connected solar + battery

3. Research how the OBBBA affected the competitive landscape:
   - Which specific clean energy technologies LOST their ITC or PTC eligibility under OBBBA?
   - Which competitors are disadvantaged? (Specifically: solar developers, wind developers, battery storage manufacturers)
   - Has any sell-side analyst quantified the competitive advantage the OBBBA creates for Bloom specifically?

SECTION B: FEOC COMPLIANCE — THE EXISTENTIAL DILIGENCE QUESTION

1. Research Bloom Energy's supply chain for FEOC exposure:
   - Solid-oxide fuel cells require yttria-stabilized zirconia (YSZ) ceramic electrolytes, lanthanum strontium manganite (LSM) cathodes, and nickel-based cermet anodes
   - Where are these ceramic and rare-earth materials primarily sourced globally? What percentage of global supply comes from China?
   - Has Bloom disclosed its supply chain compliance status for FEOC purposes in any SEC filing, earnings call, or investor presentation?
   - Search specifically for: Bloom Energy 10-K supply chain disclosures, risk factor language about "foreign entity of concern," FEOC, or "covered nation" sourcing

2. Research the FEOC compliance requirements:
   - What is the exact standard for FEOC compliance under the current Treasury/IRS guidance?
   - Is it a component-level test (any single component from a covered nation voids eligibility) or a percentage-of-value test?
   - What documentation does a manufacturer need to maintain?
   - Is there a self-certification process or does the IRS audit compliance?

3. Assess the risk:
   - If Bloom's ceramic stack components are sourced from FEOC-covered entities, what is the business impact? (30% ITC voided = effective 30% cost increase for customers = potential order cancellations or renegotiations)
   - Are there non-Chinese sources for YSZ ceramics, LSM, and nickel cermet at commercial scale?
   - How long would it take Bloom to re-source its supply chain if FEOC non-compliance is discovered?
   - Has any competitor (FuelCell Energy, Plug Power, GE Vernova) publicly confirmed FEOC compliance?
