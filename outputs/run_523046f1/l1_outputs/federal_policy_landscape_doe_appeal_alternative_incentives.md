## NOTABLE ANOMALIES — FINAL CONSOLIDATED

### Anomaly 1: The Century Aluminum Contradiction Is Now Confirmed and Is Brimstone's Strongest Political Asset

This is no longer a theoretical policy argument — it is a documented operational fact. The Trump administration is actively funding a $500M OCED grant for the first new U.S. aluminum smelter since 1980, sited in Oklahoma, while having cancelled a $189M OCED grant for the only viable domestic alumina production project, also sited in Oklahoma. 
The U.S. has just four operating aluminum plants with a combined output of 683,500 metric tons per year — while in 2024 the U.S. imported 5.46 million metric tons of aluminum.
 Building more smelters without domestic alumina supply does not solve the import dependency problem — it merely shifts the import from aluminum metal to alumina oxide. This argument should be the lead in every congressional, executive, and media communication Brimstone makes.

### Anomaly 2: The Section 232 Alumina Inclusions Process Is a Regulatory Petition That No One Has Filed Yet


BIS publicized the Section 232 Inclusions Process on April 30, 2025, with submissions accepted in two-week windows three times per year.
 This process allows any U.S. stakeholder to petition for the addition of new products to Section 232 coverage. A petition to include alumina (HTS 2818.10) — framed around China's dominance of global alumina refining, the supply chain vulnerability of the U.S. aluminum industry, and the national security case for domestic alumina production — could be filed in the next available two-week window with no Congressional action, no new legislation, and no executive order. If approved, it would impose a 50% tariff on all imported alumina, creating an immediate and durable competitive advantage for Brimstone's production. No public record exists of this petition having been filed by Brimstone or any other alumina stakeholder. This is likely the most underutilized regulatory tool available to Brimstone.

### Anomaly 3: The DIBC Has Already Deployed $777M in FY2025 — The Program Is Real and Moving


The DPA Purchases Office has made 14 investments totaling $777.1 million since the beginning of fiscal year 2025 through the DIBC OTA mechanism.
 The program is not theoretical — it has deployed more than four times the value of Brimstone's cancelled OCED grant in a single fiscal year. Award sizes confirmed in the public record range from $7M to $400M. Brimstone's $100M+ project is firmly within the program's historical range. The germanium ($18.1M), gallium, cobalt ($7M), and solid rocket motor ($53.9M) precedents confirm that the DIBC awards critical minerals projects of varying sizes rapidly, without FAR procurement overhead. Brimstone must join the DIBC and submit to the current RPP basket — this is a zero-cost, immediate action with potential upside of $100M+.

### Anomaly 4: The §45X Transferability Deadline Creates an Existential Structuring Problem That Must Be Solved Now

The transferability of §45X credits closes December 31, 2027. Brimstone's plant is targeting operations "before 2030." This means Brimstone will almost certainly be in the post-transferability period when it begins production — making it impossible to monetize 45X credits through the standard tax equity transfer mechanism. 
Century Aluminum is actively monetizing 45X credits — it received a $75 million "45X check" for FY2024 and has a $173 million receivable tied to full-year 2023 and 2025 U.S. production.
 Century can do this because it is already operational. Brimstone cannot replicate this monetization path under its current timeline. The solution is to (a) aggressively seek to bring forward the plant's commercial operations date; (b) structure a partnership with a tax equity investor at the entity level (not through credit transfer); or (c) lobby for an extension of the §45X transferability window in the 119th Congress — a straightforward technical fix that could be added to a tax extenders package.
