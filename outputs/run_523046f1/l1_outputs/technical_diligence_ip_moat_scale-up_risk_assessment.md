## SECTION 1: PROCESS CHEMISTRY — FINDINGS AND VALIDATION

### 1.1 Leaching Chemistry: What the Patents Reveal

The process is a **two-acid hydrometallurgical system with reagent regeneration**, jointly filed August 13, 2020 by the California Institute of Technology and Brimstone Energy Inc., naming Cody Finke and Hugo Leandri as inventors.

The first reacting step comprises reacting the calcium-bearing starting material with hydrochloric acid to form at least aqueous calcium chloride, aqueous aluminum chloride, and solid silica. The second reacting step comprises reacting the aqueous calcium chloride and aluminum chloride with sulfuric acid to form solid calcium sulfate, aqueous aluminum sulfate, and — critically — regenerated hydrochloric acid. The thermally treating step then heats calcium sulfate to produce calcium oxide (cement precursor).

For the alumina pathway, high HCl concentration causes AlCl₃ to precipitate from solution. AlCl₃ is then reacted with H₂SO₄ to make Al₂(SO₄)₃ and regenerate HCl; the Al₂(SO₄)₃ is then thermally decomposed to produce Al₂O₃ and SO₂, which is recycled to regenerate sulfuric acid.

For the iron pathway, the patents disclose that after Ca, Al, and Si removal, only aqueous iron sulfate and magnesium sulfate remain. At 500–700°C, iron sulfate decomposes into insoluble iron oxide, and the remaining magnesium sulfate can be dissolved away in water.

**Assessment:** The chemistry is thermodynamically sound. HCl dissolution of silicate minerals is well-established, and HCl regeneration via sulfate-swap has industrial precedent in rare earth processing. The defensible novelty is the **integration** of: leaching conditions, AlCl₃ salting-out, CaSO₄ thermal decomposition to clinker quality, and simultaneous SCM production from silica residue. Integration details (specific acid concentrations, temperatures, residence times, L/S ratios) are intentionally not fully disclosed.

### 1.2 Patent Portfolio

The core PCT application (WO2021030529A1, priority August 13, 2020) has extensive international coverage: Canada, Japan, Brazil, EPO (Europe), Mexico, China, South Korea, and Australia — sophisticated for a company at this stage.

A second key filing (US20230036470A1) covers "Cementitious material production from non-limestone material" with a larger inventor team. 
Brimstone Energy has filed 6 patents.


**Grant status:** Core US application US11718558B2 appears granted; continuation applications through 2024 are pending.

**Claim scope and prior art risk:** The fundamental claim structure covers: reacting calcium-bearing starting material with a first acid; reacting with a second acid to produce a solid second calcium salt while regenerating the first acid; and thermally treating to produce cement. The HCl leaching of calcium minerals and H₂SO₄ swap for HCl regeneration via gypsum crystallization are documented in prior hydrometallurgical literature (Demopoulos et al., ScienceDirect, 2008) independent of Brimstone. A well-resourced competitor could challenge the leaching and acid-regeneration steps; Brimstone's most defensible claims are the downstream clinker production from CaSO₄ calcinate and the specific co-production integration, which are novel.

### 1.3 Peer-Reviewed Research

CEO Cody Finke's published peer-reviewed work is in clean hydrogen production economics (Energy & Environmental Science, 2021, conducted at LBNL, JCAP, and Caltech) — not cement chemistry or mineral processing. No peer-reviewed publications on the calcium silicate leaching process, clinker formation from CaSO₄ calcinate, or alumina extraction from silicate rocks have been identified. The technology is being validated commercially rather than academically — a standard but risk-elevated path. ### 5.2 Feedrock Elemental Budget and Critical Analysis

Basalt feedrock composition (typical): 45–52% SiO₂, 14–18% Al₂O₃, 5–15% Fe₂O₃, 5–12% MgO, ~10% CaO, ~2% TiO₂.

**Steel (from iron):** The patent discloses iron oxide recovery via iron sulfate decomposition at 500–700°C. Iron oxide ≠ steel. Steel requires reduction (blast furnace or H₂-DRI) and alloying — separate capital-intensive unit operations not in any announced plant design. **Magnesium:** 
The process generates magnesium compounds that permanently absorb CO₂ from the air, making it deeply decarbonized across a wide range of energy scenarios and geographies.
 Diverting MgO to metallic magnesium production would require Pidgeon process or electrolytic cells and would potentially eliminate the carbon-negative credential. **Titanium:** At ~2% TiO₂ in basalt, the rock-to-product ratio at 100% recovery is ~50:1 — economically marginal even before selectivity losses against the silicate matrix. Current Kroll process requires high-grade rutile (>95% TiO₂). No evidence of any titanium-containing product at any scale has been disclosed.

**Assessment:** The "Fourth Great Refinery" vision is a long-duration strategic narrative with zero disclosed pilot-scale evidence for steel, magnesium metal, or titanium. Adding these streams to a plant designed for OPC + alumina + SCM would require transformative additional capital — separate reduction furnaces, electrolytic cells, or Kroll reactors. The capital cost implications are unquantified and should not be included in any base-case investment model.

Enhanced weathering rates for magnesium silicates depend strongly on particle size, surface area, moisture, and temperature. 
If the new process runs on renewable energy, the result is zero-carbon cement. If it uses the mix of energy on the grid, the emissions reduction could still be as much as 75%.
 The range itself reveals that the carbon accounting is energy-scenario-dependent and requires rigorous third-party validation. California SB 596 (net-zero cement by 2045) and voluntary carbon markets will require exactly this level of rigor before premiums can be captured.

**ANOMALY 8 — Patent Portfolio Prior Art Vulnerability [MEDIUM RISK]**

The HCl leaching of calcium-bearing minerals and H₂SO₄ swap for HCl regeneration via gypsum crystallization are documented in prior hydrometallurgical literature (Demopoulos et al., 2008) independent of Brimstone. A well-resourced competitor — an incumbent cement major (Holcim, Heidelberg, CEMEX) or mining company (Rio Tinto, BHP) — could challenge the leaching and acid-regeneration steps. Brimstone's most defensible claims are in the downstream CaSO₄-to-clinker integration and the specific co-production economics, not the leaching chemistry per se. The most effective protection is the undisclosed process integration details held as trade secrets. 
Brimstone selected Dolese Bros. Co. following a competitive selection process that evaluated over 23,000 quarries across the United States
 — the feedrock selection process itself constitutes proprietary know-how that complements the patent estate.

## BOTTOM LINE FOR STRATEGIC INVESTOR

Brimstone is a high-conviction technology investment with specific, manageable, and well-defined risks. The core chemistry is real; the ASTM C150 cement is real; the co-production economic logic is sound; the management team has demonstrated execution discipline; and the strategic alignment of a Brimstone alumina plant, Dolese feedrock supply, and EGA's $4 billion Oklahoma aluminum smelter creates a compelling integrated supply chain narrative that no other cement decarbonization company can replicate.

The capital structure risk is materially less acute than initially assessed: the DOE grant was back-loaded, the company has preserved operating capital, and the CFO hire signals an active capital raise in progress.

The three non-negotiable due diligence conditions before capital commitment are:

1. **Alumina SGA validation:** Independent assay results or a named smelter trial — ideally with EGA — confirming Fe₂O₃ < 0.02% and SiO₂ < 0.02% in pilot-scale product. This is the make-or-break technical fact.

2. **Reno pilot throughput and performance data:** The actual t/day cement equivalent operating at Reno, acid consumption, reagent loss rates, and solid-liquid separation performance. This allows calculation of the Oklahoma scale-up factor and the probability of first-time-right commissioning.

The EGA-Brimstone-Dolese triangle in Oklahoma is the most powerful potential project finance structure available to the company and should be formalized as a priority.

If these three data points are favorable, Brimstone represents a genuinely differentiated investment at the intersection of decarbonization, domestic alumina supply security, and the emerging U.S. aluminum supply chain — with a strategic moat (ASTM C150 + alumina co-production) that no current competitor can replicate.
