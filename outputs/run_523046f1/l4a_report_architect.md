## DELIVERABLE 1: STYLE GUIDE

**Issued to all section writers. Deviations require editorial approval.**

---

### Voice & Tone

Write as a senior practitioner briefing a peer — someone who runs processes and advises boards. The voice is confident, direct, and analytical. Every sentence earns its place by either establishing a fact, drawing a conclusion, or identifying a gap. There is no warming up, no throat-clearing, no "it is interesting to note." State conclusions first; then provide evidence.

**Target tone — example sentences:**

- "Brimstone's alumina carries 44% of Plant 1 revenue at smelter-grade pricing. After six years of development and $80M+ raised, no independent specification data supports the smelter-grade designation."
- "CRH has the strategic logic, the geographic proximity, and the balance sheet capacity. It does not have the legal freedom to act until Sublime formally dissolves or the offtake agreement is explicitly released."
- "The co-production cost advantage — $200–400/t below imported alumina on a delivered basis — is structural, embedded in the process architecture rather than contingent on any individual subsidy or tariff."

Do not sound breathless, promotional, or academic. Do not qualify findings you have evidence for. Do not present analysis as discovery ("we found that...") — present it as fact ("the data shows" or simply state the finding directly). Never address the reader in second person.

### Citation Format

Numbered superscript footnotes (^1, ^2...) within the text. Each major section (I through VI) ends with a **Source Notes** block listing all citations for that section. Format by source type:

- **SEC filings:** Amrize Industries, Form 10-Q, Q3 2025, filed with SEC.
- **Press releases:** Brimstone Energy, BusinessWire, "Brimstone Signs LOI with Dolese Bros. Co.," June 30, 2025.
- **Government records:** DOE Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations, award announcement, March 2024; Oklahoma Corporation Commission, PSO Green Country acquisition proceedings, Cause No. PUD 202400137.
- **Court/regulatory filings:** BIS, Section 232 Inclusions Process Final Rule, 90 FR 22534, April 30, 2025.
- **Trade press/journalism:** Bloomberg, "Sublime Systems Reduces Workforce, Pauses Holyoke Plant," March 12, 2026.
- **Estimates/inferred figures:** Mark with "[Pokee estimate]" or "[inferred from publicly available data]" and state the methodology parenthetically.

### Uncertainty Language

Three tiers only:

| Confidence | Language | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| High (≥80%) | "Evidence indicates..." / "The data confirms..." / state directly as fact | Multiple independent sources corroborate; no material counter-evidence |
| Medium (50–80%) | "Analysis suggests..." / "The pattern is consistent with..." | Single strong source or logical inference from confirmed facts |
| Low (25–50%) | "Preliminary indicators point to..." / "If confirmed, this would..." | Inferred from indirect evidence; plausible but unverified |

**Never use:** "we believe," "it is possible," "may or could" (when evidence exists), "it seems," "one might argue." When evidence is absent, say so directly: "No data exists in the public record to confirm or deny this."

### Financial Figures

- Show ranges with stated assumptions: "$7.6–9.2M EBITDA [at 35–42% blended margin]"
- Show methodology for all estimates on first use
- Format: "$X–YM" (millions), "$X–YB" (billions). Round appropriately — never "$1,247.3M"; use "$1.2–1.3B"
- Discount rates, growth assumptions, and margin assumptions stated explicitly on first use in each section

### Structure Patterns

- Every section and subsection opens with its conclusion — the reader should know the finding before seeing the evidence
- Subsection headers are descriptive claims, not labels ("The Alumina Revenue Stream Has Zero Independent Validation" — not "Alumina Analysis")
- No bullet-point lists in narrative sections (I, II, III, V, VI). Bullet lists acceptable in structured/data sections (IV, VIII) only
- Tables appropriate in IV and VIII; sparing elsewhere (one per section maximum in I–III, V–VI, and only when the data requires tabular presentation)

### Cross-References

Use descriptive references: "as detailed in the Valuation Architecture section" or "the Sublime competitive dynamics analyzed in the Tension Point Analysis." Never "see Section IV," "see page 12," or "see above."

### What to Avoid

- Generic industry observations that could apply to any cement company
- Wikipedia-level company descriptions (assume the reader knows what cement is)
- Unsourced claims presented as facts
- Promotional language about the report or its producers (Sections I–VI)
- Any reference to internal research pipeline terminology: L0, L1, L2, L3, tension point numbers (TP1, TP7), agent names, or research rounds
- Any mention of AI, automation, or machine learning in Sections I–VI
- Rhetorical questions, exclamation points, or emphasis formatting (bold/italic) used for persuasion rather than structural clarity
