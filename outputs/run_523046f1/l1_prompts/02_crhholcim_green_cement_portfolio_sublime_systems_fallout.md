COMPANY CONTEXT: Sublime Systems, a Somerville, MA-based electrochemical cement startup, announced in December 2025 that it would pause construction on its $150M low-carbon cement plant in Holyoke, Massachusetts, and cut its workforce by 10%. In 2024, CRH and Holcim had jointly invested $75M in Sublime (approximately $37.5M each) with binding offtake reservations and commitments to co-develop future "true-zero" plants. Sublime produces ASTM C1157 cement (performance specification), not ASTM C150 (prescriptive specification). Sublime had raised over $200M total.

Separately, Brimstone Energy — a competitor producing ASTM C150 portland cement from silicate rocks — is advancing its own commercial plant in Oklahoma and has Amazon as both investor and anchor customer. Brimstone's DOE grant was also cancelled, but it has not paused operations. The question is: how quickly and urgently will CRH and Holcim need to redirect their green cement strategic investments, and is Brimstone the most likely beneficiary?
