## I. Executive Situation Assessment

Brimstone Energy holds the only ASTM C150 certification for portland cement produced from non-limestone feedstock — independently verified by Twining Consulting and tested against Amazon's proprietary slab mix designs.^1 It has an Amazon commercial reservation, a confirmed ~$400M Oklahoma plant site with feedstock secured via letter of intent with Dolese Bros. Co., and a management team assembled to close an industrial-scale capital raise.^2 The cement is third-party validated. The alumina — projected at 44% of Plant 1 revenue at smelter-grade pricing — has zero independently validated specification data across any of the thirteen standard SGA parameters after six years of development and $80M+ raised.^3 No aluminum producer has signed any agreement for the alumina. Whether it qualifies as smelter-grade ($450–520/t) or only chemical-grade ($150–250/t) is a ~$5M/year revenue difference at Plant 1 and a 2–3x enterprise value swing between "critical minerals platform" and "green cement startup."^4 That single unresolved question governs an implied valuation range of approximately $80M to $285M — a span wide enough to place Brimstone in fundamentally different buyer categories, transaction structures, and strategic narratives depending on the answer.

This report maps the company's strategic situation across four dimensions, identifies the four findings of highest magnitude, and presents the valuation architecture, buyer universe, and risk framework required to act within the transaction window now opening. A partner who reads only these pages should be able to brief a client and sound like they have spent three weeks inside the company.

### A Pre-Revenue Company with One Validated Product and One Unverified Revenue Stream

Brimstone Energy, Inc. is a pre-revenue, venture-backed industrial technology company founded in 2019 at Caltech. Its patented Rock Refinery® process co-produces ASTM C150 portland cement, supplementary cementitious materials, and smelter-grade alumina from calcium silicate rocks rather than limestone — eliminating the ~60% of conventional cement CO₂ emissions chemically unavoidable from limestone decomposition.^5 The company operates bench/pilot-scale equipment in Oakland, California, is engineering a demonstration plant near Reno, Nevada, and holds a feedstock LOI with Dolese Bros. Co. for the Roosevelt quarry near Lawton, Oklahoma — selected from over 23,000 U.S. quarries screened.^6

Plant 1 is designed for 80,000 t/yr of cement and 20,000 t/yr of alumina at a total capital cost of ~$378–400M, with target production before 2030.^7 At base-case market pricing, annual revenue is estimated at $21.8M: $11.2M from cement (80,000 t × $140/t), $9.6M from alumina (20,000 t × $480/t), and ~$1.0M from SCM (15,000 t × $65/t).^8 At a 35% blended margin benchmarked to Iluka Resources (mineral sands co-production, 42% FY2024 margin) and Eagle Materials (cement/wallboard, 30% FY2025), projected EBITDA is $7.6M.^9 Revenue quality carries significant concentration risk: Amazon is the sole announced commercial customer, holding a conditional, non-binding reservation of cement and SCM volumes signed August 2025.^10

The cement validation pathway is complete — independent lab certification, customer testing, commercial reservation. The alumina validation pathway has not begun. Brimstone has published none of the thirteen standard SGA parameters: no Al₂O₃ purity, no Fe₂O₃ assay, no TiO₂ level, no crystal-phase XRD, no BET surface area.^11 The feedstock geology compounds the concern: the Glen Mountains Layered Complex underlying the Roosevelt quarry is characterized in peer-reviewed geological literature as dominated by "Fe-rich, Ti-rich gabbro," and published hydrometallurgical studies show ilmenite dissolves 93–97% under standard HCl leaching, yet the Brimstone patent describes no dedicated titanium removal step.^12 A second technical risk layers onto the first: the process calcines aluminum compounds via a sulfate precursor pathway, which at standard temperatures produces η-Al₂O₃ — not the γ/θ-Al₂O₃ mixture required for Hall-Héroult smelter cells.^13 The full technical evidence is presented in the Tension Point Analysis section on product risk and competitive position.

### Management's Narrative: "The Fourth Great Refinery" and a $2.4 Trillion Addressable Market

In December 2025, Brimstone announced an expanded product roadmap: steel, magnesium, titanium, and other critical minerals from the same calcium silicate feedstock, widening management's stated addressable market from $400B to $2.4T.^14 The "Fourth Great Refinery" positioning — explicitly analogizing to Freeport-McMoRan (copper/byproduct) and Exxon (petroleum refining) — reframes the company from a green cement startup into a foundational industrial platform.

The narrative gap is material. None of the newly announced co-products have been demonstrated at any scale. Plant 1 produces only cement, alumina, and SCM.^15 The co-production cost allocation — which management claims yields marginal alumina extraction costs of $125–225/t versus $560–585/t for standalone Bayer process economics — has not been independently validated.^16 The redesign of Plant 1 from 140,000 t/yr cement (March 2024 DOE filing) to 80,000 t/yr cement plus 20,000 t/yr alumina (January 2025) reflects the strategic pivot toward higher-value critical minerals framing, but also concentrates revenue risk in an unverified product stream.^17 A buyer paying a platform multiple needs to distinguish demonstrated chemistry from announced roadmap. The co-production architecture and its valuation implications are examined in the Tension Point Analysis section on transaction mechanics and regulatory optionality.

### A Competitive Landscape That Has Shifted Decisively

Three external developments have materially altered Brimstone's strategic position in the twelve months preceding this analysis.

Sublime Systems — the only other next-generation green cement technology with institutional backing and cement-major strategic investment — has effectively imploded. Workforce reduced from 128 to approximately 28 employees between December 2025 and March 2026; its $150M Holyoke, Massachusetts demonstration plant paused indefinitely.^18 CRH holds equity of approximately $37.5M with binding offtake reservations and "priority site selection rights for full-scale plant development in key markets."^19 Amrize (formerly Holcim U.S.) holds a convertible note and advance payments — not equity — per SEC Form 10-Q disclosure.^20 Both cement majors' green OPC technology bets are impaired. Brimstone is the sole remaining ASTM C150-certified, operationally advancing alternative. Critically, Sublime's cement meets only ASTM C1157 — a performance-based standard that addresses approximately 20–30% of U.S. cement demand — while Brimstone's C150 certification addresses 100% of the market, including the ~35–40% that is DOT-specified and requires no specification changes for adoption.^21

EGA and Century Aluminum signed a Joint Development Agreement in January 2026 to build a 750,000 t/yr aluminum smelter at Inola, Oklahoma — the first new U.S. primary aluminum smelter since 1980 — requiring approximately 1.5M t/yr of alumina with no supply agreement publicly disclosed.^22 The smelter's certainty is independently confirmed: Public Service Company of Oklahoma has committed over $3.2B in power infrastructure to serve it, including acquiring the 795 MW Green Country gas-fired plant and approving nearly 1 GW of renewable generation.^23 A regulated utility does not make $3B+ in capital commitments for a project that might not be built. Brimstone's Roosevelt quarry is 180–220 miles from Inola by truck — a domestic, tariff-immune, intrastate supply chain.

The federal funding landscape has undergone a structural reversal. The DOE OCED grant ($189M selected March 2024, $8.7M disbursed January 2025) was cancelled in May 2025; an administrative appeal is active.^24 The One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed July 4, 2025, repealed the Inflation Reduction Act's loan authority for DOE Loan Programs Office programs, permanently closing what would have been the most viable alternative government debt pathway for Plant 1.^25 Simultaneously, Century Aluminum's $500M OCED grant for the Inola smelter has been maintained — the Trump administration is funding the demand for domestic alumina while cancelling the supply.^26 That policy incoherence, paradoxically, is among Brimstone's strongest political assets and opens alternative DOD financing pathways analyzed in the Tension Point Analysis section on demand architecture and capital formation. Against this backdrop, three cement majors — CRH, Amrize, and Heidelberg Materials (whose $500M Mitchell, Indiana CCUS grant was also cancelled) — need a replacement technology bet with no government-funded alternatives remaining.^27

### A Management Team Hired to Close, and a Capital Structure Forced to Act

The most consequential recent developments are internal signals of transaction preparation against a capital structure that has narrowed to private markets alone.

Tim Rens joined as CFO in October 2025.^28 Career: CFO at CVR Energy (NYSE), Philadelphia Energy Solutions, and Monolith Materials, where he raised approximately $750M for a first-of-kind industrial plant including a $300M placement with J.P. Morgan and Goldman Sachs as agents.^29 He has raised nearly $20B across his career and departed Monolith during its cash crisis following DOE loan cancellation — an exact structural parallel to Brimstone's current position. VP Engineering John Uhrie (December 2025) brings experience deploying billion-dollar plants for complex metal sulfides; Senior Advisor Kevin Kramer, a former Alcoa/ATI executive, provides a warm channel to the aluminum sector.^30 Hugo Leandri's title change from CTO to CSO signals organizational maturation from R&D to commercialization.^31

Kajima Ventures — the CVC arm of Japan's largest general contractor (~$15B revenue, A+/R&I credit rating) — appeared in Brimstone's investor list for the first time in October 2025, absent from all prior disclosures, indicating a quiet intermediate investment with potential EPC and creditworthy-sponsor implications.^32 The company stated in September 2025 that it "has investors in place to help it continue building."^33 Crunchbase's predictive model assigns 76% probability of a funding round within six months.^34 No SEC Form D has been filed as of March 2026. The Series A ($55M, March 2022) is the last confirmed priced round — now four years old, making a Series B structurally overdue by any venture lifecycle standard.^35

With the DOE grant cancelled and the LPO pathway legislatively eliminated, the entire ~$400M plant must be privately financed.^36 The financing menu has narrowed to: a large Series B equity raise, strategic co-investment, project finance debt with a creditworthy sponsor guarantee, or acquisition. The bifurcated entity structure — Brimstone Energy, Inc. (parent) and Brimstone Commercial, LLC (project vehicle) — is transaction-ready architecture.^37 The management team's composition, banking relationships, and public engagement pattern are consistent with a company preparing for a $100–200M+ industrial capital raise. ### Four Findings That Govern the Transaction

This report identifies four findings of highest magnitude. Each is substantiated in the sections that follow.

**The alumina specification gap is the single most important diligence question and governs the entire valuation range.** At Plant 1, the revenue difference between smelter-grade alumina pricing (~$480/t) and chemical-grade pricing (~$200/t) on 20,000 t/yr is approximately $5M annually — $49M in NPV over 20 years at 8% (20,000 t × ~$250/t differential × 9.818 annuity factor).^38 More consequentially, the product classification determines the valuation multiple: a critical minerals platform producing domestic SGA commands 12–15x EBITDA; a green cement startup with a chemical-grade byproduct commands 7–8x. That 2–3x enterprise value difference defines the implied range — from approximately $80–120M (floor: ASTM C150 IP plus Amazon reservation plus 7x cement-only EBITDA of $4.1M) through ~$230M (base: Plant 1 EBITDA at SGA of $7.6M at 12x, plus C150 moat premium of $35–65M, plus EGA demand optionality of $25–50M) to ~$285M (bull: critical minerals platform validated at 15x blended EBITDA plus scale-up option value).^39 A single certified alumina assay — approximately $50–150K in laboratory costs — would collapse this range. The technical evidence, including feedstock geology, crystal-phase analysis, and decontamination factor calculations, is presented in the Tension Point Analysis section on product risk and competitive position. The valuation methodology and sensitivity analysis are detailed in the Valuation Architecture section.

**The Sublime collapse creates a once-in-a-cycle replacement-asset window for CRH and Amrize, with a competitive clock measured in months.** CRH invested approximately $37.5M in Sublime with binding offtake and co-development rights; those contractual provisions — including potential non-compete language covering C150-compliant green cement technology — remain legally in force until Sublime formally dissolves or the agreement is released.^40 CRH cannot execute an operational partnership with Brimstone without breach risk. Amrize, by contrast, holds a convertible note and can exit Sublime cleanly — its principal recovers ahead of equity in any wind-down.^41 CRH is the strategically optimal investor — $250M ventures fund with an estimated $130–170M in dry powder, Ash Grove's 12 U.S. cement plants in the South-Central corridor adjacent to Oklahoma, and the $2.1B Eco Material precedent — but the most legally constrained.^42 Any competing buyer that tables a term sheet before dissolution and before Brimstone's Series B close captures the replacement-asset premium at pre-competitive pricing. The buyer dynamics, CRH entry structures, and Amrize investor day catalyst are analyzed in the Buyer Universe and Transaction Pathways section.

**The EGA/Inola smelter creates a 1.05M tonne domestic alumina deficit 180 miles from Brimstone's plant — with no supply agreement disclosed and infrastructure design locking within months.** The Inola smelter requires approximately 1.5M t/yr of alumina. The United States has one operational alumina refinery (Atalco, Gramercy, Louisiana) running at roughly one-third capacity (~400K t/yr actual output), creating a structural domestic deficit of approximately 1.05M t/yr.^43 At Brimstone's co-production cost ($125–225/t) versus imported alumina landed in Oklahoma (~$460–500/t), the annual margin on 20,000 t/yr is $5–7.5M; at 10-plant scale (200,000 t/yr), the NPV of the cost advantage is approximately $589M over 20 years at 8% (200,000 t × ~$300/t average advantage × 9.818 annuity factor).^44 The near-term urgency is infrastructure: Bechtel's FEED study is underway and will lock alumina receiving design within 6–12 months; if a truck-delivery pathway is not specified during FEED, barge-only design may permanently exclude Brimstone's co-located supply.^45 The full supply chain analysis is presented in the Tension Point Analysis section on demand architecture and capital formation.

**The entire ~$400M plant must be privately financed, and the management team hired to close it is already in place.** The DOE OCED grant is cancelled. The OBBBA's repeal of IRA loan authority closed the DOE LPO pathway permanently.^46 The DOD's Office of Strategic Capital offers loans at Treasury rates with up to 50-year tenor and 80% LTV — the most favorable available terms — but requires a creditworthy sponsor guarantee that Brimstone cannot provide alone.^47 The MP Materials precedent ($400M DOD equity plus $150M OSC loan, July 2025) establishes the template, but the sequencing is determinative: the best government financing requires the best private partner first.^48 The CFO's confirmed banking relationships (J.P. Morgan, Goldman Sachs) and PE network (TPG Rise Climate, Warburg Pincus, Decarbonization Partners) define the likely Series B syndicate. The capital structure constraints and government financing alternatives are detailed in the Company Operating Profile and the Tension Point Analysis section on capital formation.

### The Clock: Four Converging Timelines

The transaction window is set by four converging deadlines. The Series B — structurally overdue at four years since the last priced round — is the primary near-term event; the pattern of management hires, narrative repositioning, and investor engagement is consistent with a formal process launching in Q2–Q3 2026.^49 Amrize's investor day on March 25, 2026 is the next hard catalyst: any signal that Amrize is impairing its Sublime position or pivoting its clean cement strategy accelerates competitive pressure on CRH to act.^50 CERAWeek 2026 (March 23–27) — where CEO Finke holds a confirmed speaking slot — is the premier venue for institutional energy infrastructure investor engagement and the most likely forum for initial strategic conversations.^51 The Bechtel FEED design lock for EGA's Inola smelter, expected within 6–12 months, is the infrastructure deadline: once alumina receiving design is finalized without a domestic truck-delivery pathway, Brimstone's supply thesis narrows from co-located domestic production to competition with barge-delivered imports.^52 A strategic investor who resolves the alumina specification question before the Series B close enters at pre-commercial pricing with a quantified risk-reward across the full $80–285M range. The valuation architecture, buyer universe, transaction pathways, and risk framework that follow are designed to equip that investor — or the advisor positioning them — with the analytical foundation to act within this window.
