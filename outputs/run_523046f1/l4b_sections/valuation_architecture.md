## IV. VALUATION ARCHITECTURE

**Brimstone is pre-revenue; all EBITDA figures referenced in this section are projected at Plant 1 nameplate capacity and have not been earned.** The implied enterprise value range — $80M to $285M before tariff sensitivity — is governed almost entirely by a single variable: whether the alumina co-product qualifies as smelter-grade ($450–520/t) or only chemical-grade ($150–250/t). A conventional single-multiple valuation applied to total projected EBITDA misprices this company in every direction — understating the technology IP and domestic supply franchise at the floor, and masking commodity manufacturing risk at the ceiling. The framework presented here decomposes Brimstone into three distinct assets, each requiring its own valuation lens, comparable set, and risk profile.^1 The partner who marks up these assumptions with diligence findings from the alumina specification analysis will arrive at a defensible range within a single working session.

---

### A Single EBITDA Multiple Systematically Misprices This Company

Brimstone should be valued as three separable assets, not as a single operating business assigned one blended multiple.

**Asset 1: Process Technology IP.** The ASTM C150 certification for cement produced from non-limestone feedstock is unique globally — no other company has achieved it. The patent portfolio (six filed patents across nine international jurisdictions, jointly held with Caltech) protects the hydrometallurgical process architecture, and the proprietary feedstock database (23,000+ quarries screened) creates a selection advantage no competitor can replicate without years of geological work.^2 This asset exists independent of whether Plant 1 is built. Its value rests on defensibility — ASTM C150 certification requires multi-year third-party testing, and no competing non-limestone process has entered the pipeline — and on option value: the right to deploy the process at multiple plants, in multiple geographies, producing multiple co-products. The appropriate valuation lens is technology licensor or process IP platform, not commodity manufacturing facility.

**Asset 2: Domestic Alumina Supply Franchise.** Brimstone is the only announced domestic non-bauxite alumina source in the United States, located 180–220 miles by truck from the EGA/Century smelter at Inola, Oklahoma — the first new U.S. primary aluminum smelter since 1980.^3 The structural alumina deficit created by the Inola smelter (approximately 1.05M tonnes/year against effectively zero domestic non-bauxite capacity) makes this franchise a strategic infrastructure asset. The appropriate valuation lens is supply security premium with a national defense overlay, comparable to how the Department of Defense valued MP Materials' rare earth franchise through $400M in equity and a $150M OSC loan.^4 The franchise value depends entirely on alumina grade: if smelter-grade, the strategic premium is substantial; if chemical-grade only, the franchise value collapses. As detailed in the alumina specification analysis in the Tension Point Analysis, this determination requires approximately $50–150K in independent laboratory testing.

**Asset 3: Oklahoma Plant (Commodity Manufacturing at Maturity).** At nameplate capacity, Plant 1 produces 80,000 t/yr of cement and 20,000 t/yr of alumina at an estimated cost of $378–400M.^5 At maturity, this is a commodity manufacturing facility valued on EBITDA, capacity utilization, and replacement cost — the conventional industrial lens. Projected Plant 1 revenue at base-case market pricing: $21.8M (cement at 80,000 t × $140/t = $11.2M; alumina at 20,000 t × $480/t = $9.6M; SCM at ~15,000 t × $65/t = ~$1.0M) at 35% blended margin = $7.6M EBITDA.^6 These figures match the revenue model detailed in the Company Operating Profile; the 35% blended margin is benchmarked to Iluka Resources (mineral sands co-production, 42% FY2024 margin) and Eagle Materials (cement/wallboard, ~30% FY2025 margin), adjusted downward for first-of-kind execution risk.

The logic for the sum-of-parts framework is structural. A cement major (CRH, Amrize) values Asset 3 primarily and pays a modest premium for Asset 1. The Department of Defense values Asset 2 and is indifferent to Asset 3's cement economics. A critical minerals strategic (EGA, Century Aluminum) values Asset 2 and Asset 1's multi-plant option, with Asset 3 as a financing mechanism. Applying a single multiple to total EBITDA conflates these buyer lenses and produces a number that is wrong for every potential counterparty. The Buyer Universe and Transaction Pathways section maps each named buyer to the asset combination that drives their bid.

**Section 232 Tariff Upside: Bull Case Extension to $320–350M**

If alumina is added to Section 232 tariff coverage through the BIS Inclusions Process — a mechanism requiring no Congressional action, with filing windows three times per year — a 50% tariff on imported alumina would raise landed import costs from ~$460–500/t to approximately $690–750/t.^16 The domestic price floor would reset to approximately $550–650/t.

At $600/t domestic alumina pricing: Plant 1 EBITDA rises from $7.6M to $9.1M. At the Bull case 15x multiple, the EBITDA-driven component increases from $114M to $136.5M. The domestic alumina supply franchise premium also expands — a tariff-protected, tariff-immune domestic source becomes dramatically more valuable when the import alternative is $690–750/t. Under this scenario, total implied EV extends to **$320–350M**.^17

As analyzed in the regulatory optionality treatment in the Tension Point Analysis, no stakeholder has filed this petition across any BIS submission window despite the Supreme Court's ruling confirming Section 232 as the sole durable tariff authority.^18 The next filing window is approximately April 2026. The petition costs essentially nothing to file. A coalition petition — Aluminum Association, Century Aluminum, EGA — carries substantially more weight than a single-company submission. The tariff scenario is presented as a separate overlay because it represents a regulatory outcome, not a company-level execution milestone. An investor pricing the base case at ~$230M should view the $320–350M tariff-enabled range as upside optionality with an identifiable catalyst and a defined timeline.
