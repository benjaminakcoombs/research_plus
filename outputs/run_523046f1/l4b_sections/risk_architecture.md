## VI. Risk Architecture

This analysis rests on a specific thesis: Brimstone occupies a unique position at the intersection of validated cement technology, unverified alumina economics, and a once-in-a-cycle competitive window created by Sublime's collapse and the EGA/Century smelter build. The section that follows identifies the eight specific conditions under which that thesis breaks, the timing proves wrong, or the underlying evidence base is insufficient to support a commitment. These are not disclaimers. Each risk carries a defined monitoring trigger and a decision implication — what an advisory team would do differently if the risk materialized.

---

### Thesis Risks: What Breaks the Core Argument

**The alumina product fails to meet smelter-grade specification.** This is the single highest-consequence risk in the entire assessment. As documented in the product risk analysis, Brimstone has published zero of the thirteen standard SGA parameters after six years of development and $80M+ raised.^1 The patent describes a sulfate precursor pathway that produces η-Al₂O₃ at standard calcination temperatures — not the γ/θ-Al₂O₃ mixture required for Hall-Héroult cell performance.^2 The Roosevelt quarry feedstock contains titanium-rich and iron-rich gabbro, requiring decontamination factors of 100–667x to reach SGA thresholds of TiO₂ <30 ppm and Fe₂O₃ <200 ppm.^3 If the alumina cannot meet SGA specification, the "critical minerals platform" thesis collapses to "green cement startup." The valuation impact is a 2–3x compression: from the base case of ~$230M (which prices SGA alumina at $480/t and applies a 12x blended EBITDA multiple) to $80–120M (cement-only EBITDA of $4.1M at a 7x multiple plus residual technology option value).^4 Plant 1 revenue falls from $21.8M to ~$16.2M — a 26% decline driven entirely by the alumina product classification, as detailed in the Company Operating Profile.^5 The decision implication is binary: if SGA specification fails, any investment predicated on the critical minerals narrative must be restructured as a pure cement technology bet at a cement-sector multiple, and the EGA/Century demand thesis becomes irrelevant. **Monitoring trigger:** Any independent laboratory Certificate of Analysis, XRD phase identification, or smelter trial result — positive or negative — disclosed by Brimstone, a research partner, or a smelter operator. A diligence program to resolve this costs approximately $50–150K and requires access to pilot-produced material.^6

**EGA locks in an imported alumina supply contract before engaging Brimstone.** The domestic alumina supply thesis, analyzed in the demand architecture section, depends on Brimstone entering the EGA/Century procurement conversation before infrastructure design decisions become irreversible. Bechtel's FEED study for the Inola smelter is underway; alumina receiving, storage, and delivery infrastructure design locks within 6–12 months.^7 If the FEED specifies barge-only alumina receiving (consistent with Oklahoma's Energy Secretary's public statement that alumina will arrive "by barge"),^8 a truck-delivery pathway from the Roosevelt quarry 180–220 miles south may be permanently excluded from the plant's physical configuration. More critically, a 10-year imported alumina supply agreement — standard bankability collateral for the $5B multi-tranche debt financing EGA is organizing^9 — would satisfy lender requirements and eliminate the commercial rationale for a domestic supply partnership at Plant 1 scale (20,000 t/yr, or 1.3% of the smelter's 1.5M t/yr requirement). The structural cost advantage of co-produced alumina ($200–400/t below imported landed cost, as modeled in the co-production valuation analysis) would remain theoretically valid but commercially academic without a counterparty. The decision implication: if EGA signs an imported alumina contract, the advisory thesis shifts from "alumina supply platform with cement co-production" to "cement technology company with potential long-term alumina optionality" — a materially less compelling strategic narrative for non-cement buyers. **Monitoring trigger:** Any EGA or Century Aluminum alumina supply agreement announcement, or any Bechtel FEED milestone disclosure specifying alumina logistics infrastructure. A secondary indicator: if EGA's $5B debt financing closes without any reference to domestic alumina sourcing in the bankability package, the window has effectively closed.

**Sublime Systems revives.** The replacement-asset premium that drives the competitive window analysis depends on Sublime remaining incapacitated. At approximately 28 employees with Holyoke paused indefinitely,^10 Sublime is currently non-operational. However, its IP remains intact, its Microsoft offtake (622,500 MT over 6–9 years) is still nominally in force, and its investor base includes CRH (~$37.5M equity), Holcim/Amrize (convertible note), and SCG.^11 An emergency recapitalization of $100M+ — led by CRH exercising its existing equity position, or by a consortium including SCG acquiring the technology for Asian deployment — would restore a competitive alternative and eliminate the scarcity premium embedded in Brimstone's valuation. The probability is assessed as low (Sublime's CEO has publicly described the DOE cancellation as creating challenges that "forced consideration of alternative approaches,"^12 and Massachusetts planning officials see no plant being built "in the immediate future"^13), but the consequence is high. The decision implication: if Sublime secures new capital, the urgency argument for CRH and Amrize to pivot to Brimstone evaporates, the competitive window extends indefinitely, and the "6-month clock" described in the buyer universe analysis resets. Any advisory engagement predicated on Sublime's failure as a catalyst should be restructured around Brimstone's standalone merits. **Monitoring trigger:** A Sublime bankruptcy filing would be positive for the thesis (confirming irreversibility). A new funding announcement, board appointment of a turnaround executive, or SCG acquisition of Sublime IP would be negative. Massachusetts Secretary of State UCC filings and Delaware Chancery Court records should be monitored weekly.

### Execution Risks: What Makes the Timing Wrong

**The Reno demonstration plant delays propagate through the entire timeline.** The pilot-to-commercial scale-up gap is already the highest-consequence execution risk in Brimstone's development path. The Reno demo plant was "currently being engineered" as of December 2025 — not under construction.^14 The scale-up factor from Reno pilot to Oklahoma commercial production may be 40–80x in a single step, against industry best practice that calls for an intermediate demonstration at 10,000–30,000 t/yr before committing $400M.^15 A 12-month delay in Reno construction commencement propagates directly to Oklahoma FID: the Reno plant must produce operating data (throughput, reagent consumption, product quality at continuous run) before any institutional-grade financing source — Series B investor, project finance lender, or government program — will commit capital. If Reno construction does not begin by mid-2026, the "before 2030" Oklahoma production target becomes infeasible under any realistic construction timeline, and the Amazon reservation agreement's commercial relevance erodes (Amazon's expanded testing program runs through 2025–2026,^16 but a binding contract requires a credible production date). The decision implication: any engagement timeline modeled on a 2026 Series B close and 2028–2029 construction start should carry a 12–18-month contingency buffer. An advisory team should not build a process timeline around announced milestones without independent verification of Reno engineering progress. **Monitoring trigger:** A Reno construction commencement announcement, building permit filing with Washoe County, or any Brimstone disclosure of a general contractor or EPC engagement for the Reno facility. Absence of such an announcement by Q3 2026 would be a negative signal.

**The DOE appeal succeeds — and the strategic investment window closes.** This is the counterintuitive risk. Brimstone is actively appealing the $189M OCED grant cancellation, with a decision expected mid-2026.^17 If restored, the grant would fill approximately 47% of the Plant 1 financing gap with non-dilutive capital — an unambiguously positive outcome for the company. It is a potentially negative outcome for strategic investors. With $189M in government funding restored, the immediate financing urgency that makes Brimstone receptive to a cement-major co-investment at favorable terms ($75–125M at $450–550M pre-money, as modeled in the buyer universe analysis) disappears. The company could proceed with a financial-only Series B led by existing investors (BEV, DCVC) at a higher valuation, deferring strategic partnership until Plant 1 is operational — when leverage shifts decisively to the company. The government grant also reduces the rationale for a creditworthy sponsor guarantee, weakening the structural argument for Kajima EPC involvement or a DOD Office of Strategic Capital application.^18 The decision implication: a strategic investor seeking maximum leverage should engage before the appeal decision. If the grant is restored, the terms of any strategic investment worsen materially — the company trades from a position of strength rather than necessity, and financial investors (TPG Rise Climate, Warburg Pincus, Decarbonization Partners) can outbid cement majors without offering commercial value-add. An advisory team should model both appeal outcomes and structure any indicative proposal to be compelling independent of the DOE decision. **Monitoring trigger:** DOE OCED appeal decision, expected mid-2026. No commercial OCED grantee has won an appeal to date,^19 but the critical minerals policy reframing and Congressional support (Tom Cole, chair of House Appropriations, represents Brimstone's Oklahoma district)^20 create non-zero restoration probability.

### Information Risks: What Desk Research Cannot Resolve

**Founder governance provisions may constitute a structural veto on any transaction.** Brimstone's board has only two confirmed members — co-founders Cody Finke and Hugo Leandri.^21 For a 2019-vintage company with $80M+ raised from institutional investors including Breakthrough Energy Ventures and DCVC — both of which typically require board representation — a two-person founder board is either a data error or reflects extraordinary founder control provisions. If the founders hold dual-class super-voting stock (a common structure in deep-tech startups with Caltech origins), no transaction of any kind — minority investment, majority acquisition, or asset sale — is possible without affirmative founder consent, regardless of strategic logic, valuation premium, or investor pressure. BEV's board seat (Cooper Rinzler, confirmed)^22 may carry protective provisions including change-of-control consent, but these would supplement rather than override founder voting control. The decision implication is procedural: any advisory engagement must begin with a direct founder conversation to assess alignment before investing meaningful process resources. The governance structure cannot be resolved through desk research, SEC filings, or intermediary channels. No public record — Delaware charter filings, Form D disclosures, or investor databases — contains the voting provisions. **Monitoring trigger:** None available through public sources. Resolution requires data room access or direct management engagement. Any new board appointment (particularly an independent director or investor representative) would signal governance normalization and improved transaction feasibility.

**Amazon's specific protective provisions are unknown and could block or enable specific buyers.** As analyzed in the transaction mechanics section, Amazon occupies four simultaneous roles — equity investor, commercial customer, testing partner, and SCoBA founding member — each generating independent contractual rights.^23 The specific ROFR provisions, change-of-control consent requirements, and assignability clauses in both the Amazon Climate Pledge Fund investment agreement and the commercial reservation agreement are not publicly available. Modeling from the Rivian S-1 ACPF template suggests change-of-control consent rights with estimated 80%+ probability, a 14.9% voting cap, and non-assignment clauses.^24 But the actual terms could be more restrictive (full blocking right on any M&A) or more permissive (consent required only above a specified ownership threshold). The decision implication varies by buyer: a cement major acquisition likely triggers Amazon's competitive concerns and requires affirmative consent; an aluminum-sector buyer (EGA/Century) likely faces no Amazon conflict; a financial sponsor faces a neutral consent dynamic. The most elegant resolution — Amazon converting its reservation to a binding take-or-pay as a condition of consent — would actually improve project finance bankability.^25 But without the actual agreement text, no buyer can model the consent timeline or conditionality with precision. **Monitoring trigger:** Any Amazon-related SEC filing, amended investor rights agreement, or Brimstone Form D that lists Amazon as a participant with a disclosed investment amount would provide indirect evidence of the provisions' scope. A Rivian or other ACPF portfolio company's amended S-1 or 10-K disclosing updated ACPF terms would provide a cross-reference template.

**The alumina information asymmetry is consistent with both technical failure and deliberate commercial strategy — and the distinction is existential for valuation.** Brimstone's cement is independently certified to ASTM C150 by Twining Consulting and tested by Amazon's construction team with published results.^26 Its alumina — projected at 44% of Plant 1 revenue at smelter-grade pricing — has no published specification data, no independent laboratory certification, no confirmed smelter trial, and no aluminum-sector customer engagement after six years of development.^27 This asymmetry admits two interpretations. Under the technical barrier hypothesis, the sulfate precursor chemistry and Roosevelt quarry mineralogy create genuine constraints on achievable alumina purity and crystal phase — constraints that Brimstone has not yet overcome and is managing through selective disclosure. Under the trade secret hypothesis, Brimstone has achieved acceptable SGA specification at pilot scale but is deliberately withholding publication pending patent prosecution, commercial plant construction, or strategic partnership negotiation — a rational information management strategy for a company holding proprietary process knowledge. The evidence base is consistent with either interpretation, and the valuation gap between them is the full range of the assessment: approximately $80M (technical failure, cement-only) to $285M (trade secret, full platform with validated alumina).^28 The decision implication: this ambiguity cannot be resolved without data room access. Any investment committee presentation must present both scenarios with explicit probability weighting. An advisory team should frame alumina specification verification as the single gating diligence item — the one test result that collapses the valuation range from $205M wide to a defensible point estimate. **Monitoring trigger:** Any Brimstone disclosure of alumina purity data (Al₂O₃ percentage, Fe₂O₃, TiO₂, SiO₂), XRD crystal phase identification, BET surface area measurement, or smelter trial partnership. Any aluminum industry conference presentation by Brimstone personnel. Any patent continuation filing that discloses alumina physical property claims not present in the original PCT application.
