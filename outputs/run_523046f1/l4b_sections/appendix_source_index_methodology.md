### Analytical Methodology

**Research scope.** This Situation Assessment was produced exclusively from publicly available sources. No proprietary databases, insider information, management interviews, data room access, or non-public financial data were used at any stage. The research corpus encompassed approximately 200+ discrete sources spanning SEC filings, DOE and DOD program databases, state regulatory proceedings (Oklahoma Corporation Commission), geological survey publications, U.S. patent filings, hydrometallurgical and materials science literature, trade press and financial journalism, corporate press releases, and commercial investor databases (PitchBook, Tracxn, Crunchbase, CB Insights, FactSet). All sources were accessed through standard public channels between January and March 2026. Where source availability was limited or access was restricted behind paywalls, the limitation is noted in the relevant section.

**Analytical structure.** The report was produced through a parallel structured research framework — simultaneous investigation across six distinct dimensions: operational (process architecture, plant specifications, product certification), financial (capital structure, revenue modeling, valuation architecture), technical (feedstock mineralogy, alumina specification chemistry, crystal phase analysis), competitive (Sublime Systems trajectory, cement major positioning, EGA/Century supply chain), regulatory (DOE/DOD program terms, Section 232 tariff mechanics, §45X eligibility, OBBBA legislative impact), and transactional (buyer universe, Amazon consent dynamics, Series B timing, government financing pathways). Findings were included in the final report only after cross-validation across multiple source types — a claim supported by a single source is identified as such with the appropriate confidence designation. Where independent sources corroborated a finding, it is stated as fact. Where only a single source or logical inference supports a conclusion, the evidentiary basis is disclosed.

**Confidence framework.** All findings carry an explicit confidence designation based on a three-tier system. High confidence (stated directly or as "evidence indicates") applies where multiple independent sources corroborate the finding with no material counter-evidence. Medium confidence ("analysis suggests" or "the pattern is consistent with") applies where a single strong source supports the conclusion or where logical inference from confirmed facts is required. Low confidence ("preliminary indicators point to" or "if confirmed, this would") applies where the finding rests on indirect evidence that is plausible but unverified. Information gaps — areas where no public data exists to confirm or deny a hypothesis — are identified explicitly throughout the report and consolidated in the Risk Architecture section as monitoring triggers. This framework is designed to ensure that a reader can distinguish between what is known, what is inferred, and what remains unknown, and can allocate diligence resources accordingly.
