## V. Buyer Universe & Transaction Pathways

Eight identifiable counterparties across three strategic categories have specific, actionable rationale to engage Brimstone within the next 12–24 months. The convergence of three forces — Sublime Systems' operational collapse reducing the cement majors' green OPC technology portfolio to a single viable option, the EGA/Century Oklahoma smelter creating 1.5M tonnes per year of unsecured alumina demand 180 miles from Brimstone's plant, and the legislative closure of DOE loan pathways forcing the entire ~$400M plant into private capital markets — has created a buyer environment that is both broader and more motivated than at any prior point in the company's history. Each counterparty faces a distinct structural constraint — legal, regulatory, geographic, or strategic — that shapes not only whether it engages but through which instrument and on what timeline. Amazon Climate Pledge Fund's probable change-of-control consent rights, analyzed in the Tension Point Analysis, function as the primary structural filter on the entire buyer universe: they favor aluminum-sector and financial investors over cement major acquirers, and they make a minority Series B investment categorically easier to execute than a full change-of-control acquisition.

### Cement Majors Hold the Strategic Logic but Face Structural Constraints

**CRH plc** is the strategically optimal cement-sector counterparty and the most legally constrained in the near term. CRH's $2.1B acquisition of Eco Material Technologies in September 2025 established both a valuation benchmark and a demonstrated appetite for cement-adjacent technology businesses at industrial scale.^1 Its Ash Grove subsidiary operates 12 cement plants across the U.S. and Canada, including operations in the South-Central corridor that overlap geographically with Brimstone's Oklahoma plant site.^2 CRH Ventures maintains a $250M fund for early-stage industrial technology investments, and the parent company generates approximately $5B in annual free cash flow — making a $75–125M Series B commitment financially immaterial.^3

The constraint is legal, not strategic. CRH holds equity in Sublime Systems (estimated ~$37.5M as part of the $75M joint investment with Holcim) with a binding offtake reservation and co-development commitments that remain contractually in force despite Sublime's reduction to approximately 28 employees and its indefinite Holyoke plant pause.^4 As detailed in the Tension Point Analysis, analysis suggests CRH cannot execute an operational partnership with Brimstone — which competes directly with Sublime in green OPC — without triggering breach risk. These provisions likely survive until Sublime formally dissolves, completes a licensing transaction, or contractually releases CRH from exclusivity.

The optimal entry structure circumvents this constraint: a $75–125M passive, financial-only preferred equity investment through CRH Ventures at $450–550M pre-money valuation, yielding 12–20% ownership on a fully diluted basis with a board observer seat but no operational governance rights, co-development commitments, or offtake obligations.^5 [Pokee estimate: at $75M invested on $550M pre-money, post-money ownership is 12.0%; at $125M on $450M pre-money, post-money ownership is 21.7%.] The preferred equity carries an automatic upgrade to full strategic partnership — including offtake, co-development rights, and board seat conversion — upon Sublime's formal dissolution or contractual release. A passive financial investment without operational coordination does not constitute a competing commercial partnership and therefore does not trigger the breach risk a direct offtake arrangement would create. The $450–550M pre-money range reflects a strategic premium to the fundamental analytical range developed in the Valuation Architecture section, consistent with competitive Series B dynamics and the replacement-asset urgency created by Sublime's distress. Amazon consent probability for a passive CRH financial investment: high — a minority preferred position without governance authority does not constitute a change-of-control event under standard ACPF architecture.^6

**Amrize Industries** (NYSE: AMRZ) offers the cleanest competitive alternative to CRH among the cement majors. Spun off from Holcim in June 2025 with an approximate $30B market capitalization, Amrize is a newly independent NYSE-listed company with U.S.-dollar acquisition currency and an explicit mandate to grow low-carbon product lines to over 50% of net sales.^7 Its Sublime position is structurally distinct from CRH's: Amrize's Form 10-Q discloses "a convertible note and advance payments for future supplies" — debt instruments, not equity.^8 As a creditor rather than a shareholder, Amrize holds priority over equity in any Sublime wind-down and faces no governance entanglement preventing a pivot to Brimstone. The creditor-versus-equity distinction, analyzed in the Tension Point Analysis, means Amrize can recover its principal and redirect its green cement technology mandate without the legal complexity CRH faces.

The complication is geographic cannibalization. Amrize's Dallas-Fort Worth conventional cement expansion directly competes with Brimstone's Oklahoma addressable market.^9 Amrize's board would need to address whether investing in a green OPC producer 200 miles from a new conventional capacity expansion constitutes self-competing capital allocation. This internal tension makes CRH — which has no conflicting conventional capacity expansion in the same geography — the more natural long-term Brimstone strategic partner despite its near-term legal constraints. Amrize's March 25, 2026 investor day is the immediate signal: any disclosure regarding clean cement technology strategy post-Sublime would confirm or deny engagement intent.^10 Amazon consent probability: medium for an acquisition (similar competitive dynamic to CRH), high for a minority investment.

**Heidelberg Materials** (XETRA: HEI) would engage as a defensive buyer to prevent CRH or Amrize from locking up exclusive access to the only ASTM C150-certified green cement technology. Heidelberg has demonstrated U.S. acquisition appetite through its $600M Giant Cement acquisition in November 2024, and its own $500M DOE CCUS grant for the Mitchell, Indiana plant was cancelled — leaving its decarbonization portfolio underinvested.^11 Heidelberg's strategic commitment to CCUS (evoZero) is directionally different from Brimstone's feedstock substitution approach, making a full acquisition unlikely absent a first-mover threat from CRH or Amrize. The competitive dynamic identified in the Tension Point Analysis — where Siam Cement Group's potential acquisition of Sublime's IP would strand all three cement majors' green technology investments — creates the urgency that could force Heidelberg from observer to participant. Probability of independent engagement: low. Probability conditional on CRH or Amrize moving first: medium.

### The Aluminum Supply Chain Offers the Cleanest Strategic Alignment and Amazon Consent Path

**Emirates Global Aluminium / Mubadala Investment Company** represent the highest-strategic-value counterparty for the alumina thesis that carries 44% of Plant 1 projected revenue. EGA is building a $4–5B aluminum smelter at Inola, Oklahoma, requiring approximately 1.5M tonnes per year of alumina — with no publicly disclosed supply agreement.^12 The structural alumina deficit and the Bechtel FEED design-lock timeline are detailed in the Demand Architecture analysis. EGA is actively seeking equity partners for the Inola project, and Mubadala (EGA's 50% shareholder) has demonstrated appetite for upstream supply chain investments in critical minerals.^13

EGA's preference is almost certainly offtake over equity. Brimstone's 20,000 t/yr output satisfies only 1.3% of Inola's alumina requirement — meaningful as a domestic sourcing proof of concept but insufficient as a primary supply contract. An equity co-investment of $50–100M combined with an alumina offtake option would anchor Brimstone's strategic narrative while remaining financially immaterial to EGA's $5B capital stack. CFIUS constraints are mitigated by the U.S.-UAE bilateral investment framework and EGA's existing $4–5B Oklahoma commitment, but any equity stake in a critical mineral producer above a 10% threshold would trigger a mandatory filing — the structure should be calibrated accordingly.^14 Amazon consent probability: high. An aluminum-sector investor creates no competitive conflict with Amazon's procurement interests and strengthens the domestic supply chain narrative Amazon has publicly supported through SCoBA.

**Century Aluminum Company** (NASDAQ: CENX) is EGA's 40% Inola partner and holds an investment-grade balance sheet that satisfies the Office of Strategic Capital's creditworthy sponsor requirement for government debt programs — the critical enabling condition analyzed in the Demand Architecture section.^15 Kevin Kramer, a former Alcoa and ATI executive serving as a Brimstone senior advisor, creates a warm channel to Century's corporate development team.^16 Century's $500M OCED grant (actively maintained by the current administration) and its §45X tax credit monetization ($75M received for FY2024) demonstrate both government credibility and internal tax capacity that could absorb Brimstone's §45X credits after the transferability deadline.^17 Century's balance sheet constrains the check size — at approximately $3B market capitalization, a $100M+ equity commitment is a material capital allocation — but the more natural role is as the creditworthy sponsor guarantor for an OSC loan, a support function that leverages Century's government relationship capital without requiring majority equity.^18 Amazon consent probability: high.

### Financial Sponsors and Government Capital Complete the Capital Stack

**Breakthrough Energy Catalyst and TPG Rise Climate** are the most natural financial co-investors for a Series B structure. Breakthrough Energy Ventures is Brimstone's Series A lead with a confirmed board seat (Cooper Rinzler), and Breakthrough Energy Catalyst — the deployment fund within the BEV platform — could anchor a Series B tranche without triggering Amazon's change-of-control consent provisions, because a follow-on investment by an existing investor at the same governance level does not constitute a change-of-control event under standard ACPF architecture.^19 TPG Rise Climate maintains a relationship with CFO Tim Rens through his prior role at Monolith Materials, where TPG Rise Climate was an investor.^20 A BEV Catalyst anchor of $50–75M with TPG Rise Climate co-investing $25–50M would provide $75–125M of the Series B target without introducing a new strategic investor to the governance structure. Probability of participation if a Series B launches: high.

**The U.S. Department of Defense / Office of Strategic Capital** is not a buyer but a capital partner whose terms are more favorable than any private lender. The OSC offers loans at U.S. Treasury rates with up to 50-year tenor and up to 80% loan-to-value coverage.^21 The MP Materials precedent — $400M DOD Industrial Base Fund equity plus $150M OSC loan — establishes both the structural template and the check-size range for critical minerals producers.^22 At Plant 1 scale, an IBF equity commitment of $80–200M combined with OSC debt of $150–250M would fill 60–100% of the financing gap at capital costs 300–500 basis points below private credit markets. The binding constraint: OSC requires a "repayment guarantee from a creditworthy sponsor" for pre-revenue borrowers.^23 Brimstone cannot access this pathway alone. The sequencing — strategic equity or sponsor guarantee first, then OSC application — is deterministic and consistent with the Demand Architecture analysis.

**Kajima Corporation** is already on the cap table as of October 2025 and could expand its role from venture investor to EPC-linked equity partner.^24 Kajima is Japan's largest general contractor with approximately $15B in annual revenue and an A+/R&I credit rating — a profile that independently satisfies the OSC creditworthy sponsor requirement.^25 The company maintains extensive process plant EPC capabilities and a confirmed trilateral alliance with Bechtel.^26 If Kajima provides an EPC performance guarantee for the Oklahoma plant, it collapses the financing complexity: the creditworthy sponsor problem is solved, the OSC pathway opens, and the need for a cement major co-investor shifts from necessary to optional. Kajima's strategic return extends beyond Oklahoma — Japan's Green Transformation policy mandates cement decarbonization, and a Kajima-built reference plant creates a deployment channel into Asia Pacific markets. The EPC connection is inferred from Kajima's corporate strategy and investment timing, as analyzed in the Demand Architecture section; the specific terms remain private. Amazon consent probability for an expanded Kajima role: high — an existing investor deepening its commitment triggers no change-of-control event.

### TRANSACTION PATHWAYS

Three plausible transaction structures emerge from the buyer profiles, each with a distinct trigger, timeline, and gate condition.

**Pathway A — Strategic Series B ($100–200M, Target Q3 2026).** This is the highest-probability near-term transaction. Structure: equity round led by a cement major through the passive preferred structure (CRH Ventures, or Amrize post-Sublime resolution) or by financial co-investors (BEV Catalyst + TPG Rise Climate), with strategic co-investors providing binding offtake commitments and co-development rights for future plants. Indicative pre-money: $450–550M, reflecting a strategic premium to the $230–285M fundamental range in the Valuation Architecture section.

*Trigger event:* CERAWeek 2026 (March 23–27, Houston), where CEO Finke has a confirmed speaking slot — the formal investor engagement launch.^27 *Indicative timeline:* investor engagement Q2 2026; term sheet execution late Q2 2026; first close (financial tranche) Q3 2026; second close (strategic tranche) Q4 2026. *Key gate:* Amazon consent is not triggered by a minority equity round that preserves founder control. Amazon's most likely posture is neutral to supportive — Amazon may condition its cooperation on conversion of the existing non-binding reservation to a binding take-or-pay contract, which would actually strengthen Brimstone's project finance bankability. *Strategic clock:* the Sublime competitive dynamic — where SCG's potential acquisition of Sublime's IP would strand the cement majors' green technology investments, as identified in the Tension Point Analysis — suggests CRH or Amrize must commit by mid-2026 or risk losing the replacement-asset window.^28

**Pathway B — Full Acquisition ($250–400M, Post-Plant 1 Operational 2028–2030).** Structure: outright acquisition by a cement major or critical minerals strategic after Plant 1 achieves operational proof, with the acquirer absorbing the capital stack and committing to multi-plant deployment.

*Trigger event:* Plant 1 achieving 30-day continuous production at nameplate rates, confirming both cement quality and alumina product classification. *Indicative timeline:* FID 2027; commissioning 2028–2029; acquisition process 2029–2030. *Key gate:* Amazon consent is triggered and is the critical structural constraint. The analysis in the Tension Point Analysis estimates that Amazon would block or condition consent for a cement major acquirer competing in Amazon's construction supply chain, while consenting to an aluminum-sector or financial acquirer. The $250–400M range commands a premium to pre-revenue technology value in the Valuation Architecture section, reflecting operational de-risking and confirmed product specifications that do not exist today. The most elegant resolution of the Amazon dynamic — Amazon converting its reservation to a binding take-or-pay as a consent condition — would paradoxically increase the acquisition value by improving revenue certainty.

**Pathway C — Government-Linked Co-Investment ($230–450M Total Capital, 2026–2027).** Structure: DOD Industrial Base Fund equity ($80–200M) plus OSC loan ($150–250M) plus creditworthy sponsor guarantee from Century Aluminum, Kajima Corporation, or a cement major. This pathway provides the lowest blended cost of capital and the least dilution to existing shareholders.

*Trigger event:* second OSC NOFA publication (expected H2 2026) combined with a committed sponsor guarantee.^29 *Indicative timeline:* sponsor guarantee negotiated Q2–Q3 2026; OSC pre-application Q4 2026; DOD equity committee commitment Q1 2027; full close Q2 2027. *Key gate:* the creditworthy sponsor must move first — without a Century, Kajima, or cement major guarantee on the term sheet, OSC will not process the application. Amazon consent is not directly triggered by a government co-investment, but the sponsor's identity may indirectly affect Amazon's posture. Pathways A and C are complementary, not alternative: an initial $75–125M strategic equity round that includes a creditworthy sponsor would satisfy the OSC precondition, enabling government capital to fill the remaining gap. This private-then-public sequencing is consistent with every DOD critical minerals investment precedent, including the MP Materials structure analyzed in the Demand Architecture section.
