## II-B. Capital Structure, Governance & Transaction Readiness

Brimstone's capital structure contains a $400M financing gap with no remaining government debt pathway, a two-person founder board that concentrates transaction authority in a manner atypical for a company with $80M+ in institutional capital, and a management team assembled in Q4 2025 specifically to close an industrial-scale raise. The cancellation of the $189M DOE OCED grant in May 2025 — followed by the legislative repeal of the DOE Loan Programs Office's IRA authority on July 4, 2025 — eliminated both the primary and alternative government financing routes for Plant 1 within a sixty-day window.^1 The entire ~$400M Oklahoma plant must now be financed from private capital. The company's response — hiring a CFO who has raised nearly $20B across his career, adding a VP Engineering with billion-dollar plant deployment experience, and onboarding a Japanese construction conglomerate as a strategic investor — constitutes the clearest pre-transaction signal observable from public data. The financing menu has narrowed to four options: a large Series B equity round, a strategic co-investment with an industrial partner, project finance debt with a creditworthy sponsor guarantee, or outright acquisition. The management team is configured to execute any of these structures. This section presents the evidence.

---

### The $80M Capital Stack, Its Governance Gaps, and a $400M Hole

Brimstone has raised "more than $80 million" from 23 investors.^2 The confirmed capital stack includes Breakthrough Energy Ventures (lead), DCVC (co-lead at both Seed and Series A), Amazon Climate Pledge Fund, Fifth Wall, S2G Ventures, Collaborative Fund, Acceler8, GVP Climate, Impact Science Ventures, Osage University Partners, SystemIQ, and Kajima Ventures.^3 The last confirmed priced round is the $55M Series A, closed March 11, 2022 — now more than four years old.^4 Database-tracked equity totals only $60.1M across PitchBook and Tracxn, leaving a ~$20M gap partially explained by Kajima Ventures' appearance in the October 2025 investor list despite its absence from all prior public disclosures, every funding database, and the January 2025 investor roster.^5 The most parsimonious explanation is a quiet bridge or extension round in mid-2025 that was never announced as a standalone event — Kajima's investment surfaced only within the Amazon commercial reservation press release, a deliberate disclosure choice consistent with active fundraising under NDA.

Estimated founder ownership is 28–40% on an economic basis, assuming a $150–200M pre-money valuation at Series A with subsequent dilution from bridge rounds totaling $25–35M.^6 Founder voting control almost certainly exceeds economic ownership through dual-class or super-voting provisions — standard in Activate Fellowship-originated companies of this vintage where institutional investors accepted minority governance in exchange for early access to deep-tech IP. BEV holds a confirmed board seat through Cooper Rinzler, per organizational data.^7 DCVC, as co-lead at both Seed and Series A, holds an expected board seat, though no public confirmation exists.

The board itself is the most consequential governance fact in the profile. Brimstone's board has only two confirmed members: co-founders Cody Finke and Hugo Leandri.^8 For a 2019-vintage company that has raised $80M+ from institutional investors including two of the most governance-active climate-tech funds in the market (BEV and DCVC), a two-person founder board is either a data error — reflecting incomplete public disclosure rather than actual board composition — or evidence of unusually concentrated founder control provisions in the corporate charter. The M&A implications are specific and binding: if the board is genuinely two-person, no transaction can proceed without both founders' affirmative consent regardless of investor preferences or strategic logic. There is no independent director to facilitate a sale process, no audit committee to evaluate fairness, and no board-level representative of the institutional investor base to negotiate on behalf of minority holders. An unsolicited approach is structurally impossible; even a fully negotiated process requires founder alignment as a precondition.^9 Any advisor considering engagement must resolve this governance question — directly with management — before committing process resources. As discussed in the Risk Architecture section of this report, founder governance concentration is classified as a primary information risk precisely because no public record can confirm or deny the actual charter provisions.

Amazon's position on the cap table introduces a distinct layer of transaction complexity. Amazon Climate Pledge Fund invested at Series A and holds standard CVC protective provisions with estimated 80%+ probability — including change-of-control consent rights, a voting cap (likely 14.9%), and board nomination or observer rights.^10 Amazon simultaneously functions as Brimstone's equity investor, commercial customer (reservation agreement, August 2025), product testing partner (expanded durability testing through 2025–2026 via Twining Consulting), and founding member of the Sustainable Concrete Buyers Alliance (SCoBA).^11 This quad-role generates overlapping consent rights, informational asymmetries, and counterparty dependencies that shape the entire buyer universe — a dynamic analyzed in full in the Tension Point Analysis section of this report. For present purposes, the key capital structure implication is that Amazon's consent is a binary gate on any change-of-control transaction, and the specific provisions governing that consent are private.

**The financing gap.** The March 2024 DOE OCED selection awarded Brimstone $189M in non-dilutive grant funding for the Oklahoma plant, with $8.7M disbursed in the Phase 1 tranche finalized January 13, 2025.^12 The grant was cancelled on May 30, 2025, as part of a systematic rescission of Biden-era clean energy awards totaling $20B+.^13 An administrative appeal is active but offers limited practical prospect: no commercial OCED grantee has successfully reversed a cancellation through this mechanism.^14 On July 4, 2025, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act repealed the Inflation Reduction Act's loan authority for DOE Loan Programs Office programs, closing what had been the most viable alternative government financing route — a Title XVII loan guarantee covering up to 80% of plant costs (~$320M).^15 The DOE's successor entity, the Office of Energy Dominance Financing, has pivoted toward nuclear, AI data center infrastructure, and traditional energy — not industrial decarbonization.^16

The math is unambiguous. Plant 1 carries estimated capital costs of $378–400M.^17 Against that requirement, Brimstone has $80M+ in cumulative equity raised (much of it consumed by six years of R&D, pilot operations, and overhead), $8.7M in DOE disbursements already received, and no disclosed debt facility. The financing gap is the entire plant. Four pathways remain:

First, a large Series B equity round in the $100–200M range, led by either an existing investor (BEV Catalyst) or a new strategic entrant, structured with co-development and offtake rights. Second, a strategic co-investment from a cement major (CRH, Amrize) or aluminum-sector participant (EGA, Century Aluminum, Mubadala), combining equity with a binding commercial agreement that provides project finance bankability. Third, project finance debt with a creditworthy sponsor guarantee — the DOD Office of Strategic Capital offers Treasury-rate loans at up to 80% LTV and 50-year tenor, but requires a repayment guarantee from a creditworthy entity that Brimstone alone cannot provide.^18 Fourth, outright acquisition by a strategic buyer who provides the balance sheet directly. The transaction pathway analysis in a later section of this report evaluates each structure's feasibility, timing, and Amazon consent dynamics.

One structural element merits attention. Brimstone operates through a bifurcated entity architecture: Brimstone Energy, Inc. (the Delaware parent holding all IP, employees, and investor equity) and Brimstone Commercial, LLC (the designated DOE grant recipient and likely project-level vehicle for Plant 1).^19 This structure is transaction-ready by design. It permits a strategic partner to invest at the project level — taking an equity stake in Brimstone Commercial, LLC with specific Plant 1 economics — without diluting the parent cap table or triggering parent-level protective provisions. It also isolates project finance debt from the parent's IP assets, a standard requirement for non-recourse industrial project lending. The existence of this architecture before any announced financing round is itself a signal that management is preparing the company for institutional-grade capital formation.

### The CFO Hire Is the Transaction — Everything Else Is Confirmation

Tim Rens joined Brimstone as Chief Financial Officer in October 2025.^20 His appointment is the single strongest transaction indicator in the public record — not because hiring a CFO is unusual, but because of who this CFO is, what he has done, and what he left behind.

Rens's career trajectory maps precisely onto Brimstone's financing challenge. He served as CFO at CVR Energy (NYSE: CVI), a publicly traded petroleum refiner — experience in commodity industrial operations, capital markets disclosure, and institutional investor management.^21 He served as CFO at Philadelphia Energy Solutions, the largest oil refinery on the U.S. East Coast — experience in complex project-financed industrial assets with environmental liability exposure.^22 Most critically, he served as CFO at Monolith Materials, a private industrial startup developing first-of-kind methane pyrolysis technology in Hallam, Nebraska — where he raised approximately $750M in total capital, including a $300M private placement with J.P. Morgan and Goldman Sachs as placement agents.^23 Across his career, Rens has raised nearly $20B in capital across the traditional energy and energy transition sectors.^24

The Monolith parallel is structurally exact and merits close examination. Monolith received a $900M+ conditional commitment from the DOE Loan Programs Office — subsequently cancelled. The Wall Street Journal reported in September 2024 that Monolith was "running out of cash."^25 Rens departed during that cash crisis. He then joined Brimstone — a company that had just lost its own $189M DOE grant and faced the same structural challenge: financing a first-of-kind industrial plant without government debt support. The inference is direct: Brimstone hired a CFO who has navigated this exact scenario before, who knows which private capital sources replace government loans, and who carries the banking relationships required to execute.

Those banking relationships define the likely Series B syndicate. Rens's confirmed placement agent relationships at Monolith — J.P. Morgan and Goldman Sachs — represent the two most active sustainable finance desks on Wall Street.^26 His PE network from the Monolith investor base includes TPG Rise Climate, Warburg Pincus, and Decarbonization Partners (BlackRock/Temasek JV) — three of the largest climate-focused private equity platforms globally, with combined AUM exceeding $15B in climate and energy transition strategies.^27 A reader who calls JPM's sustainable finance desk or Goldman's clean energy group and references Rens by name will find a known quantity. The question — examined further in the supporting indicators section of the Tension Point Analysis — is whether JPM and Goldman view Rens as "the CFO who led a $300M placement" or "the CFO who departed during a cash crisis." The answer determines whether the Monolith banking relationships transfer warmly to the Brimstone mandate.

The surrounding hires reinforce the pattern. John Uhrie joined as VP Engineering in December 2025, bringing a career background deploying billion-dollar processing plants for complex metal sulfides — the metallurgical discipline most analogous to Brimstone's hydrometallurgical co-production process.^28 His hiring signals that Oklahoma plant engineering is moving from conceptual design to execution planning. Kevin Kramer, appointed Senior Advisor, is a former executive at Alcoa and ATI (Allegheny Technologies) — providing a warm channel into the aluminum industry's corporate development infrastructure and a direct relationship network relevant to the EGA/Century alumina demand thesis examined in the Tension Point Analysis.^29 Hugo Leandri's title change from Chief Technology Officer to Chief Strategy Officer signals organizational maturation from R&D-led to commercialization-led, consistent with a company transitioning from technology development to capital formation and market entry.^30

The external signals confirm what the hires imply. CEO Cody Finke holds a confirmed speaker slot at CERAWeek 2026 (March 23–27, Houston) — the premier venue for institutional energy infrastructure investor engagement, where audience composition skews heavily toward sovereign wealth funds, infrastructure PE, and industrial strategics.^31 In September 2025, the company stated it "has investors in place to help it continue building" — language inconsistent with a company whose only disclosed capital is a four-year-old Series A.^32 Crunchbase's predictive model assigns a 76% probability that Brimstone will raise a funding round within six months of its late-2025 assessment date — implying a close by mid-2026.^33 No SEC Form D has been filed for a new equity round as of March 2026.^34

The composite pattern — a world-class industrial CFO with confirmed JPM and Goldman relationships, a VP Engineering hired to build a plant, a Senior Advisor with aluminum-sector access, a co-founder title change from CTO to CSO, a CERAWeek speaking slot, "investors in place" language, a 76% Crunchbase probability, and a bifurcated entity structure already in place — is a company preparing for a $100–200M+ industrial capital raise. Whether that raise takes the form of a standalone Series B, a strategic co-investment with binding offtake, or the equity anchor within a larger project finance structure depends on variables examined in the transaction pathway analysis elsewhere in this report. The management team is configured to execute any of these structures. The capital structure demands that at least one of them close within the next twelve to eighteen months.
