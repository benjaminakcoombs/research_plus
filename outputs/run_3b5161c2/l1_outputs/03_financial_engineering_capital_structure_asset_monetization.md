### 4C. PLGL Reserve — The Hidden Balance Sheet Risk


Professional liability costs jumped materially — PLGL expense rose by $61 million in 2025, claims were up 186%, and reserves increased to $155 million — and the company is prioritizing debt paydown with materially lower CapEx in 2026 to drive free cash flow.



PLGL expense increased by $61 million in 2025, driven by two main factors: the need to increase reserves for certain historic claims ($18 million, not expected to repeat in 2026) and a sharp rise in claim volume. Claims were up 186% last year after being relatively stable for three to four years, and 2026 assumptions keep claim volume at that elevated level rather than assuming reversion to prior norms.



The insurance market has changed materially, describing a shift from approximately $3 million of self-insurance in 2022 before insurance coverage applies, to $15 million today, and added that for certain sexual assault claims, cost sharing can extend above $25 million.



Acadia expects its net PLGL liability at year-end 2025 to increase significantly from the $78 million on its December 31, 2024 balance sheet to $145 million to $165 million. For 2026, PLGL expense is expected to range between $100 million and $110 million.

## NOTABLE ANOMALIES — UPDATED AND EXPANDED

**ANOMALY 1: THE REAL ESTATE IS ALREADY UNENCUMBERED — A STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGE UNDERSTATED BY EVERY ANALYST**

The most important and overlooked fact: 
The Company's obligations under the Credit Agreement are secured by a pledge of substantially all assets **excluding all real property** and certain other customarily excluded assets.
 This means Acadia's ~$1.5–2.2B of owned hospital real estate can be transferred in a sale-leaseback **without lender consent and without a collateral release process** — dramatically simplifying execution vs. most healthcare sale-leasebacks. Engine Capital's letter was right to call this out, but analysts have understated the execution advantage this exclusion creates. A $1.5B+ sale-leaseback can be launched within 90 days of a board decision without any credit agreement amendment.

**ANOMALY 2: THE PLGL CLAIMS SPIKE (186% IN ONE YEAR) IS MORE ALARMING THAN THE RESERVE INCREASE**


PLGL claims were up 186% last year after being relatively stable for three to four years, and 2026 assumptions keep claim volume at that elevated level rather than assuming reversion to prior norms.
 A 186% spike in claims is not noise — it reflects a structural increase in legal action against behavioral health operators following the NYT investigative series. The reserve of $155M covers KNOWN claims; the incurred-but-not-reported (IBNR) component for newly filed 2025–2026 claims will only become visible in the 2026 and 2027 actuarial reviews. For any PE acquirer, the PLGL tail is the single most important due diligence risk. A buyer's actuary would likely require **$400–600M in total PLGL reserve/escrow** to price this risk conservatively.

**ANOMALY 3: THE JOINT VENTURE HOSPITAL NETWORK CREATES A HIDDEN BLOCKING MECHANISM**


Acadia has 21 joint venture partnerships for 22 hospitals, with 12 hospitals already in operation and 10 additional hospitals expected to open in the coming years.

1. Change-of-control provisions that may require JV partner consent for a take-private
2. Put/call rights that could be triggered at adverse prices
3. Right-of-first-refusal provisions on JV partner buy-outs

Consolidated assets at March 31, 2025 include total assets of variable interest entities of $1,045.6 million, which cannot be used to settle the obligations of other entities.
 These VIE assets (presumably the JV hospital structures) represent over $1B of assets that are ring-fenced from debt repayment — a significant consideration in any restructuring scenario.

**ANOMALY 4: ACADIA HAS ONE SINGLE REPORTABLE SEGMENT — MAKING CTC SEPARATION STRUCTURALLY DIFFICULT AND COSTLY**


The Company has one reportable segment, behavioral healthcare services. The behavioral healthcare services segment provides inpatient and outpatient behavioral healthcare services. The Company derives revenue from 39 states and Puerto Rico and manages business activities on a consolidated basis.
