## SECTION 4: JV CHANGE-OF-CONTROL PROVISIONS
## ═══════════════════════════════════

### JV Network Overview


Joint ventures with health systems are key to Acadia's expansion strategy. As of August 2025, Acadia has 21 JV partnerships for 22 hospitals, with 13 operational and nine more expected to open, including new facilities with ECU Health, SolutionHealth, Orlando Health, Tufts Medicine, Henry Ford Health, Geisinger Health, and Ascension.



Young described JV partners as a major area of focus, including analyzing referral volumes from partner hospitals and working to replicate best-in-class performance across the JV portfolio.


### Change-of-Control Analysis

**Standard JV Change-of-Control Provisions in Healthcare:**

Healthcare JV agreements virtually universally contain:
1. **Consent rights:** Requiring the JV partner's consent before a change of control (sale, merger, acquisition of >50% of voting securities) is permitted. Failure to obtain consent can trigger termination rights.
2. **Right of First Refusal (ROFR) or Right of First Offer (ROFO):** The health system partner may have the right to acquire Acadia's JV interest at a specified price before Acadia can sell to a third party.
3. **Tag-Along Rights:** Some JV partners may have the right to participate in any sale of the enterprise.
4. **Put/Call Rights at Pre-Set Multiples:** Many healthcare JVs include rights for either party to buy out the other at negotiated or formula-based multiples.
5. **Non-compete provisions** post-closing that limit a new owner's ability to compete in the JV's market.

**Which Partners Would Consent?**
- **Most Likely to Consent:** Partners who are financially motivated and see value in a new operator with stronger capital backing. For-profit health systems (e.g., Tenet-affiliated systems) would likely be neutral-to-positive on a PE acquirer if the acquirer demonstrates commitment to the JV program.
- **Most Likely to Resist:** Non-profit, mission-driven health systems (e.g., Ascension, Intermountain) have board governance obligations to ensure JV partners are aligned with their charitable mission. These organizations' boards would require due diligence on any new owner's commitment to behavioral health mission and patient care quality — especially relevant given Acadia's ongoing DOJ investigation.
- **Specific Concerns:** Geisinger, Tufts Medicine, Henry Ford, and Ochsner are all prestigious academic medical center affiliates. Their boards would require extensive review of any change-of-control, and some may have mission-incompatibility objections to certain PE buyers.
- 
As noted by CFO Young: "the JV partner is usually beneficial and helpful in driving greater occupancy sooner. That one will get to a higher level of profitability earlier as a result."
 This implies JV partners are currently a **positive contributor** to Acadia's ramp performance — a sale that disrupts these relationships would materially impair the thesis.

**Structural Acquisition Options to Minimize JV Consent Triggers:**

1. **Stock-for-stock or cash merger of the public entity:** A standard public company merger triggers most change-of-control provisions as a matter of law and contract. All 21 JV partners would be triggered.
2. **Maintaining Acadia as a continuing subsidiary:** If a PE buyer creates a new HoldCo and acquires Acadia but maintains Acadia Healthcare Company, Inc. as the direct counterparty to JV agreements, this may reduce (but not eliminate) triggering effects if the JV agreements are written at the subsidiary level.
3. **Pre-closing JV consent campaign:** A buyer would typically run a parallel "JV consent solicitation" process during the go-shop/signing period, committing to maintain management and governance standards acceptable to health system partners.
4. **Phased acquisition:** Highly unusual for a public company, but a buyer could theoretically acquire a <50% interest and then exercise an option — almost certainly impractical in this context.

**Precedent:** The most analogous situation is KKR's acquisition of Envision Healthcare, which had extensive physician group JV structures. KKR maintained those physician relationships through careful transition management. In behavioral health, Acadia's predecessor disposition of the UK Priory business under Osteen's tenure is the most relevant Acadia-specific precedent — Osteen navigated that divestiture and is therefore the ideal CEO to manage any future disposition or sale process.

**Bottom Line on JVs:** The 21-partner JV network is both Acadia's greatest strategic asset (it provides a non-replicable pipeline of referrals and facilities) and its greatest M&A friction factor. A buyer must build a JV consent strategy into the acquisition plan from Day 1. Non-profit partners with mission-driven governance are the highest-risk consent targets.
