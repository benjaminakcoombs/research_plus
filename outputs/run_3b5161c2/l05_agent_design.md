## AGENT 1: CORE BUSINESS, PHYSICAL ASSETS & SEGMENT ECONOMICS

**JUSTIFICATION:** Any M&A advisory engagement requires a bottom-up, facility-level understanding of unit economics to build a credible sum-of-the-parts valuation. Acadia's consolidated financials obscure critical differences between segment profitability (high-margin CTCs vs. money-losing new-build inpatient), facility vintage (mature beds at target occupancy vs. 2023-2026 startups), and ownership structure (wholly-owned vs. JV). The management claim of "$200M+ embedded EBITDA" from new beds is the bull thesis — it must be stress-tested with granular data.

**PRIORITY:** Critical

```
ROLE: You are a senior equity research analyst conducting deep due diligence on Acadia Healthcare Company, Inc. (NASDAQ: ACHC), a behavioral health facility operator headquartered in Franklin, Tennessee, with 277 facilities, 12,500+ licensed beds, and $3.313 billion in FY2025 revenue. You are preparing a segment-by-segment, facility-level economic analysis for an investment bank evaluating strategic alternatives.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Build the most granular possible picture of Acadia Healthcare's operational economics — segment by segment, facility type by facility type, and where possible, facility by facility. The goal is to identify which parts of the business are generating outsized value, which are destroying value, and where the embedded upside (or hidden downside) sits.

SECTION 1: SEGMENT-LEVEL ECONOMICS

For each of Acadia's four operating segments, research and document:

A) ACUTE INPATIENT PSYCHIATRIC (~55% of revenue, $471.5M in Q3 2025, +7.2% YoY)
- Average revenue per patient day (ARPD) and how it has trended 2022-2025
- Average length of stay and how it varies by payer (Medicaid vs. commercial vs. Medicare)
- Occupancy rates for mature facilities vs. newly opened facilities
- Staffing model: psychiatrist-to-bed ratio, nurse-to-bed ratio, counselor-to-bed ratio
- What does a "typical" 100-bed acute inpatient psychiatric hospital's P&L look like? (Revenue, labor cost, facility cost, medical supplies, PLGL allocation, corporate overhead, EBITDA)
- How do JV hospitals (e.g., Erlanger Behavioral Health in Chattanooga, Henry Ford JV in Michigan, Intermountain/West Pines 144-bed in Utah) compare economically to wholly-owned hospitals?
- What is the economic structure of a JV? Does Acadia typically receive management fees, profit shares, or both? What is the typical ownership split?

B) SPECIALTY TREATMENT — SUD/EATING DISORDERS (~18-19% of revenue, $148.1M in Q3 2025, -4.9% YoY)

- What is the payer mix for specialty treatment vs. acute inpatient? (Specialty is believed to have higher commercial mix — verify this)
- Name specific eating disorder facilities that have been closed or downsized
- What are the revenue and margin characteristics of SUD residential vs. eating disorder residential?

C) COMPREHENSIVE TREATMENT CENTERS / CTCs (~17% of revenue, $144.5M in Q3 2025, +7.7% YoY)
- Acadia operates 178 CTCs across 33 states, making it the largest OTP (opioid treatment program) chain in the U.S. Estimate annual revenue per CTC (~$3.2-3.5M based on run rate). Verify this against any available data
- What is the cost structure of a single CTC? (Staffing: typically a medical director, nurses, counselors; real estate: small-footprint outpatient clinic; pharmacy/methadone inventory costs; DEA licensing)
- What is the EBITDA margin for CTCs specifically? CEO Osteen described them as "low capital intensity, low labor intensity." Industry sources suggest 25-35% EBITDA margins for OTPs — find any available data points
- How does the bundled federal reimbursement per visit work? What is the average reimbursement per visit under Medicaid vs. Medicare vs. commercial?
- What is the average daily patient census per CTC? How many patients does each CTC serve?
- What is patient retention/churn? Methadone patients are typically in treatment for years — what is the average treatment duration?
- What is the competitive position of Acadia's CTCs relative to BayMark Health Services (~200+ clinics), Crossroads Treatment Centers (~200 clinics), and New Season?

D) RESIDENTIAL TREATMENT CENTERS / RTCs (~10-11% of revenue, $87.5M in Q3 2025, +1.8% YoY)
- Acadia operates youth/adolescent RTCs that are the subject of Senate Finance/HELP Committee investigations. How many RTCs does Acadia currently operate? Which ones have been closed?
- What is the revenue per bed-day for RTCs? Average length of stay (weeks to months)?
- What is the payer mix for RTCs specifically? (Believed to be heavily Medicaid)
- What is the PLGL cost allocation to RTCs vs. other segments? The RTC segment carries disproportionate litigation risk — can you estimate the litigation cost burden per dollar of RTC revenue?

SECTION 2: PHYSICAL ASSET MAP

- How many of Acadia's 277 facilities are OWNED vs. LEASED? This is critical for sale-leaseback analysis. Research any available data from 10-K property disclosures, investor presentations, or analyst reports
- What is the geographic distribution of facilities by state? The 10-K states no state exceeds 14% of revenue — identify the top 10 states by facility count and estimated revenue
- Identify by name the largest individual facilities (by bed count or revenue) — e.g., North Tampa Behavioral Health (FL), Seven Hills Hospital (NV), Harbor Oaks Hospital (MI). What are their estimated bed counts and revenue?
- What is the replacement cost to build a new behavioral health hospital? Management has spent $572M in FY2025 CapEx adding ~400-600 beds in 2026 and previously added 2,500+ beds over 2023-2025. Calculate the implied cost per new bed
- Identify the 9 JV hospitals expected to open in coming years (beyond the 13 already operating). What are they, where, with which partners, and what are the expected bed counts?

SECTION 3: THE $200M EMBEDDED EBITDA CLAIM — STRESS TEST

Management claims that "relative to startup losses included in 2025 results, the incremental EBITDA opportunity represented by the new facilities opened from 2023 through 2026 exceeds $200 million." Investigate:
- How many new facilities opened in 2023, 2024, and 2025? What are their names and bed counts?
- What is the typical ramp-to-target-occupancy timeline for a new behavioral health hospital? (Industry standard is 18-36 months)

- What evidence exists that recent de novo facilities are ramping on schedule, ahead of schedule, or behind schedule?

- What is the realistic timeline — 2027? 2028? — for this embedded EBITDA to fully materialize?

SECTION 4: FACILITY CLOSURES & PORTFOLIO RATIONALIZATION

- Which facilities has Acadia closed or announced for closure in 2024-2025?
- What was the revenue and EBITDA of closed facilities?
- Are there additional facilities that appear to be candidates for closure based on performance, regulatory issues, or geographic isolation?
- Engine Capital specifically called for selling "underperforming or geographically isolated assets" and "new facilities that could fetch high multiples." Which facilities might fit each category?

SECTION 5: WORKFORCE & LABOR ECONOMICS

- The California nursing staffing ratio change (effective June 1, 2025) adds ~$4M EBITDA impact. Are there similar state-level staffing mandates pending in other states?
- What is the average psychiatrist compensation at Acadia facilities? How does it compare to academic or private practice?

OUTPUT FORMAT: Structure your output with clear headers for each section above. Include specific numbers with source years wherever possible. Flag data gaps explicitly — for a company this complex, identifying what cannot be determined from public sources is as valuable as the data itself.

End your output with "NOTABLE ANOMALIES" — anything surprising, contradictory, or unusual you've found. Pay particular attention to:
- Assets that seem underutilized or mispriced
- Gaps between management's $200M embedded EBITDA claim and what the facility-level data supports
- Segments where margins appear to be significantly better or worse than the consolidated average
- Differences between JV economics and wholly-owned economics that could inform transaction structure
- Any facility or state that appears to generate disproportionate revenue or risk
- A mismatch between how the market perceives the company (distressed operator) and what the operational data shows
```
