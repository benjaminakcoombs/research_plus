SECTION 1: MEDICAID POLICY ENVIRONMENT — THE DOMINANT VARIABLE

Acadia receives 57.7% of revenue from Medicaid (FY2025), up from ~51% in 2022. This concentration is rising and represents the single largest external variable.

  - IMD exclusion: The Institutions for Mental Disease exclusion historically prevented Medicaid from paying for inpatient psychiatric stays at facilities with >16 beds. What is the current status of IMD exclusion waivers? Did OBBBA change this? How many Acadia facilities are affected?
  - Per capita caps or block grants: Were any structural Medicaid financing changes enacted that would shift risk to states?
  - Estimate: What is the plausible EBITDA impact on Acadia from OBBBA Medicaid provisions in FY2026, FY2027, and FY2028? (Range: minimal to $50-100M+ depending on implementation)

SECTION 2: MOTAA LEGISLATION — THREAT TO CTC BUSINESS MODEL

The Modernizing Opioid Treatment Access Act (MOTAA) would allow board-certified addiction medicine and addiction psychiatry physicians to prescribe methadone that could be picked up at a pharmacy, rather than requiring daily visits to an OTP (opioid treatment program) / CTC.

- What is the current legislative status of MOTAA? Has it advanced beyond the Senate HELP Committee?
- What are the specific provisions? Would it immediately allow pharmacy-dispensed methadone, or would there be a phased implementation?
- Acadia and industry groups (AATOD, "Program, Not A Pill" coalition) are actively lobbying against MOTAA. What are their specific arguments?
- If MOTAA passes, what is the realistic impact on CTC patient volumes? Jefferies analysts argued the impact would be limited due to structural barriers to methadone prescribing. What are those barriers?
  - How many board-certified addiction medicine/psychiatry physicians exist in the U.S.?
  - How many pharmacies would be willing/equipped to dispense methadone?
  - Would patients actually switch from CTCs to pharmacies? (Behavioral economics: CTCs provide counseling, community, structure — not just medication)
- Model the impact: If 10% / 20% / 30% of CTC patients shifted to pharmacy-based methadone over 5 years, what would be the revenue and EBITDA impact on Acadia?
- Are there state-level methadone reforms that could preempt or exceed federal MOTAA? (e.g., California, Vermont, or other states with progressive drug policy)

SECTION 3: MENTAL HEALTH PARITY ENFORCEMENT (MHPAEA) — UPSIDE DRIVER

The Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act requires insurers to cover behavioral health at parity with medical/surgical benefits. Enforcement has historically been weak but is intensifying.

- What recent federal enforcement actions have been taken under MHPAEA?
- The 2024 final MHPAEA rules require insurers to conduct comparative analyses of non-quantitative treatment limitations (NQTLs). What does this mean for behavioral health reimbursement rates?
- If parity enforcement tightens: how much upside exists for Acadia's 24.6% commercial revenue segment? Could commercial behavioral health rates increase 10-30% over 3-5 years?
- Which specific commercial payers (Aetna, BCBS, UHC, Cigna) have been cited for MHPAEA violations? Are these Acadia's commercial contracting partners?
