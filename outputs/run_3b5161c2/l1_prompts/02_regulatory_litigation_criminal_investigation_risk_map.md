ROLE: You are a senior healthcare regulatory and litigation analyst specializing in government enforcement actions against institutional healthcare providers. You have deep expertise in DOJ False Claims Act enforcement, HHS-OIG investigations, Medicare/Medicaid exclusion proceedings, and healthcare securities litigation. You are analyzing Acadia Healthcare Company, Inc. (NASDAQ: ACHC) to produce a comprehensive risk map of all open and contingent legal/regulatory liabilities.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Build a complete, probability-weighted litigation and regulatory risk map for Acadia Healthcare. For each open matter, estimate the range of potential outcomes (financial and operational), the probability of each outcome, and the timeline. The output must be usable by an investment bank advising on transaction structuring — specifically, for sizing indemnity escrows, litigation reserves, and risk-adjusted valuation adjustments.

SECTION 1: FBI/HHS-OIG CRIMINAL INVESTIGATION — DEEP ANALYSIS

The September 2024 disclosure that FBI and HHS-OIG agents conducted searches at Acadia facilities is the defining risk factor. Investigate:

A) SCOPE & NATURE
- What specific facilities were searched? What has been publicly reported about the subjects of the investigation?
- The investigation reportedly concerns current (not just historical) practices related to patient admissions and length of stay. What specific allegations have been reported — involuntary detention of patients who should have been discharged? Admitting patients who don't meet medical necessity criteria? Billing for services not rendered?
- How does this investigation relate to the September 2024 New York Times investigation that found Acadia facilities were "locking patients in" and the March 2024 NYT report on CTC practices (falsified records, dosing non-opioid-addicted patients)?

B) COMPARABLE CASES — OUTCOMES ANALYSIS
- Research the 5-10 most comparable DOJ/FBI criminal healthcare fraud investigations against facility-based behavioral health or psychiatric providers in the past 15 years. For each:
  - What were the specific allegations?
  - What was the investigation timeline (from initial search to resolution)?
  - What was the outcome (criminal conviction, deferred prosecution agreement/DPA, non-prosecution agreement/NPA, civil settlement, no charges)?
  - What financial penalties were imposed?
  - Were Corporate Integrity Agreements (CIAs) required? What did they require?
  - Was there any Medicare/Medicaid exclusion?
  - What happened to the company's operations and stock price?

Specific cases to research as comparables:
- Universal Health Services (UHS) — FBI investigation of NatPak behavioral facilities (2019-2021)
- Psychiatric Solutions Inc. (PSI) — DOJ investigation pre-Acadia merger
- National Mentor Holdings — DOJ investigation of residential treatment
- Kindred Healthcare — various DOJ settlements
- Any behavioral health provider that faced criminal (not just civil) charges

C) PROBABILITY MATRIX
Based on comparable cases, estimate the probability of each possible outcome:
1. No charges / investigation closed — probability and precedent
2. Civil settlement only (False Claims Act) — likely financial range
3. Deferred Prosecution Agreement (DPA) with financial penalties + Corporate Integrity Agreement — likely financial range and operational restrictions
4. Criminal plea (subsidiary or corporate) — likely financial range and exclusion risk
5. Mandatory exclusion from Medicare/Medicaid (partial or total) — probability and precedent

D) TIMELINE ESTIMATE
Based on comparable investigations, what is the typical timeline from FBI search to resolution? The search occurred September 2024 — when is a resolution most likely?

SECTION 2: PLGL (PROFESSIONAL LIABILITY / GENERAL LIABILITY) RESERVE ANALYSIS

Acadia recorded $115 million of medical malpractice expense in 2025 and guided to $100-110 million in 2026. The PLGL reserve was $145-165 million at year-end 2025. Claims increased 186% in 2025. The insurance self-insured retention threshold moved from $3 million to $15 million.

- What does 186% claims growth mean in absolute numbers? How many claims are being filed? What types of claims (patient abuse, wrongful detention, wrongful death, medical malpractice)?
- Research the Southwood Psychiatric Hospital litigation filed June 2025 (five minors alleging sexual abuse "for decades"). What is the potential exposure from this single case?
- The insurance threshold moving from $3M to $15M means Acadia is now self-insured for claims up to $15M. How many claims in their current portfolio fall in the $3-15M range? What is the aggregate exposure in this "donut hole"?
- Compare Acadia's PLGL expense as a percentage of revenue (~3.5% in FY2025) to industry benchmarks. How does it compare to UHS's behavioral division PLGL rates?
- Is the $145-165M reserve adequate? What would an independent actuarial analysis likely conclude given the claims trajectory? What is the range of plausible outcomes ($100M adequate to $300M+ needed)?
