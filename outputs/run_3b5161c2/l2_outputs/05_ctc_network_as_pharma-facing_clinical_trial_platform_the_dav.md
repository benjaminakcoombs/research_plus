### 2.5 Corporate Integrity Agreement Structure

The CIA framework is the mechanism by which Acadia would most likely resolve its outstanding investigations. A CIA is a structured compliance agreement between a healthcare entity and HHS-OIG:


The entity must implement CIA requirements including hiring a compliance officer, retaining an independent organization to do reviews, and restricting employment of ineligible persons. The entity must submit an implementation report and annual reports to OIG on the status of compliance activities. They must also report certain occurrences such as overpayments, reportable events, and ongoing investigations or legal proceedings. The period of a CIA is five years.



CIAs generally require the provider to implement or expand a comprehensive employee training program, a confidential disclosure program, written standards and policies, and designate a compliance officer and committee. CIAs mandate establishing processes for managing and reporting "reportable events" including overpayments, ongoing investigations or legal proceedings, and potential violations of criminal, civil, or administrative laws applicable to federal health care programs.


**Critically for ARN:** A CIA would require Acadia to report any "reportable events" — which would include adverse events in clinical trials and any allegations of research misconduct. Under a CIA, Acadia's research operations would be subject to OIG independent review organization (IRO) auditing — an additional oversight layer that some pharma sponsors would view favorably (external audit), while others would view as a negative (government oversight of their proprietary trial data).

The precedent for operating clinical research under a CIA exists: Pfizer has operated clinical trials under CIAs, and multiple major pharma companies have maintained research programs under ongoing CIA compliance. The CIA itself does not prohibit research. The question is whether Acadia's specific investigation (admissions/billing fraud, not research fraud) makes sponsors comfortable enough to engage.
