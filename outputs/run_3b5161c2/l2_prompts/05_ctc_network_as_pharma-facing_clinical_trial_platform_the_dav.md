CONTEXT:
DaVita Clinical Research (DCR) generates approximately $48.2M annually by leveraging DaVita's 2,689 dialysis centers as a pharma-facing clinical trial enrollment platform. The model: dialysis patients visit centers 3x/week (156 visits/year), creating a captive population with known medical histories and established provider relationships. DCR activates a subset of centers as trial sites, enrolls patients into nephrology-related Phase II-IV studies, and earns site management fees of $20,000-30,000 per enrolled patient. DCR has operated successfully for over 25 years.

Acadia Healthcare's 178 Comprehensive Treatment Centers (CTCs) serve 76,000 patients daily across 33 states. Opioid Use Disorder (OUD) patients visit CTCs 365 days/year for methadone dispensing — the highest-frequency patient touchpoint in all of healthcare (vs. DCR's 156 visits/year). Each visit includes vitals, urine drug screening, and clinical assessment, generating continuous biomarker data. The patient population is roughly 4x larger than DCR's active base.

The CNS/addiction clinical trial space faces severe enrollment bottlenecks. Key pharma companies with active SUD pipelines include Indivior (injectable buprenorphine extensions), Alkermes (ALKS-2680 orexin agonist, broader CNS portfolio), Otsuka (CNS), and emerging evidence suggests GLP-1 agonists (Novo Nordisk, Eli Lilly) may have anti-addiction properties — a potential blockbuster indication requiring massive OUD-population trials. NIDA's Clinical Trials Network already conducts some studies in OTP settings.

No behavioral health equivalent of DaVita Clinical Research exists. No OTP chain has built a centralized pharma-facing research platform.
