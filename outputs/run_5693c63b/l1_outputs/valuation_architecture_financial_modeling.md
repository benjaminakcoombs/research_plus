# NOTABLE ANOMALIES — CONSOLIDATED ALL ROUNDS (DEFINITIVE)

---

### ANOMALY 1 ⚠️⚠️ — GROSS VS. NET MW: THE DOMINANT VALUATION ERROR (CONFIRMED THROUGH THREE ROUNDS)

This remains the **single most important finding** across all analysis. The error affects every analyst who uses Fervo's publicly stated "500 MW" figure:

- **Gross capacity**: 500 MW (Fervo's headline number in all press releases)
- **Net capacity**: ~400 MW (after ~20% blended parasitic load)
- **Error magnitude**: 25% revenue overstatement; 25% EV/MW understatement if using gross MW vs. Ormat's net MW benchmark

**At the $2.5B IPO midpoint:**
- Using 500 MW gross: Implied EV/MW = **$5.0M/MW** → 18% premium to Ormat ($4.22M/MW) — appears almost at par
- Using 400 MW net: Implied EV/MW = **$6.25M/MW** → 48% premium to Ormat ($4.22M/MW) — requires explicit justification


The Fervo Energy geothermal facilities are expected to achieve a significantly higher capacity factor of over 82%,
 which partially offsets the MW premium — but annual MWh production is determined by *net* MW, not gross. No amount of capacity factor discussion corrects a gross-vs-net error.

---

### ANOMALY 2 ⚠️⚠️ — CORSAC COD IS 2030, NOT NEAR-TERM (MATERIAL DCF REVISION)


Fervo Energy is contracted to deliver the core resource, supplying the 115 MW EGS capacity by 2030.


Most analyst estimates treat Corsac as contributing near-term value. A 2030 COD means:
- Cash flows begin 4 years later than assumed
- At a 15% development-stage WACC, 4 additional years of discounting reduces PV by 43%
- Corsac contributes **option value only** (~$0–$100M) to current corporate valuation
- The **Corsac expansion** is the key bull case: 
Owens confirmed the company is talking about "doubling or tripling" the size of the project, if not much more. "Every single one of Fervo's project sites is designed to be a gigawatt-plus resource."


---

### ANOMALY 3 ⚠️ — $107/MWh CORSAC PRICE IS UNCONFIRMED IN ANY REGULATORY FILING

This is the most significant input-quality problem in the analysis. The Corsac NV Energy PPA price embedded in the CTT has never been publicly disclosed: 
Google declined to share financial details about its agreement with Fervo or the cost of the electricity that the geothermal facility is producing.



Under the tariff, Google will pay the difference between the cost of geothermal energy from Fervo's project in Nevada and the lower-cost resource — solar or natural gas, in most cases — that NV Energy would have deployed under regulatory least-cost constraints.


The CTT structure means: **Fervo-to-NV Energy PPA price** + **Google incremental premium** = effective all-in cost. The two components are not separately disclosed. The $107/MWh figure appears to be a derived estimate from analyst models, not a confirmed regulatory filing price. Any model using $107/MWh for Corsac must carry a prominent "unconfirmed" flag.

---

### ANOMALY 4 ⚠️ — PHASE I IS FULLY FUNDED — IPO IS NOT A SURVIVAL EVENT


Phase I is now fully funded. With full funding in place, key permits secured, and construction well underway, Cape Station Phase I remains on track for its 2026 commercial operation date.


This completely changes the IPO narrative. The company is NOT going public because it needs capital to survive or complete Phase I. The IPO is a **liquidity event** for existing shareholders + **growth capital** for Phase II and pipeline. This changes the risk profile for IPO investors: they are not buying a distressed capital raise, they are buying into a company that has already demonstrated the ability to fund and execute. This is a meaningful positive.

### ANOMALY 5 ⚠️ — DRILLING TIME REDUCTION TO 16 DAYS IS NOT IN ANY PUBLISHED MODEL


Fervo announced that it had completed a well in 16 days. "We are in the mid-teens these days, on average, for our well drilling times," SVP Jewett confirmed.


The $4.8M/well benchmark used throughout this analysis was calibrated to earlier cycle times (~30 days). At 16-day average:
- Conservative Phase II well cost: **$3.0–$3.5M/well** (not $4.8M)
- For 36 Phase II wells: **$108–$126M** (vs. $173M prior)
- **Net CapEx savings: ~$47–$65M** on Phase II drilling alone
- At 30% ITC: After-tax net savings: **~$33–$46M**
- Capitalized at 9% WACC: **~$370–$510M in EV impact**

This is the largest unreflected upside in the model. The Phase II CapEx was already revised from prior rounds, but the 16-day confirmation provides higher confidence in the downward revision.

Multiple Fervo press releases describe Cape Station as a "500 MW" project, an "400 MW" project, and a "500 MW (initial phases)" project in the same document:
