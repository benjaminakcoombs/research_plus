## PART II: EQUITY STRUCTURE — FULL RECONSTRUCTION

### II.A — Standard Term Reconstruction (Market-Standard at These Round Sizes)

#### Liquidation Preferences: 1× Non-Participating (Standard)

At the scale of Series D ($244M) and Series E ($462M), and with the investor quality present (B Capital, Devon, CPP, CalSTRS), market data overwhelmingly supports **1× non-participating liquidation preference** as the applicable standard. This means:
- Each preferred series receives its invested amount back before common in a liquidation or sale
- In an IPO, preferred converts to common at the applicable conversion ratio; liquidation preferences become irrelevant
- Participating preferred would be highly unusual and competitively disadvantageous given the round was oversubscribed

#### Anti-Dilution: Broad-Based Weighted Average (Standard)

Full-ratchet provisions are rare and would be atypical at this investor quality level. Broad-based weighted average is the governing standard. The formula effect:

**If IPO prices at $2.5B (below Series E $2.86B post-money):**

New Conversion Price (Series E) = Old CP × (A + B) / (A + C)

Where:
- A = total fully diluted shares before IPO (unknown without cap table)
- B = shares that would have been issuable at Series E price for IPO proceeds raised
- C = actual new IPO shares issued at IPO price

**Net effect**: Series E investors receive 5–10% more common shares at IPO conversion than they would at a flat conversion, diluting all earlier holders (founders, employees, Series A–D) proportionally. Series E is "made whole" relative to the IPO price at the expense of common.

**If IPO prices at $3.5B (above Series E):** No anti-dilution trigger. Clean conversion at original Series E conversion price.

#### QIPO Auto-Conversion Threshold: The Most Critical Unknown

Standard Qualified IPO thresholds at this scale require minimum gross proceeds ($200M+) AND minimum per-share price. The QIPO price threshold for Series E is likely set at or near the Series E implied price per share. If it is:
