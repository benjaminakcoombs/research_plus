### 🔴 ANOMALY 1: PREFERRED SERIES FAR MORE COMPLEX THAN PUBLICLY KNOWN [NEW — CRITICAL]
The TradingView NASDAQ pre-IPO fund listings confirm at least **8 distinct preferred series** (A-1, B, C, C-2, D-1, D-2, D-3 inferred, D-4, E) — far more than the 5–6 assumed in prior analysis. Each series has its own OIP, conversion price, and anti-dilution formula. The multi-tranche D series (D-1 through D-4) reflects a rolling close structure across 2024, likely with different OIPs as the valuation evolved. 
Series D-1 Preferred Stock exists as a NASDAQ-listed pre-IPO fund (FERVPGX).
 
Series C-2 Preferred Stock exists as a NASDAQ-listed pre-IPO fund (FERVPFX).
 This complexity makes the anti-dilution cascade calculation substantially more intricate than previously modeled — each sub-tranche of Series D may trigger independently.

### 🔴 ANOMALY 2: IPO VALUATION COMPRESSION vs. SERIES E POST-MONEY [CONFIRMED CRITICAL]
The IPO targets $2–3B against a confirmed Series E post-money of ~$2.86–2.9B. 
Previous reporting indicates the company aims to seek a $2–$4 billion IPO, and the Series E funding round should bring it closer to the high end of that range.
 However, 
Fervo has held meetings with major investment banks including Bank of America, Goldman Sachs, and HSBC, targeting a $2–4 billion valuation.
 The narrowing of the range to $2–3B (per Axios Pro) represents compression toward the low end. The SCE PPA pricing discovery ($0.08–$0.10/kWh) and the confirmed on-schedule Cape Station construction provide revenue support for the $3B end of the range — the IPO is more likely to price at or above Series E post-money than below, if Cape Station achieves Phase I COD on schedule in October 2026.

### 🔴 ANOMALY 3: $135M DOE LOAN GUARANTEE — CONFIRMED MISIDENTIFICATION [RESOLVED]
The "$135M DOE Loan Guarantee" referenced in the original brief is the Capricorn-led **Series D-4 corporate equity round**, not a federal instrument. The only confirmed DOE instruments are a $25M grant and up to $60M in collective EGS demonstration funding. 
The DOE confirmed its commitment to invest in three innovative power projects including Cape Station, collectively set to benefit from an investment of up to $60M.
 The absence of a Title XVII guarantee simplifies the IPO — no federal change-of-control consent required.

### 🟠 ANOMALY 4: CAPE STATION PHASE I COD — EVOLVING CAPACITY FIGURES [MONITORING REQUIRED]
Three different Phase I MW figures appear in public sources:
- 
The first 70-MW phase of the project is expected to be operational by 2026.
 (June 2024 — SCE PPA announcement)
- 
The initial phase of the project, with a capacity of 90 MW, is set to commence power generation in 2026.
 (Power Technology)
- 
Fervo expects to deliver 100 MW of clean, firm power to the grid by the end of 2026.
 (Cape Station News, most recent)

The evolution from 70 MW → 90 MW → 100 MW for Phase I reflects the same well design improvements that enabled the 400 MW → 500 MW total upsize. This is a positive technical development, but the S-1 must reconcile the figure against the SCE PPA Phase I delivery obligation — if the SCE PPA specifies a 70 MW Phase I COD date, delivering 100 MW may actually put Fervo ahead of contractual obligations.

### 🟠 ANOMALY 5: GOOGLE / MERCURIA DUAL ROLES [CONFIRMED STRUCTURAL]

Google, which backed the Project Red pilot, also joined the Series E as one of Fervo's new investors.
 Google is simultaneously PPA counterparty (115 MW Nevada) and Series E equity investor. Mercuria is simultaneously a Series D and E corporate equity investor and a $120M senior secured lender. Both relationships require Related Party Transaction disclosure under Item 7 and audit committee governance post-IPO.

### 🟠 ANOMALY 6: HELMERICH & PAYNE UNDISCLOSED EQUITY STAKE [NEW FLAG]

Devon Energy and Helmerich & Payne own stakes in Fervo Energy; both businesses are familiar with drilling and resource exploration and can lend expertise as Fervo scales.
 Helmerich & Payne (HP) is a major oilfield drilling contractor — a strategic relationship that makes operational sense. However, Helmerich & Payne's equity stake in Fervo has never been announced in any Fervo press release. This is an undisclosed related-party relationship: H&P provides drilling services to Cape Station (the "strategic partnership" referenced in Cape Station news) while also holding equity. This will need to appear in Item 7 if H&P's equity stake is material, and any drilling services contract with H&P must be disclosed as a related-party transaction.

### 🟡 ANOMALY 7: SEISMICITY RISK — OPERATIONAL AND REPUTATIONAL [MONITORING]

Fervo Energy has reached a major milestone — successfully completing the first well stimulation at the Cape Station project on the Frisco 1-I well.
 The same news section notes Fervo's approach to managing induced seismicity. Hydraulic fracturing of hot dry rock can cause measurable seismic events. The S-1 must disclose seismicity risk as a material operating risk, including BLM monitoring requirements, any traffic light protocols, and insurance coverage. This is a risk factor that public market ESG investors will scrutinize closely.
