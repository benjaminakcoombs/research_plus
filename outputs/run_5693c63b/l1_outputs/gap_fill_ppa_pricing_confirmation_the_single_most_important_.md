## SECTION 10: LEVELIZED COST CONTEXT AND MARKET BENCHMARKING

### Confirmed LCOE Comparators

Binary hydrothermal geothermal LCOE: $90–$110/MWh (NLR 2025 Geothermal Market Report). EGS is declining toward conventional geothermal LCOE within the next decade per NREL ATB.

Cal Advocates used "the range of levelized costs for incremental binary geothermal" as its benchmark against Fervo's SCE price. The CPUC Staff rejected this comparison as inapplicable — not because the Fervo price was within the LCOE range, but because the benchmark was not representative of actual EGS market bids available in the 2023 MTRRFO Phase 3.

**Inference from the protest structure:** If the Fervo price were within or below the $90–$110/MWh binary geothermal LCOE range, Cal Advocates would have had no case to make — a price at or below LCOE is by definition cost-effective. The fact that Cal Advocates *did* mount a formal challenge suggests the Fervo price is **above $110/MWh** (the top of the binary hydrothermal benchmark range) — or at minimum, Cal Advocates believed it was.

**This is the strongest inferential signal in the entire analysis — and it has been systematically underweighted in prior rounds.**

If Cal Advocates' LCOE benchmark was $90–$110/MWh and they protested the Fervo price as above this range, then:
- The Fervo price is likely **≥$105/MWh** (meaningfully above the $110/MWh ceiling to justify a formal renegotiation demand)
- The CPUC rejected the protest not by defending the price as within the LCOE range, but by arguing the LCOE range was inapplicable — a subtle but important distinction
- Staff's "lowest geothermal price offered" finding means other EGS bidders were **above** Fervo's price, likely ≥$115–$130/MWh
