# DELIVERABLE 1: COMPANY SITUATION MAP

## What Fervo Energy IS

Fervo Energy is the world's only commercial-scale Enhanced Geothermal Systems (EGS) developer — a company that has adapted horizontal drilling and hydraulic fracturing techniques from shale oil and gas to create engineered geothermal reservoirs in hot dry rock, generating 24/7 baseload carbon-free electricity. This is not conventional geothermal (which taps naturally occurring hydrothermal systems); Fervo drills 14,000+ foot horizontal wells into granite, fractures the formation, circulates injected water through the created reservoir, and converts the extracted heat to electricity via Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC) surface plants. The company's tagline is accurate: it is doing for geothermal what Mitchell Energy did for shale gas.

**Physical assets and operations as of March 2026:**

- **Cape Station** (Beaver County, Utah): Fervo's flagship project. 500 MW gross (~400 MW net after ~20% blended parasitic load) across two phases. Phase I (100 MW, COD October 2026) has ~27 of 29 planned wells drilled, an electrical substation installed, three power facilities on-site, and ~350 workers actively deployed. Phase II (400 MW, COD target 2028) has ORC equipment contracted (Baker Hughes 300 MW, Turboden 180 MW) but no Phase II drilling publicly announced. BLM has permitted the site for up to 2 GW and 320 total wells. Cape Station is 100% contracted across four offtakers: Southern California Edison (398 MW, 15-year PPAs, CPUC-approved), Shell Energy (31 MW, 15-year PPA), Clean Power Alliance (48 MW), and other California CCAs.

- **Corsac Station** (Nevada): A 115 MW EGS project contracted under NV Energy's Clean Transition Tariff (CTT) for Google data centers. Currently at **permitting stage** — no BLM FONSI/ROD, no confirmed construction start, COD "later this decade" (2030). This is a decade-scale development project, not near-term revenue.

- **Project Red** (Humboldt County, Nevada): A 3.5 MW commercial pilot operating since November 2023, supplying power to Google data centers via NV Energy. Operating ~28 months with zero thermal decline reported through 12 months; no updated production data has been published for months 13-28 — a conspicuous silence.

- **Project Blanford** (Millard County, Utah): A greenfield appraisal site where Fervo drilled its hottest well to date in February 2026 (>555°F at ~11,200 ft). Early-stage but validates the resource base beyond Cape Station.

- **Leasehold pipeline**: 16+ GW of geothermal leases across Utah and Nevada, including 10 GW in Utah alone. DeGolyer & MacNaughton (D&M) independently certified >5 GW of resource at Cape Station alone at Phase I depths.

**Revenue composition**: Fervo is **pre-revenue**. The company has zero meaningful commercial revenue today. Project Red generates trivial revenue from 3.5 MW of output. All material revenue depends on Cape Station Phase I achieving COD in October 2026, at which point ~$65-72M in annualized revenue begins (100 MW net × 82% CF × ~$90-100/MWh). Full build-out revenue (400 MW net Cape Station + 69 MW net Corsac) reaches an estimated $350-430M/year at stabilization — but Corsac is a 2030+ event.

**Headcount**: ~188 employees (confirmed), plus 350 construction workers on-site at Cape Station.

**PPA pricing**: The exact SCE PPA price is sealed under CPUC confidentiality provisions. The best evidence — CPUC docket analysis, Cal Advocates protest record, NV Energy/Corsac $107/MWh comparator, and western utility regulatory documents — supports an all-in bundled price of **~$95-107/MWh** for the SCE contracts, with a best single-point estimate of ~$100/MWh. The prior widely-cited $86/MWh figure is unverifiable and has been removed from the evidence base.

## How It MAKES MONEY and How That's Changing

Today, Fervo makes effectively no money. It is a capital-deployment machine building a first-of-kind commercial asset. The business model at maturity is straightforward: contracted, fixed-price $/MWh electricity sales under 15-year PPAs to investment-grade counterparties, with 82%+ capacity factors and 60-75% EBITDA margins (no fuel cost; primary OpEx is well maintenance and pump energy). This is a high-quality infrastructure revenue model — the challenge is getting from here to there.

**Revenue quality at stabilization (2029+)**:
- **100% contracted**: Every MW at Cape Station has a signed 15-year PPA
- **Investment-grade offtakers**: SCE (A-rated utility), Shell Energy, NV Energy/Google
- **No commodity exposure**: Fixed $/MWh; no gas price, carbon price, or REC price risk
- **Regulatory backstop**: CPUC's 1,000 MW clean firm power mandate legally compels SCE procurement
- **Recurring**: 15-year PPA streams with 82%+ capacity factor = highly predictable

**Estimated stabilized financials (all 500 MW operational, ~2029)**:
- Revenue: ~$260-430M/year (range reflects PPA price uncertainty: $80-107/MWh on ~400 MW net)
- EBITDA: ~$160-300M/year (60-75% margins)
- At 12-15x EV/EBITDA (contracted infrastructure): EV of $1.9-4.5B

**The margin trajectory story is actually a CapEx story**: Fervo's drilling cost has dropped from ~$9.4M per well (first Cape Station well) to under $4M per well in under 20 days — a 4x speed improvement and 50%+ cost reduction. This learning curve is the most important unreflected upside in any financial model and transforms the economics of the 16 GW pipeline.

## What the ENVIRONMENT Is Doing

**Regulatory tailwinds — codified into law**: The OBBBA (enacted July 4, 2025) carved geothermal out of the wind/solar credit termination. §45Y/§48E credits for geothermal survive through BOC 2033 with phase-down to 2036. Cape Station (construction began 2023) is fully grandfathered. This is not a political posture — it is federal statute.

**Demand megatrend**: Data center power demand is growing at ~15%/year through 2030 (IEA). The 24/7 CFE gap — what solar/wind cannot provide and hyperscalers need — is structural. Google added 8 GW of clean energy contracts in 2024 and moved its CFE score only 2 percentage points. Fervo's baseload geothermal directly fills this gap.

**Competitive dynamics**: Fervo holds ~63% of all next-generation geothermal PPAs in U.S. history (615 MW of 984 MW total). Eavor delivered 8 MW in Germany at €250/MWh (vs. Fervo's ~$100/MWh at 500 MW). Sage Geosystems has 150 MW contracted with Meta but is still in feasibility with $97M raised (vs. Fervo's $1.5B+). The SLB-Ormat EGS partnership is the most credible future competitor but is 3-5 years behind. **There is no meaningful competitive threat to Fervo in the U.S. hyperscaler market through 2030.**

**Sector M&A**: Google closed its $4.75B acquisition of Intersect Power (intermittent solar) on March 11, 2026. Constellation acquired Calpine (including The Geysers geothermal complex). ORIX took a 22% Ormat stake at an implied $3.2B/kW. Japanese strategic buyers pay significant premiums for U.S. energy infrastructure. The sector is active and precedents support $3-5B+ valuations for platform energy assets.
