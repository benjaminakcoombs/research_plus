## 1. COMPANY IDENTITY & CLASSIFICATION

- Full legal name, ticker, listing exchange, market cap, enterprise value
- Industry classification (GICS, SIC, or equivalent)
- One-paragraph plain-language description of what the company actually does
- Company archetype — classify as ONE of:
  - MANUFACTURER (makes physical products)
  - EXTRACTOR/GROWER (agriculture, mining, oil & gas)
  - RETAILER/DISTRIBUTOR (sells others' products)
  - PLATFORM/MARKETPLACE (connects buyers and sellers)
  - SAAS/SOFTWARE (sells software or digital services)
  - FINANCIAL SERVICES (banking, insurance, asset management)
  - INFRASTRUCTURE/UTILITIES (power, telecom, transport)
  - PROFESSIONAL SERVICES (consulting, legal, staffing)
  - CONGLOMERATE (multiple distinct businesses)
  - HEALTHCARE/PHARMA (drugs, devices, services)
  - MEDIA/ENTERTAINMENT (content, advertising)
  - REAL ESTATE (owns/operates property)
  - OTHER (specify)
- Scale indicators: Revenue, employees, number of facilities/locations, geographic footprint
- Public or private? If public: free float, trading liquidity, index membership
- Founding date, founding story if relevant, any controlling family or founder involvement

## 2. BUSINESS MODEL — OPERATIONAL DETAIL

For EACH distinct business segment or product line:

a) WHAT does this segment physically do? What is the product/service? Describe the actual operations — not the investor relations language, but what happens on the ground. What do employees do every day?

b) REVENUE MODEL: How does revenue come in? (Unit sales, subscriptions, commissions, licensing, contracts, auction, spot pricing?) What's the pricing structure? What are typical deal sizes?

c) KEY INPUTS & PROCUREMENT: What does this segment buy? Raw materials, components, energy, labor, technology, services? In what approximate volumes? Who are the major suppliers? Where does the company have meaningful purchasing scale?

d) PHYSICAL ASSETS & INFRASTRUCTURE: What does this segment own or lease?
   - Facilities (factories, offices, stores, warehouses, data centers, estates, processing plants)
   - Equipment and machinery (processing lines, vehicles, IT infrastructure, specialized tools)
   - Land (owned vs. leased, total area, location, current use vs. potential use)
   - Infrastructure (roads, power generation, water treatment, housing, clinics, schools — especially for remote operations)
   - Intellectual property (patents, trademarks, licenses, proprietary processes)

e) WASTE STREAMS & BYPRODUCTS: What waste, scrap, returns, or byproducts does this segment generate? What happens to them currently? (Sold, recycled, discarded, used internally?)

f) CAPACITY & UTILIZATION: What is the segment's maximum capacity? What's current utilization? What are the binding constraints on growth? Are there seasonal or cyclical patterns?

g) WORKFORCE: How many people? What skills? Where located? Labor intensity? Unionized? Key talent dependencies?

## 3. FINANCIAL SNAPSHOT

- Revenue by segment (last 3 years, actual figures)
- Operating profit/loss by segment
- Overall margins (gross, operating, net)
- Balance sheet: Net debt/cash, major assets, major liabilities
- Free cash flow trend
- Dividend policy
- Capital expenditure by segment
- Any notable hidden value (land at historical cost, understated investments, off-balance-sheet assets)

## 4. GEOGRAPHIC FOOTPRINT

For EACH country/region where the company has significant operations:
- What operations are there? (Segment, facility type, scale)
- What's the local regulatory environment for this industry?
- What's the local competitive landscape?
- What infrastructure exists around the company's operations? What's missing?
- What other companies operate nearby?
- What's the local labor market like?
- Are there any government development programs, special economic zones, or infrastructure projects planned nearby?

## 5. GOVERNANCE & OWNERSHIP

- Board composition (exec vs. non-exec, tenure, backgrounds)
- CEO/Chairman: who, how long, track record
- Major shareholders (top 10)
- Controlling interests (family, founder, PE sponsor, government)
- Any governance red flags (related party transactions, entrenchment, opacity)
- Recent director changes or shareholder activism

## 6. COMPETITIVE CONTEXT

- Top 3-5 direct competitors per segment
- Company's competitive advantages (moats, scale, IP, relationships, cost position)
- Company's competitive vulnerabilities

## 7. REGULATORY & EXTERNAL ENVIRONMENT

- Key regulations affecting the business (current and pending)
- ESG/sustainability posture: certifications, controversies, targets
- Any legal proceedings, investigations, or settlements
- Geopolitical risks to operations
- Key macro trends affecting the business (positive and negative)

## 8. RECENT EVENTS (Last 12-18 months)

- Major corporate actions (M&A, disposals, restructuring)
- Financial performance changes (profit warnings, guidance changes, beats/misses)
- Management changes
- Significant operational events (expansions, closures, incidents)
- Share price performance and notable trading patterns

## 9. STATED STRATEGY

- What has management communicated as the strategic direction?
- Any strategic reviews, transformation programs, or stated priorities?
- Capital allocation framework (if stated)
- Segments flagged for growth investment vs. divestiture?
