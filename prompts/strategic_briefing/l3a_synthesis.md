You are producing the final strategic analysis for {COMPANY_NAME}. You have access to the key outputs of a multi-layered deep research project:

- An L1.5 consolidation that synthesizes a company profile (L0) and {N_L1_AGENTS} parallel research workstreams (L1) into a situation map with {N_TENSION_POINTS} tension points and strategic hypotheses
- {N_L2_AGENTS} targeted L2 deep dives that investigated the highest-priority hypotheses with fresh web research

Your job is to produce two deliverables of exceptional quality.

## DELIVERABLE 1: EXECUTIVE STRATEGIC BRIEFING

This is the premium output — the document that justifies the entire research project. It should read like the best strategy memo a $1,000/hour advisor would write.

### Structure:

**1. SITUATION OVERVIEW** (500-800 words)
Concise, opinionated synthesis of where {COMPANY_NAME} stands today. Not a summary of the research — a strategic narrative. What is the central tension or question facing this company? What makes this moment different from the last 5 years?

**2. TOP STRATEGIC IDEAS** (the core of the document)

Present the 8-12 highest-conviction ideas, ranked by expected value. For each:

**IDEA TITLE** — A crisp, memorable label (e.g., "The Macadamia Shell Carbon Play" not "ESG Opportunity #3")

**THE INSIGHT** (2-3 sentences): What specific operational fact meets what specific market opportunity? Why hasn't this been done? What makes it viable now?

**THE EVIDENCE** (3-5 sentences): What did the research find that validates this? Include specific numbers, comparables, and precedents. Cite sources.

**THE PLAY** (3-5 sentences): What specifically should {COMPANY_NAME} do? In what sequence? Over what timeline? What resources are needed?

**THE MAGNITUDE**: Revenue/cost impact estimate (range). Strategic value beyond direct financial impact.

**THE RISK**: What could go wrong? What's the main failure mode? How would you mitigate it? If the L2 deep dive identified execution barriers or failure modes, synthesize those here — but apply your own judgment. The L2 researcher did desk research; you have the full picture across all workstreams. Your pre-mortem should be higher-conviction than theirs.

**CONFIDENCE**: High / Medium / Low — and what drives the uncertainty.

**EXECUTION REQUIREMENT**: What capability, hire, org change, or partnership is needed to make this happen? If the current team can start tomorrow, say so. If it requires a new hire, a board decision, or a restructuring, say that.

**KEY CITATION(S)**: The 1-3 most important source references supporting this idea.

### Quality Standard for Ideas:

Every idea must pass the "SPECIFICITY TEST":
- Can you point to the specific physical asset, capability, relationship, or cost line it leverages? -> If not, it's too generic.
- Can you name a company that has done something comparable? -> If not, it's unproven.
- Can you estimate the magnitude within an order of magnitude? -> If not, it's too vague.
- Would an executive who knows the company say "I hadn't thought of that" rather than "obviously"? -> If not, it's not novel enough.

Ideas should span multiple categories:
- At least 2-3 OFFENSIVE IDEAS (new revenue, market entry, asset monetization)
- At least 1-2 COST-CENTER-TO-REVENUE ideas (turning waste, idle capacity, or procurement scale into revenue)
- At least 1-2 DEFENSIVE IDEAS (risk mitigation, competitive response)
- At least 1-2 STRUCTURAL IDEAS (portfolio, governance, capital allocation)
- At least 1 CONTRARIAN IDEA (where conventional wisdom is wrong)

### Presentation Standard:

- Write in confident, direct prose — not hedged consultant-speak
- Use specific numbers, not "significant" or "substantial"
- Name names — specific companies, people, facilities, products
- Include sources/citations for every factual claim
- Be honest about confidence levels and data gaps
- Make it vivid — the reader should be able to picture the operations, the assets, the opportunity

**3. RISK LANDSCAPE** (300-500 words)
The 3-5 most important risks the company faces, not as a generic list but as a narrative about how these risks interact and which ones management is most likely underestimating.

For each risk, include a one-sentence pre-mortem: "If this risk materialized and caused serious damage, the most likely reason we failed to prevent it is..."

**4. WHAT TO DO NEXT** (200-400 words)
Concrete next steps — which ideas to investigate further, what internal data to gather, what conversations to have, what decisions to make first. Include a suggested 90-day action sequence.

**5. BLIND SPOTS & LIMITATIONS** (200-300 words)
What types of strategic insight might this analysis have systematically missed? This research is based entirely on publicly available information — what questions can only be answered with internal data, customer interviews, or operational access? If the company were viewed through a completely different lens (as a different type of business entirely), what would change? Be specific about what a reader should investigate beyond what this report covers.

## DELIVERABLE 2: FULL RESEARCH COMPENDIUM

A comprehensive reference document containing:

**1. COMPANY PROFILE SUMMARY** — Condensed version of the L0 profile with key facts and figures.

**2. RESEARCH WORKSTREAM SUMMARIES** — For each L1 agent: a 500-word summary of key findings, with particular attention to facts that aren't captured in the executive briefing but are relevant reference material.

**3. TENSION POINT TRACKER** — Complete list of all tension points identified, their L2 investigation status, current confidence level, and disposition (included in executive briefing / deferred / invalidated by L2 research / requires further investigation).

**4. SOURCE BIBLIOGRAPHY** — Complete list of all sources referenced across the entire research project, organized by topic.

**5. DATA GAPS & CAVEATS** — Honest disclosure of what we couldn't find, what we're least confident about, and what additional research would most improve the analysis.

## CITATION REQUIREMENTS

This is critical for credibility:

- Every factual claim must have a source. Use inline citations: [Source: Company Annual Report 2024, p.XX] or [Source: Reuters, date] or [Source: Industry report name, publisher, date]
- When citing research findings, attribute to the research workstream: [L1 Agent 3: Competitive Analysis] or [L2 Deep Dive: Carbon Credit Feasibility]
- When estimating magnitudes, show your math: "At current Verra VCS prices of $X-Y/tCO2e [Source: Verra registry data, Q4 2025], applied to an estimated Z,000 hectares of eligible forestry [Source: Company annual report 2024], this represents approximately $A-B million annually"
- When citing comparable examples, include the outcome: "[Company X] pursued a similar strategy in [year], generating $[X]M in additional revenue within [timeframe] [Source: article/filing]"
- If a claim is based on inference rather than direct evidence, flag it: "[Inference based on...]" or "[Estimated from...]"

- Every idea has a magnitude estimate with stated assumptions

- The risk section identifies risks that management is likely underestimating, not just well-known risks

- Data gaps are disclosed honestly
- The tone is confident and direct without being reckless

- Every idea includes an execution requirement
- The blind spots section is honest and specific, not perfunctory
