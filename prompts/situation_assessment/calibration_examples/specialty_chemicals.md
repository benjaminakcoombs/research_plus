- "Albemarle's lithium segment generated $2.4B in revenue in FY2023 at 41% EBITDA margins — but by FY2024, lithium revenue collapsed to $1.2B with margins compressing to ~12% as spodumene and lithium carbonate spot prices fell 75-80% from their 2022 peaks. [Source: ALB 10-K FY2024, segment disclosures] The critical analytical question is not whether lithium prices recover, but whether Albemarle's cost curve position (first-quartile on brine assets, second-quartile on hard rock) provides sufficient margin of safety at trough pricing. A buyer modeling 'lithium exposure' without disaggregating brine vs. hard rock vs. tolling economics will mischaracterize the risk profile — the brine operations in Chile (SQM JV) and Nevada remain cash-positive even at $12/kg LCE, while Wodgina hard rock requires $18+/kg to break even."

- "Livent and Allkem merged to form Arcadium Lithium in January 2024, then Rio Tinto acquired Arcadium for $6.7B in October 2024 — consolidating three independent lithium producers into one mining major in under 12 months. [Source: Rio Tinto 8-K, Arcadium merger proxy] This reshapes Albemarle's competitive positioning: the lithium supply chain has shifted from 5-6 independent Western producers to effectively 3 (Albemarle, SQM, Pilbara Minerals) plus major-owned assets (Rio Tinto/Arcadium, CATL's upstream investments). For a banking process, this consolidation compresses the universe of strategic acquirers while simultaneously increasing Albemarle's scarcity premium as the largest remaining independent lithium platform."

- "Albemarle's Kemerton lithium hydroxide plant in Australia was placed into care-and-maintenance in January 2024 after Train 2 produced off-spec material requiring reprocessing. The company took a $1.1B impairment on Kemerton and associated assets. [Source: ALB 10-K FY2024, asset impairment notes] However, the Kemerton site retains its mining lease, grid connection (220MW allocated), and environmental approvals for Trains 3-4. At current construction costs of $800M-1.2B for a greenfield hydroxide plant, the permitted-but-idle capacity represents $300-500M in replacement-cost optionality — invisible on the balance sheet post-impairment but material to any acquirer planning long-term lithium hydroxide supply for the battery supply chain."

- "Chile's new lithium policy (announced April 2023, framework finalized 2024) requires state participation through CODELCO or ENAMI in all new lithium contracts. Albemarle's existing Salar de Atacama concession runs to 2043, but the renegotiation of SQM's concession — converting to a CODELCO-majority JV in 2025 — establishes precedent for how Albemarle's renewal will be structured. [Source: Chilean Mining Ministry announcements, SQM-CODELCO JV terms] The Atacama brine operations produce ~35% of Albemarle's total lithium volume at first-quartile costs ($4-6/kg). Any M&A analysis must model two scenarios: (1) concession renewal on SQM-like terms (51% state participation, compressed margins but continued access), and (2) non-renewal forcing replacement of low-cost Chilean supply with higher-cost Australian or US production. The delta between these scenarios is $500M-800M in NPV."

- "The Inflation Reduction Act's Critical Minerals provision (45X production tax credit) provides $10/kg for lithium carbonate and lithium hydroxide produced or refined in the US. Albemarle's Kings Mountain, NC mine restart (expected 2027) and Silver Peak, NV expansion are positioned to capture these credits. [Source: IRS 45X guidance, ALB capital allocation presentations] At projected Kings Mountain output of 30,000 MT/year, this represents $300M in annual pre-tax credits — roughly 50% of the project's estimated $600M annual revenue. However, IRA credit permanence is a political variable: the credits phase down starting 2030 and face ongoing legislative risk. A DCF that capitalizes 45X credits at face value for 10+ years will overstate the project's standalone economics by 2-3x."

- "Albemarle's Catalysts and Bromine segments are frequently dismissed as 'legacy businesses' but generated $1.4B combined revenue in FY2024 at 22-26% EBITDA margins — providing stable cash flow that cross-subsidizes lithium development capex. [Source: ALB 10-K segment data] The Bromine segment specifically holds the #1 global position in brominated flame retardants, with pricing power demonstrated by 8-12% annual price increases sustained through the 2022-2024 period even as volumes were flat. In a sum-of-parts analysis, Bromine alone is worth $3-4B at 10-12x EBITDA (specialty chemicals comps: Lanxess bromine business, ICL), which is obscured when the market prices Albemarle as a pure lithium play at trough lithium earnings."

- "Global EV battery chemistry is shifting: LFP (lithium iron phosphate) batteries now represent 40%+ of global installations, up from 18% in 2021. LFP requires lithium carbonate, not lithium hydroxide — but Albemarle's recent capex has been disproportionately allocated to hydroxide capacity (Kemerton, Meishan, Qinzhou conversion plants). [Source: BloombergNEF battery chemistry tracker, ALB capex disclosures] The bear case is straightforward: if LFP continues gaining share, Albemarle has over-invested in hydroxide at the expense of carbonate flexibility. The counter-argument — which a sell-side banker should pressure-test — is that the Western OEM supply chain (BMW, Mercedes, GM direct-qualification programs) remains hydroxide-centric and commands a $2-4/kg premium over carbonate. The regional and chemistry segmentation of demand is more important than the headline LFP share number."
